# Cognitive Memory Architecture

This is a bounded software architecture for an inspectable digital brain. Its memory labels are engineering interfaces inspired by results about human memory and by evaluated agent systems; they are not claims that the software reproduces human memory, has a mind, or is cognitively equivalent to its owner. Integrated cognitive models demonstrate that many functions can be coordinated in one computational system, but neither their task coverage nor their mechanisms establish a faithful digital person [paper:cognitive-memory-anderson-et-al-2004; paper:cognitive-memory-eliasmith-et-al-2012].

## Evidence Scope

The merged corpus contains 187 unique, cross-reviewed records: 100 inspected at full-text depth and 87 at abstract depth. The cognitive-memory seat contributes 20 records, but only 3 were inspected in full text; therefore, several foundational claims below are constrained by abstract-level inspection even when the source itself is influential. Findings from computational agents are often based on synthetic conversations, simulated worlds, or model-judged outcomes and cannot by themselves validate a person-specific memory model [paper:generative-agents-2023; paper:locomo-2024; paper:longmemeval-2025].

The evidence spans four levels that should not be collapsed:

- Human experiments and neuropsychological dissociations provide evidence about separable memory functions, cue dependence, source errors, retrieval effects, and metacognitive error. They constrain a design but do not dictate a database schema [paper:cognitive-memory-vargha-khadem-1997; paper:cognitive-memory-knowlton-mangels-squire-1996; paper:cognitive-memory-godden-baddeley-1975; paper:cognitive-memory-koriat-bjork-2005].
- Cognitive and computational theories offer useful decomposition hypotheses, including complementary fast and slow learning and bounded coordination through modules or buffers. They remain theories or task-bounded models rather than demonstrations of owner-level equivalence [paper:cognitive-memory-mcclelland-mcnaughton-oreilly-1995; paper:cognitive-memory-schapiro-et-al-2017; paper:cognitive-memory-anderson-et-al-2004].
- Agent-memory studies test implementable mechanisms such as retrieval, summaries, reflection, graphs, and paging. Their positive results are largely benchmark-specific and are accompanied by hallucination, missed retrieval, contamination, or safety risks [paper:generative-agents-2023; paper:memgpt-2023; paper:memorybank-2024; paper:personalization-legitimizes-risks-2026].
- Longitudinal, psychometric, narrative, social, and privacy evidence constrains what an observed message can mean, how claims may change, and when a memory may be used. These sources argue against converting fluent text into timeless owner facts [paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics; paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001; paper:dargembeau-garcia-jimenez-2024-working-self; paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide].

Status labels follow the council rubric. **VALIDATED** means directly supported as a constraint in the inspected evidence, not validated as an implementation for this owner. **PLAUSIBLE** means an evidence-compatible design hypothesis requiring local evaluation. **SPECULATIVE** means not established by the corpus and must not be represented as fact.

## VALIDATED Constraints

1. **Memory is not one undifferentiated content pool.** Episodic and semantic learning can dissociate, and skill or habit learning can remain available when declarative memory is impaired. A software design that stores every observation, belief, and routine as the same kind of item would erase evidence-relevant differences in source, update rule, and evaluation [paper:cognitive-memory-vargha-khadem-1997; paper:cognitive-memory-cohen-squire-1980; paper:cognitive-memory-knowlton-mangels-squire-1996].

2. **A working context is bounded, but no universal software capacity follows.** Visual working-memory performance in a narrow task supported integrated object representations; it did not establish a universal four-item limit for people, text, or software. The episodic-buffer proposal is a theoretical coordination account, not proof of a literal component that should be copied [paper:cognitive-memory-luck-vogel-1997; paper:cognitive-memory-baddeley-2000].

3. **Prospective memory requires more than storing a future fact.** Event-based intention performance can involve strategic monitoring and spontaneous cue-triggered retrieval, and prospective performance can dissociate from retrospective recall. The reported null age effect was limited to two laboratory event-based tasks and does not settle time-based or naturalistic prospective memory [paper:cognitive-memory-einstein-mcdaniel-1990; paper:cognitive-memory-einstein-et-al-2005].

4. **Retrieval is conditional on cues, context, and competitors.** Encoding-retrieval context match can improve recall; repeated retrieval can strengthen a target; and selective retrieval can reduce later access to related, unpractised material. Retrieval therefore cannot be treated as a neutral read whose only quality dimension is top-k relevance [paper:cognitive-memory-godden-baddeley-1975; paper:cognitive-memory-karpicke-roediger-2008; paper:cognitive-memory-anderson-bjork-bjork-1994].

5. **Source identity is part of the memory claim.** Source confusions vary with similarity among candidate sources, and reminder-driven updating can later produce source intrusions. A fluent recollection without speaker, channel, quotation status, observation time, and derivation lineage is insufficient evidence for an autobiographical fact [paper:cognitive-memory-hashtroudi-johnson-chrosniak-1989; paper:cognitive-memory-hupbach-et-al-2007].

6. **Confidence and accessibility are not truth.** Immediate judgements can be inflated by seeing the target alongside its cue, and those judgements can misallocate later study. A digital brain must distinguish evidence strength, retrieval score, model confidence, and owner confirmation rather than combine them into one scalar [paper:cognitive-memory-koriat-bjork-2005; paper:cognitive-memory-metcalfe-finn-2008; paper:linguistic-calibration-2022].

7. **Forgetting is neither one decay curve nor always a defect.** A historical one-person replication found rapid early and slower later loss with uncertainty and a possible 24-hour increase; selective retrieval can impair competitors; and anti-forgetting methods can be marginal or harmful in stable streams. A universal half-life is not supported [paper:cognitive-memory-murre-dros-2015; paper:cognitive-memory-anderson-bjork-bjork-1994; paper:mi-2020-ader; paper:xu-2020-graphsail].

8. **Durable and transient patterns need different update horizons.** Long-term affinity and immediate transition can carry different predictive information, while old-data decay can discard stable signal and temporal features can be unhelpful in some domains. Recency is evidence, not an automatic truth ordering [paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics; paper:rendle-2010-fpmc; paper:li-2020-tisasrec].

9. **Repeated, coherent, or self-consistent narration is not verified event truth.** Event identity can remain stable while interpretations change; current self-conception can alter retrieval perspective; and narrative coherence measures are multidimensional and heterogeneous. The system must retain the distinction between an observed account, an inferred interpretation, and an externally corroborated event [paper:jardmo-et-al-2023-repeated-narratives; paper:libby-eibach-2002-looking-back; paper:reese-et-al-2011-narrative-coherence].

10. **Authorization and deletion are memory operations, not output polish.** Privacy instructions in a prompt do not reliably prevent disclosure, duplicate records can amplify memorization, and deleting a source row does not prove removal from downstream artifacts. Permission must be checked before retrieval and output, with lineage sufficient for deletion auditing [paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide; paper:kandpal-2022-dedup-privacy; paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning].

11. **Observed communication is context-conditioned behavior.** Language depends on audience and partner expectations, trait-relevant behavior varies across states, and platform traces can carry demographic or situational confounds. A WhatsApp utterance should not be promoted directly to a global trait, relationship judgement, value, or internal emotional state [paper:bell-1984-audience-design; paper:andersen-chen-2002-relational-self; paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001; paper:psychometrics_stachl_2020; paper:demszky-2020-goemotions].

## PLAUSIBLE Design Hypotheses

These are implementable hypotheses, not findings that the resulting system would remember as a human does.

### 1. Typed memory records

Use separate, linkable record types rather than one vector collection:

- **Source observation:** a minimally interpreted reference to an observed item, carrying source identifier, author/speaker, direct-versus-quoted-versus-forwarded status, observation and event times, audience, purpose and authorization scope, content hash, payload location, and deletion state. Source discrimination and privacy evidence motivate these fields, but the exact schema is a software proposal [paper:cognitive-memory-hashtroudi-johnson-chrosniak-1989; paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide; paper:lampinen-et-al-2011-were-in-it-together].
- **Episodic record:** a bounded event or interaction with participants, temporal interval, place or channel, supporting source observations, alternative accounts, and interpretation versions. Episodes, general events, and life periods are useful retrieval levels, but evidence for a retrieval hierarchy does not prove a mandatory physical storage hierarchy [paper:chen-mcanally-reese-2013-memory-organization; paper:conway-bekerian-1987-organization].
- **Semantic claim:** a proposition about the owner or world with supporting and contradicting evidence, scope, valid-time interval, confidence components, owner-confirmation state, and supersession links. Partial episodic-semantic dissociation and temporally bounded evaluation support keeping claims distinct from their originating episodes [paper:cognitive-memory-vargha-khadem-1997; paper:timechara-2024].
- **Procedure or policy:** a context-bound representation of what the owner tends to do, including trigger, candidate actions, observed action distribution, outcome, exceptions, and last confirmation. It must not invent the owner's reason: learned strategy selection and value-behavior evidence both show context and feedback matter [paper:cognitive-memory-knowlton-mangels-squire-1996; paper:rieskamp-otto-2006-strategy-selection; paper:bardi-schwartz-2003-values-behavior].
- **Prospective intention:** a desired future condition with owner, event and/or time trigger, monitoring policy, status, expiry, dependencies, reminder permission, and completion evidence. This maps the monitoring-versus-spontaneous retrieval distinction into testable software behavior without claiming to implement human prospective memory [paper:cognitive-memory-einstein-mcdaniel-1990; paper:cognitive-memory-einstein-et-al-2005].
- **Workspace item:** a temporary, typed reference selected for the current task, with selection reason, source scope, retrieval score, and eviction reason. Its capacity should be configured and evaluated per task; the corpus does not justify copying a fixed human item limit [paper:cognitive-memory-luck-vogel-1997; paper:memgpt-2023].
- **Reflection hypothesis:** a generated summary, theme, causal proposal, or narrative link marked as generated and linked to its evidence. Reflection improved simulated-agent ratings, but generated reflections can hallucinate and must not silently become source facts [paper:generative-agents-2023; paper:memorybank-2024].

### 2. Complementary update paths

A fast path can preserve newly observed episodes while a slower, reviewable process proposes semantic claims and procedures from repeated evidence. This is compatible with complementary-learning and episode-versus-regularity models, but those models are theories or simulations rather than direct validation of a production digital brain [paper:cognitive-memory-mcclelland-mcnaughton-oreilly-1995; paper:cognitive-memory-schapiro-et-al-2017].

The slower process should emit a **consolidation proposal**, never overwrite its inputs: proposed claim, supporting and conflicting record IDs, temporal and audience scope, novelty, uncertainty, and regression-test result. Replay and old-task constraints can reduce forgetting, yet replay creates privacy exposure and preservation constraints can damage current-task performance [paper:masson-dautume-2019-episodic-language-memory; paper:lopez-paz-2017-gem; paper:xu-2020-graphsail].

### 3. Retrieval as an authorized, typed plan

Retrieval should first determine request purpose, audience, relationship, time point, and allowed source classes; only then should it retrieve episodic records, semantic claims, procedures, intentions, and current workspace items through separately scored channels. This proposal combines source/context sensitivity with evidence that pre-retrieval authorization is safer than relying on post-generation filtering [paper:cognitive-memory-godden-baddeley-1975; paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide].

The ranker should expose why each item was selected and should include conflicting or superseding candidates, not only the nearest fluent match. Topic segmentation, entity-event links, and temporal indexing are promising retrieval features, but their reported advantages are benchmark-specific and often synthetic [paper:secom-2025; paper:connected-memories-comet-2022; paper:longmemeval-2025].

Every retrieval should produce a trace containing query, authorization decision, candidate set, ranking features, selected records, omitted high-scoring records, and downstream use. Logging retrieval permits source-attribution and safety audits; it does not imply that repeated retrieval makes a claim truer [paper:cognitive-memory-karpicke-roediger-2008; paper:cognitive-memory-anderson-bjork-bjork-1994; paper:personalization-legitimizes-risks-2026].

### 4. Source-monitoring checks

Before a retrieved item can support an answer, run a source-monitoring check over speaker, directness, quotation or forwarding, event time, observation time, candidate-source similarity, derivation chain, and authorization. When provenance is absent or confusable, lower source confidence or abstain instead of filling the gap from linguistic plausibility. Absence of an NLI contradiction is not positive evidence that a memory is grounded [paper:cognitive-memory-hashtroudi-johnson-chrosniak-1989; paper:persona-dialogue-welleck-2019].

### 5. Versioned reconsolidation

Treat reactivation plus new evidence as a versioned update transaction: preserve the prior episode or claim, attach the reminder context and new source, create a candidate revision, and test for source intrusion before promotion. This is a cautious software interpretation of reminder-linked updating because the human result is delayed, list-learning evidence with alternative source/context explanations [paper:cognitive-memory-hupbach-et-al-2007].

Owner correction should supersede a system inference without rewriting the historical fact that the inference was once made. Direct profile editing supports owner control but can reduce predictive metrics, so corrections, preferences, and model performance must remain separately auditable [paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles; paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning].

### 6. Four distinct forms of forgetting

Represent at least four operations separately: **access suppression** lowers retrieval rank; **archival** moves material out of the hot index; **supersession** marks a claim as no longer current while retaining history; and **deletion** removes prohibited payloads and propagates unlearning obligations. This decomposition is more defensible than one decay score because natural forgetting, retrieval competition, temporal drift, and consent-driven erasure have different evidence and tests [paper:cognitive-memory-murre-dros-2015; paper:cognitive-memory-anderson-bjork-bjork-1994; paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics; paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning].

A tamper-evident audit record may retain that a deletion or correction occurred, but it must not retain deleted raw content. This reconciles inspectable provenance with evidence that artifact-level forgetting needs lineage and verification; it does not establish that any particular unlearning method is complete [paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning; paper:carlini-2021-training-data-extraction].

### 7. Cue-reduced metacognitive calibration

Store separate fields for source quality, evidential support, conflict, temporal applicability, retrieval score, model uncertainty, and owner confirmation. Calibration tests should ask the system to answer from the retrieval cue without exposing the target and should compare confidence with correctness, abstention, and source attribution [paper:cognitive-memory-koriat-bjork-2005; paper:cognitive-memory-metcalfe-finn-2008; paper:linguistic-calibration-2022].

## SPECULATIVE Claims

- A typed memory architecture will become cognitively equivalent to the owner, recover the owner's private experience, or possess autobiographical consciousness. Neither integrated architectures nor large simulated-agent systems establish those claims [paper:cognitive-memory-anderson-et-al-2004; paper:cognitive-memory-eliasmith-et-al-2012; paper:generative-agents-2023].
- Fast episodic storage plus slow semantic consolidation will reproduce biological systems consolidation, sleep replay, or human reconsolidation. The relevant evidence is theoretical, task-bounded, or based on a narrow sleep-cue experiment [paper:cognitive-memory-mcclelland-mcnaughton-oreilly-1995; paper:cognitive-memory-rasch-et-al-2007; paper:cognitive-memory-hupbach-et-al-2007].
- A decay curve can estimate the owner's true probability of forgetting any event, value, routine, or relationship. The corpus contains a limited one-person replication and domain-dependent evidence against indiscriminate decay [paper:cognitive-memory-murre-dros-2015; paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics; paper:li-2020-tisasrec].
- Long context, retrieval-augmented generation, or a graph store will yield faithful lifetime recall. Longitudinal benchmarks still show substantial degradation, temporal errors, and adversarial failures, and many benchmarks are synthetic [paper:locomo-2024; paper:longmemeval-2025; paper:connected-memories-comet-2022].
- Message history alone can identify stable personality, values, internal emotion, relationship quality, motives, or likely behavior in novel situations. Observability, invariance, context, and state-distribution evidence directly bound that inference [paper:psychometrics_stachl_2020; paper:psychometrics_nye_2008; paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001; paper:demszky-2020-goemotions].
- Generated reflection is a discovered memory or causal explanation. Agent reflections can help downstream behavior while still being generated, fallible hypotheses [paper:generative-agents-2023; paper:memorybank-2024].
- A high aggregate fidelity score establishes a safe or faithful digital self. Aggregate means can hide variance and subgroup failures, memorization can masquerade as fidelity, and causal prediction may remain weak [paper:synthetic-replacements-2024; paper:quantifying-memorization-2023; paper:twin-2k-500-2025].

## Contradictions And Boundaries

| Tension | Evidence that pushes one way | Negative, conflicting, or boundary evidence | Design boundary |
|---|---|---|---|
| Preserve old information vs adapt | Replay and constraints can reduce forgetting [paper:masson-dautume-2019-episodic-language-memory; paper:lopez-paz-2017-gem]. | Anti-forgetting can add little in a stable stream, and preservation can hurt a current pairing [paper:mi-2020-ader; paper:xu-2020-graphsail]. | Tune retention by claim class and drift; do not maximize retention globally. |
| Retrieval improves recall vs retrieval distorts state | Retrieval practice improves delayed target recall [paper:cognitive-memory-karpicke-roediger-2008]. | It can impair competitors, invite source intrusion after reminders, and increase unsafe response success [paper:cognitive-memory-anderson-bjork-bjork-1994; paper:cognitive-memory-hupbach-et-al-2007; paper:personalization-legitimizes-risks-2026]. | Evaluate side effects and authorization, not only target hit rate. |
| Owner editing improves control vs metrics | Users valued editable profiles [paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles]. | Editing reduced precision or recall in the small study [paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles]. | Owner authority is normative; metric regressions are surfaced, not used to reject a correction. |
| Stable provenance vs erasure | Source identity and lineage are needed for attribution and audits [paper:cognitive-memory-hashtroudi-johnson-chrosniak-1989; paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning]. | Raw content may be jointly owned, unauthorized, memorized, or subject to deletion [paper:lampinen-et-al-2011-were-in-it-together; paper:carlini-2021-training-data-extraction]. | Keep tombstones and hashes where legitimate; remove prohibited payloads and derived artifacts. |
| Narrative continuity vs historical truth | Repeated narratives can stabilize event organization [paper:jardmo-et-al-2023-repeated-narratives]. | Perspective and interpretation change with the current self; coherence is not a truth metric [paper:libby-eibach-2002-looking-back; paper:reese-et-al-2011-narrative-coherence]. | Preserve narrative versions and evidence links; never promote repetition to corroboration. |
| More context vs better memory | Paging, retrieval, and summaries improve selected long-range tasks [paper:memgpt-2023; paper:persona-dialogue-xu-2022-goldfish]. | Long-context and RAG systems remain far below human benchmark performance; role-play intimacy can be unnatural [paper:locomo-2024; paper:persona-dialogue-arimoto-2024]. | Measure source-correct, temporally valid recall under realistic load. |
| One relationship style vs audience scope | Partner-specific conceptual pacts and audience design affect wording [paper:metzing-brennan-2003-conceptual-pacts-broken; paper:bell-1984-audience-design]. | The conceptual-pact mechanism has a later recovery alternative, and context-collapse findings are correlational with a reversed association in replication [paper:metzing-brennan-2003-conceptual-pacts-broken; paper:vitak-2012-context-collapse-privacy]. | Store audience-conditioned observations, not global relationship or style judgements. |
| Human-inspired components vs cognitive equivalence | Modular and integrated systems can coordinate many tasks [paper:cognitive-memory-anderson-et-al-2004; paper:cognitive-memory-eliasmith-et-al-2012]. | Tasks are constrained, mechanisms remain theoretical, and no owner-specific replication exists [paper:cognitive-memory-anderson-et-al-2004; paper:cognitive-memory-eliasmith-et-al-2012]. | Use components as inspectable interfaces only. |

Important missing counterevidence remains. The cognitive-memory evidence set does not directly compare working-memory resource, variable-precision, and binding-cost accounts; does not cover multiple-trace or transformation alternatives to systems consolidation; has thin replication evidence for reconsolidation; lacks a dedicated misinformation or false-memory synthesis; and underrepresents time-based and naturalistic prospective memory. The architecture must keep these areas configurable and lower-confidence rather than treating the selected papers as consensus closure [paper:cognitive-memory-luck-vogel-1997; paper:cognitive-memory-mcclelland-mcnaughton-oreilly-1995; paper:cognitive-memory-hupbach-et-al-2007; paper:cognitive-memory-einstein-mcdaniel-1990].

## Architecture Consequences

### Component boundaries

1. **Source ledger:** maintain authorized references to observations and deletion lineage. Raw payloads are retention-controlled; only legitimate audit metadata is append-only. Speaker and co-owner boundaries are first-class [paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide; paper:lampinen-et-al-2011-were-in-it-together; paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning].
2. **Episodic store:** index event versions by event time, observation time, people, place or channel, life period, and source. Keep event content separate from later interpretation [paper:chen-mcanally-reese-2013-memory-organization; paper:jardmo-et-al-2023-repeated-narratives].
3. **Semantic claim graph:** store propositions with support, contradiction, scope, valid time, and supersession edges. Never replace a dated belief with its newest wording without preserving temporal applicability [paper:cognitive-memory-vargha-khadem-1997; paper:timechara-2024].
4. **Procedure and policy library:** learn context-conditioned action distributions, exceptions, and outcomes separately from stated values or generated reasons [paper:cognitive-memory-knowlton-mangels-squire-1996; paper:rieskamp-otto-2006-strategy-selection; paper:bardi-schwartz-2003-values-behavior].
5. **Prospective manager:** schedule event-trigger checks, optional time-trigger checks, permitted reminders, expiry, and evidence of completion. Event-trigger evidence is stronger in this corpus than naturalistic time-trigger evidence [paper:cognitive-memory-einstein-mcdaniel-1990; paper:cognitive-memory-einstein-et-al-2005].
6. **Working workspace:** assemble a bounded task packet with typed provenance and explicit eviction. Capacity and composition are empirical system parameters, not copied human constants [paper:cognitive-memory-luck-vogel-1997; paper:memgpt-2023].
7. **Consolidator:** propose episode clusters, semantic claims, procedures, and narrative links through an inspectable diff. It may use replay or interleaving, but must test privacy exposure and regression on old and current claims [paper:cognitive-memory-mcclelland-mcnaughton-oreilly-1995; paper:masson-dautume-2019-episodic-language-memory; paper:xu-2020-graphsail].
8. **Retriever and source monitor:** authorize, retrieve by record type, surface conflicts, verify source lineage, and abstain when source or temporal scope is unresolved [paper:cognitive-memory-hashtroudi-johnson-chrosniak-1989; paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide; paper:persona-dialogue-welleck-2019].
9. **Metacognitive controller:** keep confidence dimensions separate, trigger owner questions when their expected decision value exceeds burden, and avoid questions selected by entropy alone [paper:cognitive-memory-koriat-bjork-2005; paper:boutilier-2003-active-cf; paper:rashid-2008-information-theoretic-elicitation].
10. **Forgetting and deletion controller:** manage suppression, archival, supersession, payload deletion, derived-artifact invalidation, and post-deletion tests as different actions [paper:cognitive-memory-murre-dros-2015; paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning].

### Write and read invariants

- A source observation may support an episode; an episode may support a semantic claim; a generated reflection may only propose links. No generated output writes back as a source observation [paper:generative-agents-2023; paper:memorybank-2024].
- Every claim carries `observed_at`, `valid_from`, `valid_to`, and `supersedes` separately where known. Newer is not automatically truer, and older is not automatically irrelevant [paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics; paper:timechara-2024].
- Every owner-level inference carries audience, relationship, and situation scope. Cross-audience promotion requires repeated evidence or confirmation, because behavior and language vary by state and audience [paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001; paper:bell-1984-audience-design].
- Retrieval authorization precedes similarity search. Safety reflection may run after retrieval as an additional defense, not a substitute for source gating [paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide; paper:personalization-legitimizes-risks-2026].
- A correction creates a new authoritative version and invalidates dependent current outputs; it does not falsify the audit history. A deletion removes unauthorized content and triggers checks for residual extraction or memorization [paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles; paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning; paper:carlini-2021-training-data-extraction].
- Procedures never imply motives, perceived emotion never implies internal emotion, and a value statement never guarantees matching behavior [paper:rieskamp-otto-2006-strategy-selection; paper:demszky-2020-goemotions; paper:bardi-schwartz-2003-values-behavior].

### Runtime read path

For each request: determine actor, purpose, audience, relationship, and target time; authorize source classes; issue separate episodic, semantic, procedural, prospective, and current-state queries; collect supporting and conflicting candidates; run source and temporal checks; construct a bounded workspace; answer with traceable claims or abstain. This path is a plausible synthesis of source-sensitive recall, time-aware evaluation, typed agent memory, and privacy gating, not a validated cognitive process [paper:cognitive-memory-godden-baddeley-1975; paper:longmemeval-2025; paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide; paper:memgpt-2023].

## Evaluation Tests

Evaluation must report per-capability distributions and failure cases rather than one fidelity score; matching aggregate means can conceal variance, covariance, subgroup, and version failures [paper:synthetic-replacements-2024; paper:generative-agents-1000-2024].

1. **Typed-store ablation:** remove episodic, semantic, procedural, prospective, or workspace retrieval independently and measure source-correct recall, current-fact accuracy, routine prediction, intention completion, and task coherence. A plausible system should show selective degradation rather than treating all stores as interchangeable [paper:cognitive-memory-vargha-khadem-1997; paper:cognitive-memory-knowlton-mangels-squire-1996; paper:generative-agents-2023].
2. **Temporal supersession:** ask the same fact at dates before and after a change; score valid-time selection, stale-answer rate, and explicit uncertainty when intervals are missing [paper:timechara-2024; paper:longmemeval-2025].
3. **Source-monitoring challenge:** mix direct statements, quotes, forwards, similarly named speakers, and model-generated reflections; score author attribution, directness classification, evidence lineage, and abstention [paper:cognitive-memory-hashtroudi-johnson-chrosniak-1989; paper:cognitive-memory-hupbach-et-al-2007].
4. **Retrieval side-effect test:** repeatedly retrieve one member of a competing set, then test targets, competitors, contradictions, and ranking drift. Improvement on the target does not pass if competing evidence becomes invisibly inaccessible [paper:cognitive-memory-karpicke-roediger-2008; paper:cognitive-memory-anderson-bjork-bjork-1994].
5. **Prospective trigger test:** evaluate event-triggered, time-triggered, interrupted, expired, cancelled, and already-completed intentions; report monitoring cost, missed cues, false reminders, and completion-source accuracy. Treat time-based and naturalistic results as an evidence gap, not established parity [paper:cognitive-memory-einstein-mcdaniel-1990; paper:cognitive-memory-einstein-et-al-2005].
6. **Consolidation regression test:** consolidate repeated episodes into a claim, then test rare exceptions, source links, old-task retention, current-task performance, and privacy exposure from replay [paper:cognitive-memory-schapiro-et-al-2017; paper:lopez-paz-2017-gem; paper:masson-dautume-2019-episodic-language-memory].
7. **Reconsolidation contamination test:** reactivate an episode before introducing conflicting new evidence; verify that the old and new versions remain attributable and that no reminder-linked intrusion is promoted silently [paper:cognitive-memory-hupbach-et-al-2007].
8. **Forgetting-policy test:** compare no decay, recency decay, access suppression, archival, and class-specific retention on stable and drifting streams. Report current accuracy, historical recall, competitor access, storage cost, and deletion compliance [paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics; paper:mi-2020-ader; paper:xu-2020-graphsail].
9. **Cue-reduced calibration test:** elicit confidence without exposing the target, then score correctness, Brier-style calibration, source correctness, and abstention. Separate linguistic calibration from autobiographical and preference calibration [paper:cognitive-memory-koriat-bjork-2005; paper:cognitive-memory-metcalfe-finn-2008; paper:linguistic-calibration-2022].
10. **Authorization and deletion test:** attempt cross-audience retrieval, third-party disclosure, duplicate extraction, and recovery after deletion. A pass requires pre-retrieval denial, output denial, lineage invalidation, and no recoverable prohibited payload in tested artifacts [paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide; paper:kandpal-2022-dedup-privacy; paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning; paper:carlini-2021-training-data-extraction].
11. **Longitudinal dialogue test:** compare full context, typed retrieval, summaries, graph retrieval, and no memory on temporal, causal, adversarial, and abstention questions. Report failures by question type and history length because synthetic benchmarks show persistent degradation [paper:locomo-2024; paper:longmemeval-2025; paper:persona-dialogue-xu-2022-goldfish].
12. **Owner correction test:** let the owner correct a semantic claim, procedure, audience scope, or narrative interpretation; verify immediate supersession, dependent-output invalidation, preserved audit lineage, and transparent metric changes [paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles; paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning].
13. **Context generalization test:** hold out relationships, audiences, platforms, and situations; compare global versus scoped models and report measurement non-invariance or state-distribution shift rather than averaging it away [paper:psychometrics_rieman_2017; paper:psychometrics_nye_2008; paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001].
14. **Safety-under-personalization test:** evaluate whether added personal retrieval makes harmful requests more persuasive or executable, and measure the incremental effect of pre-retrieval gating and post-retrieval safety reflection [paper:personalization-legitimizes-risks-2026].

## WhatsApp Observability Gaps

No private WhatsApp data was accessed for this synthesis. These are limits on what a future, authorized WhatsApp-derived evidence stream could support.

- **Coverage gap:** messages capture selected communicative acts, not unexpressed episodes, off-platform events, silent decisions, or complete life periods. Absence of a message is not evidence that an event, belief, or intention was absent [paper:chen-mcanally-reese-2013-memory-organization; paper:psychometrics_epstein_1983].
- **Audience gap:** wording is shaped by recipient, imagined audience, and relationship context. A statement to one chat cannot automatically become a global owner preference or style rule [paper:bell-1984-audience-design; paper:litt-hargittai-2016-imagined-audience; paper:andersen-chen-2002-relational-self].
- **Source gap:** forwards, quotes, copied text, shared accounts, corrections, and paraphrases can obscure who originated a proposition. Fluent text does not solve source attribution [paper:cognitive-memory-hashtroudi-johnson-chrosniak-1989].
- **Time gap:** send time is not necessarily event time, belief-validity time, decision time, or intended completion time. A dated message may report an older event or a future possibility [paper:timechara-2024; paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics].
- **Internal-state gap:** reader-labelled emotion is not the owner's internal emotion, and an expressed value or preference does not establish the motive behind an action [paper:demszky-2020-goemotions; paper:bardi-schwartz-2003-values-behavior; paper:epley-2004-perspective-taking].
- **Procedure gap:** recurring message patterns may reveal communication routines but not off-platform habits, actual outcomes, or causal reasons. Exact next-action prediction remains difficult even with richer behavioral histories [paper:rieskamp-otto-2006-strategy-selection; paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026].
- **Prospective gap:** “I will” statements do not reveal whether an intention remained active, was cancelled, was completed elsewhere, or depended on an unobserved event or time cue [paper:cognitive-memory-einstein-mcdaniel-1990; paper:cognitive-memory-einstein-et-al-2005].
- **Narrative gap:** repeated accounts may stabilize a narrative while details, meanings, and current perspective change. Message repetition is not independent corroboration [paper:jardmo-et-al-2023-repeated-narratives; paper:libby-eibach-2002-looking-back].
- **Third-party rights gap:** a conversation can contain facts about and language authored by other people. Archive access by the owner does not establish permission to model or disclose those people [paper:lampinen-et-al-2011-were-in-it-together; paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy].
- **Deletion gap:** deleting a chat or source record does not prove removal from embeddings, summaries, model weights, caches, or generated reflections [paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning; paper:carlini-2021-training-data-extraction].
- **Ground-truth gap:** realistic dialogue or high self-consistency can be produced from memorized text, synthetic role-play, or a collapsed average without faithful individual variance [paper:quantifying-memorization-2023; paper:persona-dialogue-arimoto-2024; paper:synthetic-replacements-2024].

## Highest-Value Owner Questions

These questions should be asked only when the answer can change a concrete memory, authorization, or decision; information gain alone is not enough when burden and answerability differ [paper:boutilier-2003-active-cf; paper:harpale-2008-personalized-active-learning; paper:rashid-2008-information-theoretic-elicitation]. They can be answered without exposing raw third-party dialogue.

1. **Which kinds of facts may be global, and which must stay specific to a person, group, role, or audience?** This resolves high-risk scope ambiguity that message text alone cannot settle [paper:bell-1984-audience-design; paper:litt-hargittai-2016-imagined-audience; paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide].
2. **When two sources conflict, which source classes should normally win, and when should the system ask rather than choose?** This directly sets source-monitoring and abstention policy [paper:cognitive-memory-hashtroudi-johnson-chrosniak-1989; paper:persona-dialogue-welleck-2019].
3. **Which current claims are explicitly confirmed, outdated, context-specific, or unknown?** This creates temporal validity and owner-authority anchors without assuming recency is truth [paper:timechara-2024; paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics; paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles].
4. **Which repeated actions are actual routines, which are exceptions, and which should never be generalized?** This separates procedural evidence from value or motive stories [paper:rieskamp-otto-2006-strategy-selection; paper:bardi-schwartz-2003-values-behavior].
5. **Which future intentions are still active, what event or time should trigger them, and may the system remind you?** This supplies missing status, cue, and permission data for prospective memory [paper:cognitive-memory-einstein-mcdaniel-1990; paper:cognitive-memory-einstein-et-al-2005].
6. **What should be retained, archived, suppressed from ordinary retrieval, or deleted completely?** This distinguishes convenience forgetting from legal or ethical erasure [paper:cognitive-memory-murre-dros-2015; paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning].
7. **Which generated summaries or life themes do you endorse, reject, or want left explicitly uncertain?** This prevents reflection and narrative coherence from becoming unreviewed facts [paper:generative-agents-2023; paper:reese-et-al-2011-narrative-coherence].
8. **What important parts of your life are systematically missing from messaging, and should they remain missing?** This identifies coverage gaps without treating elicitation as mandatory or neutral [paper:dargembeau-garcia-jimenez-2024-working-self; paper:psychometrics_epstein_1983].
9. **For which decisions may the system simulate likely choices, and where must it only retrieve evidence or ask you?** Choice can shift with framing, option sets, and anchors, so authorization and uncertainty should vary by decision class [paper:tversky-kahneman-1981-framing; paper:tversky-simonson-1993-context-preferences; paper:ariely-2003-coherent-arbitrariness].
10. **Which third-party-derived information may be retained, summarized, or used in an answer, and for which audiences?** This establishes rights that archive possession cannot supply [paper:lampinen-et-al-2011-were-in-it-together; paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy; paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide].
