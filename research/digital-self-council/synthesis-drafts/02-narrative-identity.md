# Autobiographical and Narrative Identity

Record IDs in backticks are citations to the deduplicated `corpus.jsonl`. This
draft treats narrative identity as an owner-governed set of autobiographical
organizations, self-interpretations, and tellings, not as one latent story that
the system is entitled to complete (`paper:conway-bekerian-1987-organization`,
`paper:mcadams-et-al-2006-continuity-change`,
`paper:reese-et-al-2011-narrative-coherence`).

(`paper:generative-agents-1000-2024`, `paper:behaviorchain-2025`,
`paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026`).

## VALIDATED Constraints

### Working definitions

| Term | Operational definition for this program | Boundary that must remain visible | Evidence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Life period** | An owner-named, editable span of personal history that groups recurring situations, general events, or episodes and may overlap other periods. | A useful retrieval cue does not prove that human memory has one fixed hierarchical storage topology; culturally expected milestones are prompts, not inferred biography. | `paper:conway-bekerian-1987-organization`; `paper:thomsen-berntsen-2008-life-script-chapters`; `paper:chen-mcanally-reese-2013-memory-organization` |
| **Episode** | A source-linked representation of a particular event or bounded occurrence, with uncertain event time, participants, source class, and factual claims kept distinct from later meaning. | Episodic access is not interchangeable with semantic self-knowledge, and a recalled episode is not automatically historically verified. | `paper:cognitive-memory-vargha-khadem-1997`; `paper:blagov-singer-2004-four-dimensions`; `paper:cognitive-memory-hupbach-et-al-2007` |
| **Self-image** | An owner-generated identity expression, such as an `I am` statement, used as a retrieval cue and versioned with scope, estimated formation, retirement, support, and counterexamples. | It is not a timeless trait label or permission to infer identity from message style. Concrete and abstract self-images differ in temporal anchoring. | `paper:rathbone-moulin-conway-2008-self-centered`; `paper:haslam-et-al-2011-self-knowledge-identity`; `paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001` |
| **Narrative version** | A dated account that links one or more episodes to the owner's then-current meaning, affect, agency, perspective, audience, and purpose of telling. | A later version may revise interpretation without correcting facts, and an earlier version must not be silently overwritten. | `paper:mcadams-et-al-2006-continuity-change`; `paper:jardmo-et-al-2023-repeated-narratives`; `paper:mclean-2005-meaning-telling`; `paper:libby-eibach-2002-looking-back` |
| **Reconstructive recall** | A retrieval product shaped by cues, current self-concept, context, source competition, and later information rather than a literal playback of a stored record. | Reconstruction does not mean that every memory is false, and confidence or repetition does not establish accuracy. | `paper:conway-bekerian-1987-organization`; `paper:cognitive-memory-godden-baddeley-1975`; `paper:cognitive-memory-hupbach-et-al-2007`; `paper:libby-eibach-2002-looking-back` |
| **Continuity and change** | Separate observations about what recurs and what changes across event selection, factual claims, self-images, tone, agency, meaning, perspective, and relationship scope. | Recurrence is measured stability, not an immutable truth flag; change may reflect reconstruction, audience, development, or genuine revision. | `paper:mcadams-et-al-2006-continuity-change`; `paper:kober-habermas-2017-personal-past-stability`; `paper:jardmo-et-al-2023-repeated-narratives`; `paper:dargembeau-garcia-jimenez-2024-working-self` |
| **Owner elicitation** | A logged, optional, answerability-aware intervention that asks the owner to supply, distinguish, confirm, contest, scope, or withdraw autobiographical information. | Prompt, order, timing, audience, and skip behavior are part of provenance because elicitation can alter immediate self-description. | `paper:dargembeau-garcia-jimenez-2024-working-self`; `paper:rashid-2002-getting-to-know-you`; `paper:boutilier-2003-active-cf`; `paper:harpale-2008-personalized-active-learning` |
| **Narrative coherence** | Separate contextual, chronological, causal-motivational, and thematic annotations appropriate to the narrative unit and elicitation setting. | Coherence is not a single truth, maturity, health, or fidelity score, and the system must not repair an account into apparent coherence. | `paper:reese-et-al-2011-narrative-coherence`; `paper:habermas-de-silveira-2008-global-coherence`; `paper:kober-schmiedek-habermas-2015-coherence-development`; `paper:adler-2012-living-into-story` |

### Evidence-backed constraints

1. **Expose layered personal-history cues without asserting one hidden
   topology.** Owner-specific lifetime periods and general events can facilitate
   retrieval, while the same evidence leaves open whether the organization is
   stored hierarchically or constructed during search
   (`paper:conway-bekerian-1987-organization`,
   `paper:chen-mcanally-reese-2013-memory-organization`).

2. **Keep event evidence, semantic self-knowledge, and narrative interpretation
   distinct.** Severe episodic impairment can coexist with substantial semantic
   learning, while semantic and episodic self-knowledge show an unresolved,
   plausibly bidirectional relation to identity strength
   (`paper:cognitive-memory-vargha-khadem-1997`,
   `paper:haslam-et-al-2011-self-knowledge-identity`).

3. **Treat self-images as owner-authored retrieval handles.** Memories cluster
   around retrospectively dated self-image formation, especially for concrete
   roles, but those dates are estimates and the small follow-up does not justify
   converting them into timeless traits
   (`paper:rathbone-moulin-conway-2008-self-centered`).

4. **Do not reduce a self-defining memory to importance.** Specificity, meaning,
   content, and affect are separable coded dimensions, and relationship memories
   can contain closeness, separation, and conflict at the same time
   (`paper:blagov-singer-2004-four-dimensions`,
   `paper:mclean-thorne-2003-relationship-memories`).

5. **Store telling context as provenance.** Meaning differs with the purpose of
   telling, remembered audiences shift across development, and imagined
   audiences can change from message to message even when technical access does
   not (`paper:mclean-2005-meaning-telling`,
   `paper:litt-hargittai-2016-imagined-audience`).

6. **Keep coherence dimensions separate and descriptive.** Context, chronology,
   theme, temporal order, and causal-motivational structure develop on different
   schedules, and brief training or repeated narration did not reliably increase
   whole-life coherence (`paper:reese-et-al-2011-narrative-coherence`,
   `paper:habermas-de-silveira-2008-global-coherence`,
   `paper:kober-schmiedek-habermas-2015-coherence-development`).

7. **Represent continuity and revision together.** Repeated scenes and life-story
   segments retain recognizable features while tone, agency, complexity, and
   identity meaning can change (`paper:mcadams-et-al-2006-continuity-change`,
   `paper:kober-habermas-2017-personal-past-stability`,
   `paper:jardmo-et-al-2023-repeated-narratives`).

8. **Version recall rather than overwriting it.** Current self-compatibility can
   shift visual perspective, and reactivation followed by new information can
   produce delayed source intrusions; neither effect identifies a single correct
   reconstruction mechanism (`paper:libby-eibach-2002-looking-back`,
   `paper:cognitive-memory-hupbach-et-al-2007`).

9. **Do not force redemption, growth, resolution, or positivity.** Exploration
   and positive resolution follow different paths, differentiated growth
   reasoning was conditional on event valence, and coherence did not improve in
   the psychotherapy study even while agency increased
   (`paper:pals-2006-difficult-experiences`,
   `paper:lilgendahl-mcadams-2011-self-growth`,
   `paper:adler-2012-living-into-story`).

10. **Scope apparent contradictions by relationship, audience, and situation
    before globalizing them.** Relational-self activation, audience-conditioned
    self-presentation, and independent situation effects show why two different
    presentations may both be genuine observations
    (`paper:andersen-chen-2002-relational-self`,
    `paper:tice-et-al-1995-when-modesty-prevails`,
    `paper:psychometrics_sherman_2015`).

11. **Treat elicitation as measurement with cost and reactivity.** Questions are
    most defensible when answers can change a consequential decision and the
    owner can reasonably answer; a skip or `not sure` is query feedback, not
    negative autobiographical evidence
    (`paper:boutilier-2003-active-cf`,
    `paper:harpale-2008-personalized-active-learning`,
    `paper:dargembeau-garcia-jimenez-2024-working-self`).

## PLAUSIBLE Design Hypotheses

1. **Versioned autobiographical graph.** A graph with typed `life_period`,
   `general_event`, `episode`, `self_image`, `semantic_self_claim`, and
   `narrative_version` objects is a defensible engineering synthesis, provided
   that every edge retains source, time, scope, confidence, and owner status.
   No reviewed paper directly validates this software ontology
   (`paper:conway-bekerian-1987-organization`,
   `paper:haslam-et-al-2011-self-knowledge-identity`,
   `paper:jardmo-et-al-2023-repeated-narratives`,
   `paper:persona-dialogue-wang-2024-emg-rag`).

2. **Owner-authored period cueing.** Asking for owner-named chapters before
   individual episodes may improve coverage over generic keyword search, but
   generated chapter candidates should remain editable questions because prompts
   and cultural scripts shape what becomes salient
   (`paper:conway-bekerian-1987-organization`,
   `paper:thomsen-berntsen-2008-life-script-chapters`,
   `paper:chen-mcanally-reese-2013-memory-organization`).

3. **Probabilistic event matching.** A matcher can propose that two tellings refer
   to one episode using time, participants, setting, actions, and owner judgment,
   but it should preserve multiple candidates when details diverge. Recurrence
   supports identity matching, not factual adjudication
   (`paper:kober-habermas-2017-personal-past-stability`,
   `paper:jardmo-et-al-2023-repeated-narratives`,
   `paper:cognitive-memory-hashtroudi-johnson-chrosniak-1989`).

4. **Narrative-version branching.** `revises`, `coexists_with`, `contradicts`, and
   `supersedes` edges can distinguish factual correction from changed meaning,
   audience adaptation, or current-self distancing
   (`paper:mcadams-et-al-2006-continuity-change`,
   `paper:jardmo-et-al-2023-repeated-narratives`,
   `paper:libby-eibach-2002-looking-back`).

5. **Decision-aware elicitation scheduler.** Questions can be ranked by expected
   decision change, answerability, uncertainty, coverage, sensitivity, and owner
   burden, while difficult memories remain opt-in and skippable
   (`paper:rashid-2002-getting-to-know-you`, `paper:boutilier-2003-active-cf`,
   `paper:harpale-2008-personalized-active-learning`,
   `paper:pals-2006-difficult-experiences`).

6. **Fast evidence, slow identity update.** New observations can enter an
   inspectable episodic layer immediately, while generalized self-claims are
   proposed slowly and tested against older supporting and contradicting
   evidence (`paper:cognitive-memory-mcclelland-mcnaughton-oreilly-1995`,
   `paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics`,
   `paper:psychometrics_epstein_1983`).

7. **Claim-type calibration.** Event facts, remembered meanings, self-images,
   relationship-scoped interpretations, and predictions can carry separate
   confidence models and abstention thresholds; factual conversational
   calibration is only an indirect precedent
   (`paper:linguistic-calibration-2022`,
   `paper:cognitive-memory-hashtroudi-johnson-chrosniak-1989`).

8. **Owner-visible narrative diffs.** Showing what changed in facts, meaning,
   affect, agency, audience, or scope may make correction safer than editing an
   opaque profile, although short profile-control studies provide mixed evidence
   about objective effects (`paper:jardmo-et-al-2023-repeated-narratives`,
   `paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles`,
   `paper:bostandjiev-2012-tasteweights`).

## SPECULATIVE Claims

The following claims are useful research targets, not supported product facts:

- **WhatsApp can reveal the owner's complete life-period structure.** Messaging
  samples selected relationships and tellings, while life periods in the
  evidence were explicitly elicited and culturally shaped
  (`paper:chen-mcanally-reese-2013-memory-organization`,
  `paper:mclean-2005-meaning-telling`,
  `paper:litt-hargittai-2016-imagined-audience`).
- **A model can infer a durable self-image from repeated language.** Self-images
  were owner-generated in the relevant study, and repeated states remain
  situation-dependent rather than direct trait readouts
  (`paper:rathbone-moulin-conway-2008-self-centered`,
  `paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001`,
  `paper:psychometrics_sherman_2015`).
- **Narrative coherence predicts truth, maturity, health, or fidelity.** The
  coherence dimensions are method- and development-dependent, brief training
  produced null change, and psychotherapy coherence did not reliably increase
  (`paper:reese-et-al-2011-narrative-coherence`,
  `paper:habermas-de-silveira-2008-global-coherence`,
  `paper:adler-2012-living-into-story`).
- **A narrative graph will improve behavioral simulation.** Interview-grounded
  agents improve some survey predictions, but gains vary by domain and real
  sequential action remains difficult (`paper:generative-agents-1000-2024`,
  `paper:behaviorchain-2025`, `paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026`).
- **The base model can fill missing life history with plausible events.** Fluent
  population simulation can compress variance or substitute normative answers,
  and future information can leak into past-person responses
  (`paper:synthetic-replacements-2024`, `paper:timechara-2024`,
  `paper:turing-experiments-2023`).
- **More elicitation yields a more authentic self.** Repeated narration and brief
  reasoning training did not reliably raise coherence, elicitation can shift the
  immediate working self, and owner editing can reduce objective profile
  performance (`paper:habermas-de-silveira-2008-global-coherence`,
  `paper:dargembeau-garcia-jimenez-2024-working-self`,
  `paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles`).

## Contradictions And Boundaries

| Evidence tension | Boundary for synthesis |
| --- | --- |
| Lifetime-period cues speed retrieval, but the same full-text paper cannot distinguish stored hierarchy from cue-built retrieval plans (`paper:conway-bekerian-1987-organization`). | Expose cue paths; do not claim one biological or software topology is necessary. |
| Coherence generally rises across development, but short training and repetition produced null effects, and psychotherapy coherence did not reliably rise (`paper:habermas-de-silveira-2008-global-coherence`, `paper:kober-schmiedek-habermas-2015-coherence-development`, `paper:adler-2012-living-into-story`). | Annotate dimensions; never optimize the owner toward a presumed coherent endpoint. |
| Semantic self-knowledge statistically mediated episode-identity relations, but reverse mediation also fit (`paper:haslam-et-al-2011-self-knowledge-identity`). | Keep links bidirectional and causal direction unknown. |
| Life-story segments recur over years, yet repetition is not historical accuracy and current self-concept changes recall perspective (`paper:kober-habermas-2017-personal-past-stability`, `paper:libby-eibach-2002-looking-back`). | Store recurrence and perspective separately from factual confidence. |
| Positive or differentiated growth reasoning correlates with some well-being measures, but effects depend on event valence, socioeconomic context, selection, and retrospective interpretation (`paper:lilgendahl-mcadams-2011-self-growth`, `paper:pals-2006-difficult-experiences`). | Do not generate redemption or infer well-being from narrative form. |
| Users report valuing profile control, while direct editing harmed objective metrics in one small study and added feedback confounded another (`paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles`, `paper:bostandjiev-2012-tasteweights`, `paper:bakalov-2013-controllable-user-models`). | Owner authority is non-negotiable, but controls need preview, scope, rollback, and separate evaluation. |
| Interview and survey grounding improve some person-level predictions, while economic games, covariance, sequential behavior, and real next actions remain weak or inconsistent (`paper:generative-agents-1000-2024`, `paper:synthetic-replacements-2024`, `paper:behaviorchain-2025`, `paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026`). | Report fidelity by domain and horizon; never average into one digital-self score. |
| Different audiences elicit different self-presentations, and imagined audiences vary per message (`paper:tice-et-al-1995-when-modesty-prevails`, `paper:litt-hargittai-2016-imagined-audience`, `paper:andersen-chen-2002-relational-self`). | Test relationship and audience scope before declaring a contradiction or stable change. |
| Reactivation can be followed by delayed intrusion, but ordinary source confusion remains a viable explanation (`paper:cognitive-memory-hupbach-et-al-2007`). | Preserve versions and source candidates; do not label every later detail reconsolidated truth. |

### Core objects

| --- | --- | --- |

### Write and retrieval rules

1. Append a source observation without promoting it to an episode, self-image,
   or identity claim until attribution and scope are known
   (`paper:cognitive-memory-hashtroudi-johnson-chrosniak-1989`,
   `paper:psychometrics_sherman_2015`).
2. Preserve source evidence separately from derived narrative objects; factual
   corrections create a new version, while authorized deletion propagates
   through indexes and derived artifacts (`paper:cognitive-memory-hupbach-et-al-2007`,
   `paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning`).
3. Never use generated responses, summaries, inferred motives, or imagined
   future events as autobiographical evidence merely because the model produced
   them (`paper:dargembeau-garcia-jimenez-2024-working-self`,
   `paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026`).
4. Retrieve by owner-defined period, event, time, relationship, audience, and
   current task, returning competing versions and source uncertainty rather than
   one polished story (`paper:conway-bekerian-1987-organization`,
   `paper:litt-hargittai-2016-imagined-audience`,
   `paper:cognitive-memory-godden-baddeley-1975`).
5. Filter information by what was known at the requested point in time and let
   the system state `unknown then` even when a later record contains the answer
   (`paper:timechara-2024`, `paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics`).
6. Annotate coherence without rewriting the owner's sequence, causal account,
   or theme; ambiguity and contradiction remain queryable states
   (`paper:reese-et-al-2011-narrative-coherence`,
   `paper:adler-2012-living-into-story`).
7. Require owner confirmation before a high-impact narrative interpretation or
   self-image changes durable behavior, and expose the behavioral effect and
   rollback path (`paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles`,
   `paper:bostandjiev-2012-tasteweights`).
8. Enforce third-party and purpose constraints before retrieval, not by asking a
   generator to remember privacy instructions after private context is present
   (`paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy`,
   `paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide`).

## Evaluation Tests

| Test | Passing evidence | Anchoring records |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Life-period cue retrieval | Owner-named periods improve retrieval coverage or latency over generic cues without increasing unsupported episode claims; results are reported per owner and culture. | `paper:conway-bekerian-1987-organization`; `paper:chen-mcanally-reese-2013-memory-organization` |
| Episode versus semantic separation | The system distinguishes a remembered event, generalized self-knowledge, and an unsupported inference, and can retrieve one without fabricating the other. | `paper:cognitive-memory-vargha-khadem-1997`; `paper:haslam-et-al-2011-self-knowledge-identity` |
| Episode identity and versioning | Repeated tellings can be linked to one candidate event while changed facts, meanings, and perspectives remain separately visible. | `paper:kober-habermas-2017-personal-past-stability`; `paper:jardmo-et-al-2023-repeated-narratives`; `paper:libby-eibach-2002-looking-back` |
| Source-confusion challenge | Later details, reminders, quotations, and same-class sources do not silently merge; the system returns source candidates and calibrated uncertainty. | `paper:cognitive-memory-hupbach-et-al-2007`; `paper:cognitive-memory-hashtroudi-johnson-chrosniak-1989` |
| Coherence non-manufacture | Low or mixed coherence is preserved verbatim; annotation changes no event order, causal link, theme, affect, or resolution unless the owner edits it. | `paper:reese-et-al-2011-narrative-coherence`; `paper:habermas-de-silveira-2008-global-coherence`; `paper:adler-2012-living-into-story` |
| Continuity/change discrimination | Tests separately score stable event identity, stable and changed meanings, relationship scope, and factual correction across chronological holdouts. | `paper:mcadams-et-al-2006-continuity-change`; `paper:jardmo-et-al-2023-repeated-narratives`; `paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics` |
| Relationship-scope test | The same episode or self-description can differ appropriately by audience without leaking one relationship's information or flattening all versions into a global persona. | `paper:andersen-chen-2002-relational-self`; `paper:tice-et-al-1995-when-modesty-prevails`; `paper:lampinen-et-al-2011-were-in-it-together` |
| Point-in-time test | The system answers from information available at the target cutoff, marks future and never-known facts, and reports temporal leakage separately. | `paper:timechara-2024`; `paper:synthetic-replacements-2024` |
| Elicitation reactivity test | Re-asking or changing prompt order does not silently overwrite the profile; induced shifts are logged and consequential claims are confirmed outside the immediate prompt context. | `paper:dargembeau-garcia-jimenez-2024-working-self`; `paper:habermas-de-silveira-2008-global-coherence` |
| Question-policy test | Questions improve consequential uncertainty or coverage at acceptable burden, while skips and `not sure` reduce repeated unanswerable prompts rather than altering identity. | `paper:boutilier-2003-active-cf`; `paper:harpale-2008-personalized-active-learning`; `paper:rashid-2002-getting-to-know-you` |
| Calibration and abstention | Reliability curves are separate for episode facts, owner meanings, inferred self-images, and predictions; unsupported claims trigger abstention. | `paper:linguistic-calibration-2022`; `paper:persona-dialogue-welleck-2019` |
| Owner correction and rollback | Typed corrections update only the intended scope, previews match committed behavior, and rollback and deletion reach summaries, graphs, indexes, and learned artifacts. | `paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles`; `paper:bostandjiev-2012-tasteweights`; `paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning` |
| Predictive validity by domain | Narrative features are evaluated on held-out owner responses or actions, normalized by owner retest reliability, with variance, covariance, and trajectory errors kept separate. | `paper:generative-agents-1000-2024`; `paper:synthetic-replacements-2024`; `paper:behaviorchain-2025`; `paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026` |
| Privacy and co-ownership gate | A test suite attempts cross-recipient disclosure, unauthorized third-party reuse, purpose drift, and deleted-memory recovery; a severe failure blocks release. | `paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy`; `paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide`; `paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning` |

## WhatsApp Observability Gaps

No private WhatsApp content, profile, database, credential, or key was accessed
for this synthesis.

- A message shows a selected telling to a particular audience, not the owner's
  complete life story, untold memories, or stable identity
  (`paper:mclean-2005-meaning-telling`,
  `paper:litt-hargittai-2016-imagined-audience`).
- A send timestamp is not necessarily event time, life-period boundary, self-image
  formation, or the validity interval of a belief
  (`paper:rathbone-moulin-conway-2008-self-centered`,
  `paper:timechara-2024`).
- Silence and non-mention do not distinguish forgetting, privacy, irrelevance,
  an offline channel, or a missing cue
  (`paper:cognitive-memory-godden-baddeley-1975`,
  `paper:kober-habermas-2017-personal-past-stability`).
- Message text rarely establishes whether an event is self-defining, what it
  means now, its recalled affect, or the owner's visual perspective
  (`paper:blagov-singer-2004-four-dimensions`,
  `paper:libby-eibach-2002-looking-back`).
- Repetition may identify a recurring telling but does not verify event accuracy,
  importance, or unchanged meaning
  (`paper:kober-habermas-2017-personal-past-stability`,
  `paper:jardmo-et-al-2023-repeated-narratives`).
- Quotation, forwarding, irony, roleplay, group consensus, and later source
  intrusion complicate ownership and source attribution
  (`paper:cognitive-memory-hashtroudi-johnson-chrosniak-1989`,
  `paper:cognitive-memory-hupbach-et-al-2007`).
- Differences across chats can reflect relational self, audience design, role,
  topic, or situation rather than global identity change
  (`paper:andersen-chen-2002-relational-self`,
  `paper:tice-et-al-1995-when-modesty-prevails`,
  `paper:psychometrics_sherman_2015`).
- Messages do not expose alternatives considered, offline action, later regret,
  or whether a narrated intention was enacted
  (`paper:tversky-kahneman-1981-framing`,
  `paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026`).
- Relationship memories can be central to the owner while disclosing another
  person's information; archive ownership does not settle that person's consent
  (`paper:mclean-thorne-2003-relationship-memories`,
  `paper:lampinen-et-al-2011-were-in-it-together`,
  `paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy`).
- Missing media, edits, deletions, calls, disappearing content, screenshots, and
  off-platform retellings make the observed archive incomplete and its reachable
  audience uncertain (`paper:marwick-boyd-2011-context-collapse`,
  `paper:litt-hargittai-2016-imagined-audience`,
  `paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide`).
- Channel-conditioned imitation is not evidence of life-story fidelity or future
  behavior; acted long-term chats can show distorted intimacy progression
  (`paper:persona-dialogue-arimoto-2024`,
  `paper:behaviorchain-2025`, `paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026`).

## Highest-Value Owner Questions

Questions are optional, scoped, and skippable. Difficult experiences should
never be required to complete a profile (`paper:pals-2006-difficult-experiences`,
`paper:lilgendahl-mcadams-2011-self-growth`).

| Priority | Owner question | Why this has high information value |
| ---: | --- | --- |
| 1 | "What chapters or periods would you use to divide your life, and which ones overlap?" | Establishes owner-authored retrieval cues without importing a cultural life script (`paper:conway-bekerian-1987-organization`; `paper:thomsen-berntsen-2008-life-script-chapters`). |
| 2 | "For this period, what recurring situations belong here, and which specific events stand out?" | Separates lifetime periods, general events, and episodes without demanding equal detail in every period (`paper:chen-mcanally-reese-2013-memory-organization`; `paper:conway-bekerian-1987-organization`). |
| 3 | "Which `I am` descriptions feel current, former, relationship-specific, aspirational, or never quite true?" | Creates owner-authored self-image cues while recording scope and retirement instead of timeless traits (`paper:rathbone-moulin-conway-2008-self-centered`; `paper:andersen-chen-2002-relational-self`). |
| 4 | "For this event, what happened, what are you unsure about, and what does it mean to you now?" | Separates factual claims, source uncertainty, and current meaning (`paper:blagov-singer-2004-four-dimensions`; `paper:cognitive-memory-hashtroudi-johnson-chrosniak-1989`). |
| 5 | "Have you told this story differently before? What changed: facts, emphasis, feeling, audience, or meaning?" | Distinguishes correction from ordinary narrative revision and audience adaptation (`paper:jardmo-et-al-2023-repeated-narratives`; `paper:mclean-2005-meaning-telling`). |
| 6 | "What event or example does not fit this self-description?" | Solicits counterevidence before a self-image or theme becomes durable (`paper:rathbone-moulin-conway-2008-self-centered`; `paper:psychometrics_epstein_1983`). |
| 7 | "Who was this story for, who else might hear it, and what should remain private to that relationship?" | Captures intended, imagined, and permitted audiences and third-party boundaries (`paper:litt-hargittai-2016-imagined-audience`; `paper:lampinen-et-al-2011-were-in-it-together`; `paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy`). |
| 8 | "Does this difference across times or relationships feel like change, a different role, an unresolved conflict, or something else?" | Prevents role and situation variation from being forced into global change or inconsistency (`paper:andersen-chen-2002-relational-self`; `paper:psychometrics_sherman_2015`; `paper:mcadams-et-al-2006-continuity-change`). |
| 9 | "Would you like to leave this difficult experience unresolved, describe it without a lesson, or skip it entirely?" | Preserves ambivalence and rejects forced growth or positive resolution (`paper:pals-2006-difficult-experiences`; `paper:lilgendahl-mcadams-2011-self-growth`; `paper:adler-2012-living-into-story`). |
| 10 | "Which version should guide responses now, which older versions should remain visible, and which should not be used?" | Separates current validity, historical preservation, and no-use controls (`paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics`; `paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles`; `paper:timechara-2024`). |
| 11 | "May this event and its interpretation be retained, retrieved, summarized, or used for simulation, and whose information also appears in it?" | Makes capability- and subject-specific permission explicit before reuse (`paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy`; `paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide`). |
| 12 | "Is there anything the system should correct, mark uncertain, scope to one context, hide, or delete?" | Elicits typed correction instead of one destructive edit and provides an executable deletion intent (`paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles`; `paper:bakalov-2013-controllable-user-models`; `paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning`). |
