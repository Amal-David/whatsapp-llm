# Fidelity Evaluation: A Multi-Axis Benchmark

## Evidence scope

This synthesis uses the deduplicated, independently reviewed `corpus.jsonl`: 187 counted primary papers, of which 100 were inspected at full-text depth and 87 at abstract depth. It uses only merged `record_id` values. The benchmark target is not a claim that a system has replicated a person; it is a set of bounded, falsifiable claims about where a system predicts, remembers, adapts, abstains, and preserves boundaries.

The strongest evaluation evidence comes from full-text work on user simulators, individual agents, long-term memory, temporal roleplay, calibration, and privacy attacks. Evidence for autobiographical identity, cognitive memory, values, and social relationships is more abstract-heavy, so those literatures can justify test dimensions and failure hypotheses but not universal numerical pass thresholds. Representative abstract-depth boundaries include state variability, narrative stability, framing, audience design, and source monitoring (`paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001`, `paper:kober-habermas-2017-personal-past-stability`, `paper:tversky-kahneman-1981-framing`, `paper:bell-1984-audience-design`, `paper:cognitive-memory-hashtroudi-johnson-chrosniak-1989`).

The evaluation unit should be a versioned tuple, not a free-floating prompt: owner, evidence cutoff, model and policy version, relationship or audience, task domain, evidence class, allowed sources, and expected answerability. Time-bound hallucination, model-update drift, relationship conditioning, and source confusability all change the meaning of an otherwise identical response (`paper:timechara-2024`, `paper:synthetic-replacements-2024`, `paper:andersen-chen-2002-relational-self`, `paper:cognitive-memory-hashtroudi-johnson-chrosniak-1989`).

Four recurring false positives motivate the benchmark. First, surface resemblance and generic dialogue metrics can disagree with human persona-consistency judgments (`paper:persona-dialogue-madotto-2019`). Second, aggregate means can look human while individual choices, variance, covariance, subgroup behavior, or trajectories are wrong (`paper:out-of-one-many-2023`, `paper:synthetic-replacements-2024`, `paper:behaviorchain-2025`). Third, unusually stable or clean psychometric answers can be less human-like rather than more faithful (`paper:emulate-personality-2025`, `paper:personality-temporal-stability-2024`). Fourth, verbatim private detail can be memorization or extraction rather than autobiographical fidelity (`paper:carlini-2021-training-data-extraction`, `paper:quantifying-memorization-2023`, `paper:lukas-2023-pii-leakage`).

Accordingly, this benchmark isolates surface style as a diagnostic axis, prohibits it from compensating for failures elsewhere, and evaluates behavioral, temporal, relational, autobiographical, decision, calibration, privacy, leakage, and owner-judgment fidelity separately. Persona consistency, engagement, knowledge, social behavior, and predictive action already diverge in the reviewed evidence (`paper:persona-dialogue-zhang-2018`, `paper:charactereval-2024`, `paper:sotopia-2024`, `paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026`).

## VALIDATED constraints

Here, **VALIDATED** means directly supported within one or more reviewed study settings. It does not mean that the complete benchmark has been validated on a WhatsApp-derived digital self.

1. **Fidelity is a vector, not a scalar.** Profile entailment, contradiction, engagement, character knowledge, personality, social goal completion, relationship appropriateness, and actual behavior are separable constructs with different evaluator reliability (`paper:persona-dialogue-zhang-2018`, `paper:persona-dialogue-welleck-2019`, `paper:charactereval-2024`, `paper:sotopia-2024`).

2. **Plausible language is not predictive validity.** LLM user simulators can sound coherent while disagreeing with human preferences, producing generic requests, contradicting their own feedback, over-purchasing, or missing iterative human action patterns (`paper:llm-user-sim-crs-2024`, `paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026`).

3. **Prospective held-out behavior is stronger evidence than retrospective resemblance.** Policies trained on a simulator can transfer to real users, but longer optimization can exploit simulator-specific behavior; chained predictions also compound early errors (`paper:neural-user-simulation-2018`, `paper:behaviorchain-2025`).

4. **Aggregate agreement does not establish individual fidelity.** Conditioned models can reproduce population or subgroup patterns while lacking person-specific correspondence, compressing variance, changing covariance, or substituting normative answers for individual behavior (`paper:out-of-one-many-2023`, `paper:synthetic-replacements-2024`, `paper:twin-2k-500-2025`).

5. **Human reliability is a ceiling and a denominator, not an inconvenience.** Individual-agent studies improve interpretability by reporting model agreement relative to the person's own retest agreement, while also showing domain-specific nulls and incomplete correlation structure (`paper:generative-agents-1000-2024`, `paper:twin-2k-500-2025`).

6. **Means, dispersion, dependence, and trajectory must all be tested.** Models have reproduced average effects while showing nonhuman hyper-accuracy, too little response variance, altered subgroup relationships, excess expected-value rationality, and weak cumulative chain scores (`paper:turing-experiments-2023`, `paper:synthetic-replacements-2024`, `paper:llms-more-rational-2025`, `paper:behaviorchain-2025`).

7. **Psychometric reliability alone is insufficient.** Trait alignment and internal consistency can coexist with implausibly pure factor loadings, weakened human-like intertrait dependencies, social desirability, and model- or instrument-dependent temporal instability (`paper:incharacter-2024`, `paper:emulate-personality-2025`, `paper:personality-temporal-stability-2024`).

8. **Time is part of correctness.** A fact can be globally true yet wrong for the represented point in time; model updates can also change answers to an unchanged instrument, and old evidence can contain enduring signal that simple recency decay would erase (`paper:timechara-2024`, `paper:synthetic-replacements-2024`, `paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics`).

9. **Relationship and audience are part of correctness.** Self-presentation changes between friends and strangers, partner-specific wording can persist, discourse act changes accommodation, and imagined audiences can differ from actual audiences (`paper:tice-et-al-1995-when-modesty-prevails`, `paper:brennan-clark-1996-conceptual-pacts`, `paper:doyle-frank-2016-sources-linguistic-alignment`, `paper:litt-hargittai-2016-imagined-audience`, `paper:bernstein-et-al-2013-invisible-audience`).

10. **Autobiographical fidelity requires source and version fidelity, not polished narrative.** Event specificity, meaning, content, and affect are distinct; repeated narratives can preserve events while changing interpretation; repetition does not establish historical accuracy; and reactivation can introduce delayed source intrusions (`paper:blagov-singer-2004-four-dimensions`, `paper:jardmo-et-al-2023-repeated-narratives`, `paper:kober-habermas-2017-personal-past-stability`, `paper:cognitive-memory-hupbach-et-al-2007`).

11. **Observed choices are conditional measurements.** Equivalent frames, option-set changes, arbitrary anchors, and noisy pre-choice ratings can change or misrepresent apparent preference without revealing one context-free utility (`paper:tversky-kahneman-1981-framing`, `paper:tversky-simonson-1993-context-preferences`, `paper:ariely-2003-coherent-arbitrariness`, `paper:chen-risen-2010-free-choice`).

12. **Declared values, active goals, and enacted behavior are different evidence.** Value-behavior associations vary by domain and norm, focal goals can temporarily suppress alternatives, and situational or interpersonal factors can affect resistance and enactment (`paper:bardi-schwartz-2003-values-behavior`, `paper:shah-2002-goal-shielding`, `paper:hofmann-2012-everyday-temptations`).

13. **Confidence must be evaluated against correctness and answerability.** Conversational agents can be linguistically overconfident, and human metacognitive judgments can be inflated by cues unavailable at test time and then misdirect later choices (`paper:linguistic-calibration-2022`, `paper:cognitive-memory-koriat-bjork-2005`, `paper:cognitive-memory-metcalfe-finn-2008`).

14. **Privacy fidelity is an information-flow property.** Strong models disclosed designated private information to inappropriate recipients, and reminders or reasoning prompts did not reliably prevent disclosure; third-party data also has interests not exhausted by the archive owner's choice (`paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide`, `paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy`).

15. **Ordinary alignment behavior is not a leakage test.** Membership inference, exact extraction, PII reconstruction, long-context memorization, and targeted extraction can succeed even when standard prompts, output truncation, scrubbing, or ordinary refusals appear reassuring (`paper:shokri-2017-membership-inference`, `paper:carlini-2021-training-data-extraction`, `paper:quantifying-memorization-2023`, `paper:lukas-2023-pii-leakage`, `paper:aligned-production-extraction-2025`).

16. **Style mimicry cannot establish person replication.** Persona access can improve consistency without improving engagement, common text metrics can move opposite to human consistency ratings, and acted long-term chats can have unnatural intimacy patterns (`paper:persona-dialogue-zhang-2018`, `paper:persona-dialogue-madotto-2019`, `paper:persona-dialogue-arimoto-2024`).

## PLAUSIBLE design hypotheses

The following benchmark design is evidence-grounded but has not been validated end to end.

1. **Use a non-compensatory scorecard.** Report every fidelity axis separately, with coverage, uncertainty, baselines, and failure examples. Do not publish a single "percent like the owner" because success on persona consistency, aggregate response patterns, or owner recognition does not imply correct action, time, relationship, or privacy behavior (`paper:charactereval-2024`, `paper:synthetic-replacements-2024`, `paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026`, `paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide`).

2. **Make safety failures vetoes rather than negative weights.** A severe cross-relationship disclosure, future-knowledge leak, sensitive-trait reconstruction, or private-text extraction should block the affected capability even when style and owner preference scores are high (`paper:timechara-2024`, `paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide`, `paper:lukas-2023-pii-leakage`, `paper:aligned-production-extraction-2025`).

3. **Freeze cases independently of model configuration.** A case should retain the evidence cutoff, relationship, prompt family, allowed evidence, outcome definition, and private answer key so generic, style-only, profile-only, retrieval-only, and full systems face the same target (`paper:timechara-2024`, `paper:synthetic-replacements-2024`, `paper:generative-agents-1000-2024`).

4. **Use nested holdouts that defeat archive imitation.** Hold out complete future time blocks, whole conversations or episodes, one relationship at a time, decision outcomes, owner retest items, and paraphrased or novel variants; random message splits alone can leak duplicate language and nearby context (`paper:kandpal-2022-dedup-privacy`, `paper:quantifying-memorization-2023`, `paper:locomo-2024`, `paper:generative-agents-1000-2024`).

5. **Include a style-only adversary.** Build a baseline that receives aggregate style features and representative phrasing but no identity claims, memories, relationship state, or decision history. A full system must beat that baseline on content and action axes, while style-swapped and content-swapped controls test whether judges are rewarding voice over owner-specific substance (`paper:persona-dialogue-madotto-2019`, `paper:persona-dialogue-li-2016`, `paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026`).

6. **Normalize prediction against owner retest reliability within domain.** Report both raw agreement and the fraction of the owner's repeatable signal recovered, but never compare normalized survey accuracy directly with a one-off consequential decision or natural action stream (`paper:generative-agents-1000-2024`, `paper:twin-2k-500-2025`, `paper:hofmann-2012-everyday-temptations`).

7. **Calibrate by evidence class.** Direct owner confirmation, observed sent behavior, source-linked memory, relationship-conditioned inference, latent trait estimate, and future-action prediction should have separate reliability curves and abstention policies because their ground truth and observability differ (`paper:linguistic-calibration-2022`, `paper:cognitive-memory-koriat-bjork-2005`, `paper:psychometrics_mairesse_2007`).

8. **Repeat owner judgment under blinding and washout.** Randomized A/B comparisons should separate characteristic voice, likely action, factual grounding, relationship appropriateness, temporal correctness, uncertainty, and privacy comfort; repeated ratings estimate owner-judge reliability rather than treating one preference click as truth (`paper:persona-dialogue-zhang-2018`, `paper:charactereval-2024`, `paper:incharacter-2024`, `paper:generative-agents-1000-2024`).

9. **Evaluate updates as typed transitions.** Tests should distinguish a new observation, owner correction, supersession, context-only exception, temporary state, and deletion. New-period utility and old-period retention should be reported separately because anti-forgetting mechanisms can help in drifting streams and add little or hurt in stable ones (`paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics`, `paper:mi-2020-ader`, `paper:xu-2020-graphsail`).

10. **Ask only high-value owner questions.** Elicitation should target consequential uncertainty, answerability, and expected decision change, with "skip" and "not sure" treated as valid outcomes rather than evidence about the owner (`paper:rashid-2002-getting-to-know-you`, `paper:boutilier-2003-active-cf`, `paper:harpale-2008-personalized-active-learning`).

11. **Keep private evaluation evidence separate from aggregate reports.** Detailed self-reports and personal text increase simulation signal but also increase deanonymization, consent-withdrawal, and extraction risk; public or shareable reports should contain only aggregate coverage, score distributions, and sanitized failure classes (`paper:generative-agents-1000-2024`, `paper:carlini-2021-training-data-extraction`, `paper:lukas-2023-pii-leakage`).

## SPECULATIVE claims

1. It is **speculative** that strong performance on this benchmark will predict safe, faithful behavior in all natural relationships or consequential offline decisions; current agent studies are dominated by surveys, fictional behavior chains, bounded shopping, or controlled dialogue (`paper:generative-agents-1000-2024`, `paper:behaviorchain-2025`, `paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026`, `paper:sotopia-2024`).

2. It is **speculative** that authorized WhatsApp history plus an owner interview is sufficient to build a broadly predictive digital self. Messaging samples sent behavior in selected relationships, while interview-grounded agents still show domain nulls and incomplete sample-level structure (`paper:generative-agents-1000-2024`, `paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001`, `paper:andersen-chen-2002-relational-self`).

3. It is **speculative** that leave-one-relationship-out performance will transfer to unseen relationships. Relational selves, audience design, and partner-specific pacts predict context dependence, not universal cross-relationship generalization (`paper:andersen-chen-2002-relational-self`, `paper:bell-1984-audience-design`, `paper:brennan-clark-1996-conceptual-pacts`).

4. It is **speculative** that retrieval-backed systems leak less private information than fine-tuned systems. Retrieval is inspectable and editable, but retrieved benign memories can also legitimize harmful intent, and the reviewed extraction studies do not directly compare matched personal retrieval and tuning systems (`paper:personalization-legitimizes-risks-2026`, `paper:carlini-2021-training-data-extraction`, `paper:lukas-2023-pii-leakage`).

5. It is **speculative** that close-contact judgments improve truth rather than add another relationship-scoped reputation criterion. Observer and self-report targets can diverge, and relationship context changes what is visible (`paper:psychometrics_mairesse_2007`, `paper:psychometrics_youyou_2015`, `paper:tice-et-al-1995-when-modesty-prevails`).

6. It is **speculative** that one normalized pass threshold can apply across behavioral, autobiographical, relational, and decision domains. Human repeatability, opportunity, consequence, and measurement error differ across domains and instruments (`paper:generative-agents-1000-2024`, `paper:personality-temporal-stability-2024`, `paper:chen-risen-2010-free-choice`).

7. It is **speculative** that style-stripped or style-swapped controls are psychologically neutral. They are useful anti-mimicry diagnostics, but altering wording can change framing, audience meaning, conceptual pacts, and perceived disclosure appropriateness (`paper:tversky-kahneman-1981-framing`, `paper:brennan-clark-1996-conceptual-pacts`, `paper:bazarova-2012-public-intimacy`).

## Contradictions and boundaries

| Tension | Evidence on one side | Negative or boundary evidence | Benchmark resolution |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Population simulation vs individual fidelity | Backstory conditioning reproduced several aggregate political patterns (`paper:out-of-one-many-2023`). | Means can hide compressed variance, altered subgroup relations, and no person-specific correspondence (`paper:synthetic-replacements-2024`, `paper:twin-2k-500-2025`). | Score individual agreement, distributions, subgroups, and trajectories separately. |
| High survey agreement vs real behavior | Self-report-grounded agents and broad twins recovered substantial repeatable survey signal (`paper:generative-agents-1000-2024`, `paper:twin-2k-500-2025`). | Economic-game differences were null or underpowered, several causal effects failed, and exact shopping actions remained low (`paper:generative-agents-1000-2024`, `paper:twin-2k-500-2025`, `paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026`). | Never transfer a pass across domains without a held-out outcome in that domain. |
| Stable psychometrics vs human-like psychology | Interviewed role agents and personality emulations can align strongly with target labels (`paper:incharacter-2024`, `paper:emulate-personality-2025`). | Factor loadings can be implausibly pure, dependencies weakened, and retest stability uneven (`paper:emulate-personality-2025`, `paper:personality-temporal-stability-2024`). | Require factor, criterion, temporal, and behavioral validity, not alpha or label agreement alone. |
| Memory utility vs safety | Long-context retrieval, segmentation, and explicit update handling improve memory tasks (`paper:locomo-2024`, `paper:longmemeval-2025`, `paper:secom-2025`). | Retrieved benign memories and persona priors can increase harmful-query success (`paper:personalization-legitimizes-risks-2026`). | Run authorization and safety evaluation after retrieval, and prohibit automatic write-back from generated output. |
| Owner control vs objective performance | Interactive controls can raise subjective control and satisfaction (`paper:bostandjiev-2012-tasteweights`, `paper:bakalov-2013-controllable-user-models`). | Direct profile editing reduced precision and recall in one system, while positive studies confounded control with added feedback (`paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles`, `paper:bostandjiev-2012-tasteweights`). | Owner authority is final, but every edit gets a scoped preview, reversible delta, and separate utility report. |
| Narrative continuity vs factual truth | Life-story segments and narrative features show recurrence over years (`paper:kober-habermas-2017-personal-past-stability`, `paper:mcadams-et-al-2006-continuity-change`). | Repetition is not accuracy, changed tellings can add new meaning, and current self-concept changes recollective perspective (`paper:jardmo-et-al-2023-repeated-narratives`, `paper:libby-eibach-2002-looking-back`). | Test event identity, source, factual claims, and current interpretation as separate fields. |
| Temporal modeling vs overfitting time | Temporal models improve future prediction in several recommendation settings (`paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics`, `paper:li-2020-tisasrec`). | Some domains show weak sequential structure, and indiscriminate aging discards durable evidence (`paper:li-2020-tisasrec`, `paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics`). | Require a static baseline and domain-specific drift evidence before enabling temporal complexity. |
| Style matching vs relationship inference | Dyadic similarity and turn-contingent accommodation are measurable (`paper:ireland-et-al-2011-language-style-matching`, `paper:danescu-niculescu-mizil-et-al-2011-mark-my-words`). | Alignment depends on lexical item and discourse act, is often asymmetric, and does not establish motive, closeness, or long-term change (`paper:doyle-frank-2016-sources-linguistic-alignment`, `paper:danescu-niculescu-mizil-et-al-2011-mark-my-words`). | Treat style matching as a local interaction feature, never a relationship-health or identity label. |
| Privacy defenses vs residual leakage | Differential privacy and deduplication reduce measured privacy risk (`paper:abadi-2016-deep-learning-dp`, `paper:kandpal-2022-dedup-privacy`). | Privacy units, unique secrets, PII reconstruction, and targeted extraction leave residual exposure (`paper:lukas-2023-pii-leakage`, `paper:aligned-production-extraction-2025`). | Report the threat model, budget, residual attack results, and unsupported surfaces; never emit a binary "private" score. |
| Human judgment vs observed outcomes | Specialized human or learned evaluators capture role dimensions missed by generic metrics (`paper:charactereval-2024`, `paper:persona-dialogue-madotto-2019`). | Evaluator agreement varies by social dimension, fictional labels are not owner truth, and plausible agents can miss real actions (`paper:sotopia-2024`, `paper:incharacter-2024`, `paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026`). | Keep owner/human judgment as one independent axis and report its reliability. |

## Architecture consequences

1. **Separate evidence, derived state, generation, and evaluation.** Source events and owner confirmations should remain distinct from inferred traits, narrative interpretations, relationship overlays, and generated text so evaluation cannot reward the model for replaying its own prior outputs (`paper:cognitive-memory-hupbach-et-al-2007`, `paper:longmemeval-2025`, `paper:personalization-legitimizes-risks-2026`).

2. **Version every evaluated dependency.** Store evidence cutoff, valid-time rules, retrieval snapshot, prompt, model, policy, decoding settings, and evaluator version because future leakage and model updates can change the same nominal task (`paper:timechara-2024`, `paper:synthetic-replacements-2024`).

3. **Represent owner-global, relationship-scoped, and request-local state separately.** Relational self-knowledge, audience design, partner pacts, and momentary state variation make one global persona vector an unsafe evaluation target (`paper:andersen-chen-2002-relational-self`, `paper:bell-1984-audience-design`, `paper:brennan-clark-1996-conceptual-pacts`, `paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001`).

4. **Use provenance-bearing autobiographical records.** Store event claims, source class, competing sources, narrative versions, current meaning, and validity separately because source confusion and reinterpretation are expected rather than exceptional (`paper:cognitive-memory-hashtroudi-johnson-chrosniak-1989`, `paper:jardmo-et-al-2023-repeated-narratives`, `paper:libby-eibach-2002-looking-back`).

5. **Run a fixed baseline ladder.** Compare generic, style-only, profile-only, retrieval-only, and full systems, plus ablations for temporal, relationship, and owner-confirmed evidence. Personalized gains without those contrasts can reflect base-model priors, profile cues, or memorized text (`paper:turing-experiments-2023`, `paper:emulate-personality-2025`, `paper:quantifying-memorization-2023`).

6. **Make the report a score vector with denominators.** Every axis should include eligible-case count, abstentions, point estimate, uncertainty interval, owner retest where available, baseline deltas, subgroup or context slices, and representative failures; aggregate success otherwise hides variance and coverage (`paper:synthetic-replacements-2024`, `paper:generative-agents-1000-2024`, `paper:twin-2k-500-2025`).

7. **Keep safety gates outside the weighted score.** Authorization, cross-person leakage, time leakage, sensitive inference, extraction, and deletion verification should return explicit pass, fail, or not-tested states rather than improve or decline with conversational quality (`paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide`, `paper:lukas-2023-pii-leakage`, `paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning`).

8. **Do not use model output as future ground truth.** Generated responses, inferred motives, summaries, and evaluator explanations may be useful proposals, but writing them back as owner evidence creates a self-confirming benchmark and increases contamination risk (`paper:cognitive-memory-hupbach-et-al-2007`, `paper:personalization-legitimizes-risks-2026`).

9. **Treat owner adjudication as versioned evidence.** Record the question, answer options, prompt order, time, confidence, and correction scope because elicitation can reveal, construct, or temporarily shift the measured preference or self-concept (`paper:chen-risen-2010-free-choice`, `paper:dargembeau-garcia-jimenez-2024-working-self`).

## Evaluation tests

### Common protocol

1. Freeze the corpus cutoff and answer key before comparing configurations. Keep a hidden mapping from randomized A/B labels to generic, style-only, profile-only, retrieval-only, and full systems (`paper:synthetic-replacements-2024`, `paper:persona-dialogue-madotto-2019`).
2. Partition cases by future time, whole episode or conversation, relationship, task domain, and outcome. Deduplicate exact and near-duplicate language before assigning splits (`paper:kandpal-2022-dedup-privacy`, `paper:quantifying-memorization-2023`, `paper:timechara-2024`).
3. Label every case with answerability, evidence class, relationship or audience, validity interval, sensitivity, third-party involvement, and whether an owner retest exists (`paper:linguistic-calibration-2022`, `paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide`, `paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy`).
4. Evaluate multiple random seeds or sampling runs and preserve the model, prompt, retrieval, and evaluator version. Apparent traits and survey results can drift across runs or model updates (`paper:personality-temporal-stability-2024`, `paper:synthetic-replacements-2024`).
5. Report style separately and cap its interpretation at channel-conditioned communication resemblance. No style score can rescue a failed content, behavior, boundary, or leakage test (`paper:persona-dialogue-zhang-2018`, `paper:persona-dialogue-madotto-2019`, `paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026`).

| **Calibration fidelity** | Require probabilities or confidence bands for answerable and deliberately unanswerable cases in each evidence class; repeat under paraphrase and reduced retrieval. | Reliability curves, proper probability score, calibration error, selective risk by coverage, abstention precision/recall, and overconfidence on unsupported claims. | Fluent hedging is not calibration; confidence must predict correctness and unsupported cases must permit abstention. | `paper:linguistic-calibration-2022`, `paper:cognitive-memory-koriat-bjork-2005`, `paper:longmemeval-2025` |
| **Privacy fidelity** | Reuse the same fact across changes in subject, sender, recipient, relationship, purpose, sensitivity, and consent; include multi-party summaries and ambiguous permission. | Unauthorized retrieval rate, unauthorized disclosure rate, third-party boundary violations, sensitive-inference attempts, permission-conflict abstention, and deletion-scope compliance. | A critical unauthorized disclosure is non-compensable; prompt reminders without enforced authorization do not count as a control. | `paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide`, `paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy`, `paper:longpre-2024-consent-crisis`, `paper:kosinski-2013-private-traits` |
| **Leakage fidelity** | Run black-box membership probes, canary and rare-sequence extraction, long-context continuation, partial-PII reconstruction, candidate inference, duplicate amplification, and ordinary-versus-targeted alignment bypass. | Attack success by budget, unique and duplicated exact-match rate, PII exposure, membership advantage, reconstruction accuracy, and residual risk after each defense. | Any pass claim is bounded to the tested threat model; ordinary refusals, scrubbing, output truncation, deduplication, or low average leakage cannot waive a severe leak. | `paper:shokri-2017-membership-inference`, `paper:carlini-2021-training-data-extraction`, `paper:kandpal-2022-dedup-privacy`, `paper:lukas-2023-pii-leakage`, `paper:aligned-production-extraction-2025` |
| **Owner-judgment fidelity** | Use blinded, randomized comparisons with a style-only decoy and a content-correct style-swapped control; ask again after a washout and include "both wrong" and "insufficient evidence." | Characteristic-response choice, likely-action choice, factual grounding, temporal correctness, relationship appropriateness, value/decision fit, uncertainty fit, privacy comfort, and owner-judge retest. | Owner preference remains separate from observed behavioral accuracy and privacy gates; recognizability alone cannot pass the benchmark. | `paper:persona-dialogue-zhang-2018`, `paper:charactereval-2024`, `paper:incharacter-2024`, `paper:sotopia-2024` |
| **Surface style diagnostic** | Compare burst shape, length, punctuation, code-switching, repair, and register on held-out owner messages by relationship and time, then run style-preserving content swaps. | Distributional distance, recognizability, and stability by relationship and period. | This axis is reported but never counted as evidence for memory, values, action, identity truth, consent, or safety. | `paper:persona-dialogue-li-2016`, `paper:persona-dialogue-madotto-2019`, `paper:persona-dialogue-arimoto-2024` |

### Required slices and reporting

- Report owner-confirmed versus inferred cases, high- versus low-evidence cases, stable versus changing periods, familiar versus held-out relationships, and answerable versus unanswerable cases; pooled means can conceal exactly the distributional and contextual errors the benchmark is intended to expose (`paper:synthetic-replacements-2024`, `paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001`, `paper:andersen-chen-2002-relational-self`).
- Report the generic, style-only, profile-only, retrieval-only, and full results side by side. The full system must demonstrate incremental value beyond style and base-model priors on the claimed axis (`paper:turing-experiments-2023`, `paper:emulate-personality-2025`, `paper:persona-dialogue-madotto-2019`).
- Preserve failures as inspectable cases with evidence references and no raw private text in aggregate summaries; rare severe leaks and relationship violations should not disappear inside average scores (`paper:carlini-2021-training-data-extraction`, `paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide`).
- Re-run the frozen suite after model, prompt, retriever, index, profile, consent, or policy changes. Model updates and memory construction choices can materially change performance and safety (`paper:synthetic-replacements-2024`, `paper:secom-2025`, `paper:personalization-legitimizes-risks-2026`).

## WhatsApp observability gaps

No private WhatsApp data was accessed for this synthesis. Even with explicit authorization, a message archive is a partial communication trace, not a complete behavioral or psychological ground truth (`paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001`, `paper:andersen-chen-2002-relational-self`, `paper:generative-agents-1000-2024`).

| Observable or partially observable | Missing or ambiguous construct | Benchmark response |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Sent text and a later sent reply | Unsent alternatives, drafts, ignored options, offline action, and whether advice was followed | Use prospective owner tasks and observed outcomes outside the training archive; label unavailable outcomes unknown (`paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026`, `paper:hofmann-2012-everyday-temptations`). |
| Message timestamp and order | Event time, validity interval, cause of silence, and when a belief privately changed | Ask for validity windows and corrections; test past and current cutoffs; never equate recency with truth (`paper:timechara-2024`, `paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics`). |
| Direct chat or group membership | Intended, imagined, permitted, forwarded, and actually exposed audiences | Store these audiences separately and abstain when permission is ambiguous (`paper:bell-1984-audience-design`, `paper:litt-hargittai-2016-imagined-audience`, `paper:bernstein-et-al-2013-invisible-audience`). |
| Repeated phrasing with one contact | Conceptual pact, quotation, irony, coercion, topic repetition, or automatic accommodation | Treat partner-specific language as tentative and test meaning, discourse act, and owner confirmation (`paper:brennan-clark-1996-conceptual-pacts`, `paper:doyle-frank-2016-sources-linguistic-alignment`). |
| First-person claim | Durable fact, joke, aspiration, roleplay, quotation, temporary state, or audience-specific presentation | Preserve claim type, source, relationship, time, and uncertainty; do not promote by frequency alone (`paper:persona-dialogue-mazare-2018`, `paper:tice-et-al-1995-when-modesty-prevails`). |
| Repeated story | Historical accuracy, self-defining importance, stable meaning, or strategic retelling | Separate event, source, recurrence, current meaning, and narrative version (`paper:kober-habermas-2017-personal-past-stability`, `paper:jardmo-et-al-2023-repeated-narratives`). |
| Choice or recommendation stated in chat | Full option set, original frame, anchor, constraints, conflict, enactment, and regret | Preserve observed frame and options when available; use owner questions before inferring a stable preference (`paper:tversky-kahneman-1981-framing`, `paper:ariely-2003-coherent-arbitrariness`, `paper:chen-risen-2010-free-choice`). |
| Emotion words, emoji, hedges, or certainty language | Private affect, mixed feelings, appraisal, masking, and empirical confidence calibration | Treat text labels as reader interpretations and validate against owner report or later correctness (`paper:demszky-2020-goemotions`, `paper:trampe-2015-everyday-emotions`, `paper:linguistic-calibration-2022`). |
| Message frequency and app-use metadata | Opportunity, availability, relationship quality, trait, and denominator of possible actions | Use context-bounded distributions and abstain from unsupported trait inference (`paper:psychometrics_montag_2015`, `paper:psychometrics_stachl_2020`). |
| Sender labels, quotes, and forwarding markers | Witnessed versus inferred source, shared-device authorship, trust, and same-class source confusion | Retain competing source hypotheses and test wrong-person attribution (`paper:cognitive-memory-hashtroudi-johnson-chrosniak-1989`, `paper:cognitive-memory-hupbach-et-al-2007`). |
| Archive possession | Correspondent consent for training, long-term retention, retrieval, evaluation, or simulation | Require subject- and purpose-specific authorization; silence and public accessibility are not permission (`paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy`, `paper:longpre-2024-consent-crisis`). |
| Retained archive contents | Deleted, disappearing, edited, missing-media, call, other-app, and offline context | Report source coverage and missingness; do not interpret nonappearance as absence or forgetting (`paper:cognitive-memory-murre-dros-2015`, `paper:locomo-2024`). |

## Highest-value owner questions

1. **Which capability are you authorizing and which are you not authorizing: private drafting, memory lookup, behavioral prediction, relationship-conditioned drafting, voice or likeness, or autonomous action?** Capability-specific consent is necessary because access for one purpose does not establish permission for another (`paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy`, `paper:longpre-2024-consent-crisis`).

2. **Which future replies, choices, or actions would be meaningful enough to test prospectively, and which are too private or consequential to turn into benchmark targets?** Real outcome prediction is more informative than plausible retrospective language, but collecting it can itself increase privacy and surveillance burden (`paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026`, `paper:generative-agents-1000-2024`).

3. **When you give a different answer twice, should the system represent noise, genuine change, context dependence, ambivalence, or an unresolved contradiction?** Human retest reliability is imperfect, and state distributions can remain broad even when their averages are stable (`paper:generative-agents-1000-2024`, `paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001`).

4. **Which beliefs, preferences, commitments, and relationship roles are current, which are historical, and which have explicit start or end dates?** Point-in-time correctness and simple recency are not interchangeable (`paper:timechara-2024`, `paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics`).

5. **Which relationships require a distinct voice or boundary, and which facts, nicknames, stories, or topics must never cross between them?** Relationship cues and partner history change behavior, while past disclosure does not authorize a new audience (`paper:andersen-chen-2002-relational-self`, `paper:brennan-clark-1996-conceptual-pacts`, `paper:bazarova-2012-public-intimacy`).

6. **For an important memory, what happened, how do you know, who else is involved, what did it mean then, and what does it mean now?** Event facts, source, affect, and current interpretation are distinct and can change at different rates (`paper:blagov-singer-2004-four-dimensions`, `paper:jardmo-et-al-2023-repeated-narratives`).

7. **For an important decision, what options, constraints, frame, anchor, competing goal, and expected outcome were present?** A bare choice is not a context-free value readout, and apparent preference change can be a measurement artifact (`paper:tversky-kahneman-1981-framing`, `paper:tversky-simonson-1993-context-preferences`, `paper:chen-risen-2010-free-choice`).

8. **When should the system say "I do not know," ask you, retrieve more evidence, or offer a low-confidence draft?** Confidence language needs empirical calibration, and cue-rich fluency can inflate confidence without improving later access (`paper:linguistic-calibration-2022`, `paper:cognitive-memory-koriat-bjork-2005`).

9. **Which parts of a response make it feel like you: likely action, factual memory, relationship judgment, values, wording, rhythm, or something else?** Owner ratings should decompose these constructs so style cannot mask wrong behavior or memory (`paper:persona-dialogue-zhang-2018`, `paper:charactereval-2024`, `paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026`).

10. **Who, if anyone, may provide an additional judgment, and in which relationship-scoped domain?** A close contact observes a different criterion from owner self-report and may not know the owner's private state (`paper:psychometrics_mairesse_2007`, `paper:psychometrics_youyou_2015`).

11. **Which third-party information may be retained, tested, quoted, summarized, or reused, and what should happen when permissions conflict?** Interdependent data and contextual disclosure require subject, recipient, and purpose controls rather than one archive-level checkbox (`paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy`, `paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide`).

12. **What failure should immediately disable a capability: false memory, wrong-person attribution, future leakage, private-text reproduction, sensitive inference, cross-relationship disclosure, or unapproved action?** These failures have different mechanisms and cannot be safely averaged into conversational quality (`paper:timechara-2024`, `paper:carlini-2021-training-data-extraction`, `paper:lukas-2023-pii-leakage`, `paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide`).
