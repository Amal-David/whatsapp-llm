# Construct Validity and Measurement

## Evidence scope

**Corpus inventory, not a literature claim.** This draft uses the deduplicated merged authority in corpus.jsonl: 187 unique, cross-reviewed primary-paper records, of which 100 were inspected at full-text depth and 87 at abstract depth. Seat-note counts are not summed because the merged corpus removes cross-seat aliases.

The program must distinguish six measurement questions. A positive result on one question does not answer the others. (paper:psychometrics_park_2015; paper:emulate-personality-2025; paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001; paper:psychometrics_rieman_2017; paper:psychometrics_schwartz_2013)

| Measurement question | Operational meaning | What success does not establish |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Behavioral prediction** | Predict a later, held-out response or action under a declared task, time, and scoring rule. | It does not establish a latent trait, motive, or general fidelity outside that criterion and domain. (paper:generative-agents-1000-2024; paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026) |
| **Psychometric reliability** | Reproduce an estimate across sampled episodes, text partitions, retests, seeds, or model versions. | Repeatability does not establish that the intended construct was measured or that the response process is human-like. (paper:psychometrics_epstein_1983; paper:psychometrics_borkenau_2004; paper:emulate-personality-2025; paper:personality-temporal-stability-2024) |
| **Construct validity** | Show convergent, discriminant, factorial, criterion, and incremental evidence for a named target such as owner self-concept, observer reputation, or future behavior. | Agreement with one questionnaire, informant, or evaluator does not reveal a context-free true self. (paper:psychometrics_park_2015; paper:psychometrics_mairesse_2007; paper:psychometrics_youyou_2015) |
| **State-trait distinction** | Separate occasion-specific expression and situation response from a slower distribution of recurring tendencies. | One message, one choice, or one emotional expression is not a trait observation. (paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001; paper:psychometrics_sherman_2015; paper:kuppens-2010-dynaffect) |
| **Context stability** | Test whether a measurement retains meaning and calibration across relationships, audiences, languages, platforms, time periods, and elicitation modes. | Performance in one relationship or platform cannot be transported by assumption. (paper:psychometrics_rieman_2017; paper:andersen-chen-2002-relational-self; paper:tice-et-al-1995-when-modesty-prevails) |
| **Confounding** | Test whether topic, partner, demographics, opportunity, norms, administration mode, prompt, model prior, or data selection explains the signal. | A predictive proxy is not automatically the construct or its cause. (paper:psychometrics_schwartz_2013; paper:psychometrics_montag_2015; paper:psychometrics_laajaj_2019; paper:synthetic-replacements-2024) |

The strongest human state-trait and several value, narrative, and relationship constraints are abstract-depth records; they support directional constraints but not universal thresholds, fixed effect sizes, or WhatsApp-specific parameter values. Direct LLM, simulator, distributional, longitudinal-memory, and many language-model validity findings are mostly full-text inspections, but their datasets remain platform-, population-, task-, and model-version-bound. (paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001; paper:bardi-schwartz-2003-values-behavior; paper:reese-et-al-2011-narrative-coherence; paper:generative-agents-1000-2024; paper:synthetic-replacements-2024)

## VALIDATED constraints

### Behavioral prediction is criterion- and domain-bound

Language and behavioral traces can predict some questionnaire criteria in held-out samples: Facebook language showed convergent, discriminant, external, and six-month stability evidence, and 30-day smartphone logs predicted some Big Five domains and facets. Predictability was uneven, including a failed agreeableness result, and the criteria were self-report measures rather than direct observations of an inner trait. (paper:psychometrics_park_2015; paper:psychometrics_stachl_2020)

Rich self-reports can improve prediction of later survey answers relative to demographic baselines, especially when interview and survey evidence are combined. The same study did not show clear agent-type gains in economic games, and its aggregate experimental test was underpowered; Twin-2K-500 likewise combined encouraging held-out answer accuracy with failures on several experimental effects and normative-answer substitution. (paper:generative-agents-1000-2024; paper:twin-2k-500-2025)

Natural action prediction is materially harder than plausible response generation. Prompted agents reached at most 11.86% exact next-action accuracy in real shopping sessions, sequential persona simulation accumulated early errors, and persona recognition remained much stronger than free generation in a multi-context benchmark. (paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026; paper:behaviorchain-2025; paper:twinvoice-2026)

**Constraint:** every predictive result must name its target, domain, horizon, base rate, observation window, and baseline. Survey agreement, next-message accuracy, style recognizability, and next-action prediction remain separate capabilities. (paper:generative-agents-1000-2024; paper:llm-user-sim-crs-2024; paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026)

### Reliability is necessary and insufficient

Single acts are usually too narrow for broad disposition estimates; appropriate aggregation over occasions and episodes can improve reliability and informant agreement, while poorly chosen aggregation can erase meaningful contingencies. Gains in one thin-slice study largely saturated after several structured episodes, but that count cannot be transferred to text, relationships, or cultures. (paper:psychometrics_epstein_1983; paper:psychometrics_borkenau_2004)

Long text histories can yield reliable lexical estimates, and one Facebook language model showed six-month stability, but those results establish stability only for sampled language in those platforms and windows. They do not establish lifelong immutability or cross-relationship reliability. (paper:psychometrics_yarkoni_2010; paper:psychometrics_park_2015)

High repeatability can be a warning rather than a success. GPT-4 personality emulations produced unusually pure factor loadings and weakened human-like intertrait dependencies, while repeated LLM questionnaires showed model-, trait-, instrument-, and date-dependent stability with socially desirable profiles. (paper:emulate-personality-2025; paper:personality-temporal-stability-2024)

**Constraint:** report reliability separately from validity, and estimate it by construct, context, horizon, instrument, model version, and evidence source. A stable wrong construct remains wrong. (paper:psychometrics_park_2015; paper:emulate-personality-2025; paper:personality-temporal-stability-2024)

### Construct validity requires an explicit criterion

Observable cues can align more with reputation than self-concept. In naturalistic speech, observer-target models outperformed self-report-target models, while the self-report models did not beat baseline; brief behavior also gave judges nonoverlapping information. (paper:psychometrics_mairesse_2007; paper:psychometrics_mehl_2006; paper:psychometrics_borkenau_2004)

Trace-based, self-report, and informant measurements are complementary rather than interchangeable. Like-based estimates sometimes agreed more with self-ratings than one friend's brief rating, but the model and friend had unequal information and instruments, and each channel retained distinct information. (paper:psychometrics_youyou_2015)

Construct validity also requires a human-relative response structure. Strong alignment to prompted trait totals can coexist with implausibly high loadings, weakened cross-trait dependencies, static fictional labels, or evaluator-model error. (paper:emulate-personality-2025; paper:incharacter-2024; paper:charactereval-2024)

Measurement invariance is an empirical gate. A broad factor configuration can coexist with failed metric and scalar invariance, acquiescence correction can help without fully solving cross-country comparability, and reasonable invariance in another instrument required flexible cross-loadings and partial exceptions. (paper:psychometrics_nye_2008; paper:psychometrics_rammstedt_2013; paper:psychometrics_mezquita_2019)

**Constraint:** the system must label an estimate as owner self-description, owner-confirmed claim, informant/reputation estimate, channel-conditioned behavioral prediction, or model hypothesis. None may silently serve as ground truth for the others. (paper:psychometrics_mairesse_2007; paper:psychometrics_youyou_2015; paper:psychometrics_park_2015)

### States and traits must remain separate

People express broad within-person ranges of Big Five-relevant states even when their central tendencies and distributional features are relatively stable, and contemporaneous situations independently predict behavior and emotion alongside person effects. (paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001; paper:psychometrics_sherman_2015)

Affect is likewise dynamic: a home base, variability, and return strength describe different aspects of core affect, and everyday reports frequently contain mixed positive and negative emotions. A single latest emotion label is not an adequate affective state, much less a personality trait. (paper:kuppens-2010-dynaffect; paper:trampe-2015-everyday-emotions)

Elicitation can change the measured state. Reflecting on past or future self-defining events shifted immediate self-description and perceived temporal continuity, and current self-compatibility changed autobiographical visual perspective. (paper:dargembeau-garcia-jimenez-2024-working-self; paper:libby-eibach-2002-looking-back)

**Constraint:** maintain occasion state, recurring situation-response contingencies, medium-term phase, and slow trait posterior as separate time-indexed objects. Promotion from one layer to another requires repeated evidence or explicit owner confirmation. (paper:psychometrics_epstein_1983; paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001; paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics)

### Context stability must be tested, not presumed

Significant-other cues can activate relationship-specific self-knowledge, people present themselves differently to friends and strangers, and imagined audiences can change from message to message even when technical permissions do not. (paper:andersen-chen-2002-relational-self; paper:tice-et-al-1995-when-modesty-prevails; paper:litt-hargittai-2016-imagined-audience)

Language alignment is not a context-free trait. Turn-level accommodation varies by linguistic dimension, word, discourse act, dyadic baseline, and topic, and symmetric matching cannot be converted into directional influence or relationship quality. (paper:danescu-niculescu-mizil-et-al-2011-mark-my-words; paper:doyle-frank-2016-sources-linguistic-alignment; paper:ireland-et-al-2011-language-style-matching)

Cross-platform transfer is a new measurement problem. Naive Facebook-to-Twitter and user-to-county reuse produced implausible outliers and spurious associations; adaptation improved plausibility and stability but did not supply direct labels for most target units. (paper:psychometrics_rieman_2017)

**Constraint:** key every estimate by relationship, role, audience, channel, language, platform, time window, and elicitation mode. Unsupported transfer returns unknown rather than a recycled score. (paper:psychometrics_rieman_2017; paper:psychometrics_nye_2008; paper:tice-et-al-1995-when-modesty-prevails)

### Confounds must remain visible

Social-language features jointly encode personality criteria, age, gender, topic, education, vocabulary, and platform conventions; WhatsApp-use duration showed stronger age and gender patterns than most trait associations. (paper:psychometrics_schwartz_2013; paper:psychometrics_montag_2015)

Administration and sampling can change the apparent construct. Across non-WEIRD samples, acquiescence, education, enumerator interaction, translation, mode, and self-selection jointly affected factor recovery, so country or culture is not a sufficient correction variable. (paper:psychometrics_laajaj_2019)

Prompts and base-model priors are confounds in simulation. Synthetic survey means concealed variance and covariance failures and changed after a model update; risky-choice simulators were too expected-value-rational, especially with chain-of-thought; famous experiments can be reproduced while obscure factual knowledge reveals a nonhuman response process. (paper:synthetic-replacements-2024; paper:llms-more-rational-2025; paper:turing-experiments-2023)

**Constraint:** every measurement record must retain tested confounds and unresolved alternatives. Prediction after adjusting for a covariate is not a causal explanation, and model-generated rationales are not evidence of the owner's cognition. (paper:psychometrics_schwartz_2013; paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026; paper:baker-2017-bayesian-mentalizing)

### What messaging traces can and cannot establish

With consent and sufficient repeated sampling, messaging can establish what was sent, when, through which channel, to an observed audience, plus recurring lexical, interactional, disclosure, and relationship-conditioned patterns in that observed channel. Those patterns can support bounded prediction or retrieval tests when the criterion is independently held out. (paper:psychometrics_pennebaker_king_1999; paper:psychometrics_borkenau_2004; paper:doyle-frank-2016-sources-linguistic-alignment; paper:persona-dialogue-salemi-2024-lamp)

Messaging alone cannot establish a context-free trait, private motive, stable value hierarchy, experienced emotion, factual autobiographical truth, offline action policy, behavior in unseen relationships, or complete digital self. The same trace can reflect situation, norms, audience, opportunity, framing, quotation, current goals, or partner elicitation. (paper:psychometrics_sherman_2015; paper:bardi-schwartz-2003-values-behavior; paper:demszky-2020-goemotions; paper:kober-habermas-2017-personal-past-stability; paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026)

High predictability of sensitive labels from traces does not make those labels individually true, ethically legitimate, or appropriate product targets. Protected- and sensitive-trait inference must remain suppressed even when a benchmark is predictive. (paper:kosinski-2013-private-traits; paper:gong-2016-attribute-inference; paper:buolamwini-2018-gender-shades; paper:scheuerman-2019-computers-see-gender)

## PLAUSIBLE design hypotheses

These are evidence-informed designs assembled from component findings, not end-to-end validated digital-self measurements. (paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001; paper:generative-agents-1000-2024; paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026)

1. **Hierarchical measurement state.** Store source observations first, estimate context-conditioned states second, aggregate recurring contingencies third, and update a slow trait or preference posterior last. Preserve the full state distribution and exceptions rather than only its mean. (paper:psychometrics_epstein_1983; paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001; paper:psychometrics_sherman_2015)
2. **Parallel criterion views.** Maintain owner self-description, owner-confirmed facts, observer reputation, trace-based prediction, and generated hypotheses as separate views that may disagree. (paper:psychometrics_mairesse_2007; paper:psychometrics_youyou_2015; paper:haslam-et-al-2011-self-knowledge-identity)
3. **Typed promotion gates.** A recent behavior may affect request-local state without becoming a durable identity claim; promotion should require repeated cross-occasion evidence, an owner declaration, or prospective validity that survives confound and context tests. (paper:psychometrics_epstein_1983; paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics; paper:psychometrics_park_2015)
4. **Active owner measurement.** Ask only questions likely to change a consequential decision, resolve a contradiction, or prevent a durable misupdate; score expected information value together with answerability, burden, disclosure sensitivity, and a skip option. (paper:boutilier-2003-active-cf; paper:harpale-2008-personalized-active-learning; paper:rashid-2008-information-theoretic-elicitation)
5. **Context-specific calibration.** Estimate reliability and validity separately by language, relationship, audience, platform, time period, construct, and criterion; share parameters only after transport tests pass. (paper:psychometrics_rieman_2017; paper:psychometrics_nye_2008; paper:psychometrics_mezquita_2019)
6. **Confound-aware abstention.** Return several plausible explanations or unknown when partner, topic, role, framing, or base-model knowledge could account for a signal and no independent criterion adjudicates them. (paper:doyle-frank-2016-sources-linguistic-alignment; paper:tversky-kahneman-1981-framing; paper:turing-experiments-2023)
7. **Versioned narrative and preference state.** Preserve one event identity while versioning its interpretation, and supersede a changed preference without erasing prior valid periods or treating repetition as historical truth. (paper:mcadams-et-al-2006-continuity-change; paper:jardmo-et-al-2023-repeated-narratives; paper:longmemeval-2025)
8. **Owner authority with measured effects.** Owner corrections should be authoritative, typed, reversible, and previewable, while the system separately measures whether a correction improves prediction; direct editing can reduce conventional metrics or be confounded with extra feedback. (paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles; paper:bostandjiev-2012-tasteweights; paper:bakalov-2013-controllable-user-models)

## SPECULATIVE claims

- A WhatsApp archive alone can recover the owner's true personality, values, motives, affect dynamics, or narrative identity. Existing evidence is criterion-, context-, and elicitation-bound and does not validate that inference. (paper:psychometrics_mairesse_2007; paper:bardi-schwartz-2003-values-behavior; paper:demszky-2020-goemotions; paper:reese-et-al-2011-narrative-coherence)
- High next-message, survey-answer, or persona-consistency accuracy establishes a faithful digital self. Survey gains fail to transfer uniformly, persona access can improve consistency without engagement, and real sequential action remains difficult. (paper:generative-agents-1000-2024; paper:persona-dialogue-zhang-2018; paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026)
- A trait model trained in one platform, language, relationship, or administration mode retains the same scale and meaning elsewhere. Direct transfer failures and invariance failures reject that presumption. (paper:psychometrics_rieman_2017; paper:psychometrics_nye_2008; paper:psychometrics_laajaj_2019)
- Reliable LLM questionnaire output reveals a human-like latent personality. High internal consistency can coexist with implausibly pure factors, socially desirable responding, and weak temporal stability. (paper:emulate-personality-2025; paper:personality-temporal-stability-2024)
- Emotional wording, emoji, or a reader's label establishes the writer's internal emotion. Context can override expressive cues, fine-grained text classification remains error-prone, and observer inference does not verify hidden state. (paper:carroll-russell-1996-face-context; paper:demszky-2020-goemotions; paper:wu-2018-emotion-mental-states)
- An isolated choice reveals a stable utility, value, or decision policy. Frames, option sets, anchors, norms, and measurement order can change or manufacture the apparent preference. (paper:tversky-kahneman-1981-framing; paper:tversky-simonson-1993-context-preferences; paper:ariely-2003-coherent-arbitrariness; paper:chen-risen-2010-free-choice)
- Repeated narration or polished coherence establishes factual memory, maturity, well-being, or identity completeness. Repetition measures recurrence, coherence dimensions follow different trajectories, and coherence did not increase in one longitudinal psychotherapy study. (paper:kober-habermas-2017-personal-past-stability; paper:reese-et-al-2011-narrative-coherence; paper:adler-2012-living-into-story)
- A generated rationale, reflection, or inferred commonsense edge is evidence about what the owner thought. Base-model knowledge and category commonsense can override or fabricate personal evidence. (paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026; paper:persona-dialogue-gao-2023-peacok; paper:turing-experiments-2023)

## Contradictions/boundaries

| Evidence tension | Boundary for synthesis |
| --- | --- |
| Language scores showed multimethod and six-month validity in one Facebook ecosystem, while self-report speech models failed in a small conversation corpus and cross-platform reuse produced implausible outputs. (paper:psychometrics_park_2015; paper:psychometrics_mairesse_2007; paper:psychometrics_rieman_2017) | Language can be a valid source-specific measure; it is not a universal personality instrument. |
| Computer Like-based judgments exceeded one friend's brief ratings on several criteria, but channels had unequal evidence and retained distinct information. (paper:psychometrics_youyou_2015) | Compare channels under equivalent criteria and information before claiming superior psychological access. |
| Interview-, survey-, and combined agents achieved strong normalized survey accuracy, while economic-game differences were unclear, experimental effects failed in another twin set, and real shopping prediction remained low. (paper:generative-agents-1000-2024; paper:twin-2k-500-2025; paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026) | Holdout performance is domain-specific; do not average it into general person fidelity. |
| Synthetic means and classic effects sometimes matched humans, while variance, covariance, subgroup patterns, temporal reproducibility, and obscure-knowledge behavior were nonhuman. (paper:out-of-one-many-2023; paper:synthetic-replacements-2024; paper:turing-experiments-2023) | Evaluate distributions and response processes, not means alone. |
| LLM trait emulations had high convergence and internal consistency, yet showed implausibly pure factors; repeated LLM profiles also drifted by model and instrument. (paper:emulate-personality-2025; paper:personality-temporal-stability-2024) | Reliability and target-score agreement are not construct validity. |
| Average state distributions can be stable while each person expresses wide momentary ranges, and situations independently predict expression. (paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001; paper:psychometrics_sherman_2015) | Preserve within-person variability and situation effects rather than forcing a fixed persona. |
| Relationship cues, audience, and discourse act systematically alter self-presentation and language, while aggregate matching can correlate with relationship outcomes. (paper:andersen-chen-2002-relational-self; paper:tice-et-al-1995-when-modesty-prevails; paper:doyle-frank-2016-sources-linguistic-alignment; paper:ireland-et-al-2011-language-style-matching) | Matching is not a trait, motive, power, closeness, or relationship-health detector. |
| Narrative dimensions correlate with some adjustment measures, yet coherence is multidimensional, developmentally confounded, elicitation-reactive, and not uniformly associated with improvement. (paper:blagov-singer-2004-four-dimensions; paper:reese-et-al-2011-narrative-coherence; paper:dargembeau-garcia-jimenez-2024-working-self; paper:adler-2012-living-into-story) | Narrative coding can annotate an account; it cannot diagnose the owner or certify adaptation. |
| Value structure is coherent at the population level, but value-behavior links vary and norms can obscure them; preferences also shift with frame, option set, and anchor. (paper:schwartz-2012-refined-values; paper:bardi-schwartz-2003-values-behavior; paper:tversky-kahneman-1981-framing; paper:ariely-2003-coherent-arbitrariness) | Behavior is evidence about values and preferences, not a direct sensor for them. |
| Users valued profile visibility and control, but editing reduced objective metrics in one study and positive results in others were small or confounded with added feedback. (paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles; paper:bostandjiev-2012-tasteweights; paper:bakalov-2013-controllable-user-models) | Owner authority is a governance requirement; correction quality remains an empirical outcome. |
| Several psychometrics papers share source corpora or the myPersonality ecosystem. (paper:psychometrics_pennebaker_king_1999; paper:psychometrics_mehl_2006; paper:psychometrics_mairesse_2007; paper:psychometrics_schwartz_2013; paper:psychometrics_park_2015; paper:psychometrics_youyou_2015) | Paper count must not be interpreted as independent replication count. |

## Architecture consequences

1. **Canonical evidence ledger.** Store each authorized message or event with speaker, source, timestamp, channel, relationship, audience evidence, modality, and provenance; keep inferred state outside the canonical event. Source confusion and later integration can affect content while leaving origin uncertain. (paper:cognitive-memory-hashtroudi-johnson-chrosniak-1989; paper:cognitive-memory-hupbach-et-al-2007)
2. **Measurement registry.** Every estimate needs construct, criterion, instrument or model, calibration population, observation window, evidence count, source modality, reliability, validity evidence, invariance status, model version, and uncertainty. (paper:psychometrics_park_2015; paper:psychometrics_mezquita_2019; paper:personality-temporal-stability-2024)
3. **Parallel evidence views.** Keep owner declarations, owner corrections, observer reputation, behavioral predictions, narrative interpretations, and model hypotheses separate, with explicit disagreements. (paper:psychometrics_mairesse_2007; paper:psychometrics_youyou_2015; paper:haslam-et-al-2011-self-knowledge-identity)
4. **Temporal state layers.** Represent request-local state, episode state, medium-term phase, recurring context contingency, and slow trait or preference posterior separately; attach valid-from, valid-to, expiry, and supersession. (paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001; paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics; paper:longmemeval-2025)
5. **Context keys.** Scope estimates to relationship, role, audience, language, platform, channel, topic, and elicitation mode; global reuse requires explicit transport and invariance evidence. (paper:andersen-chen-2002-relational-self; paper:litt-hargittai-2016-imagined-audience; paper:psychometrics_rieman_2017)
6. **Confound register.** Track tested and unresolved effects for demographics, education, opportunity, partner, topic, norms, framing, prompt order, model prior, and data-selection lineage. (paper:psychometrics_schwartz_2013; paper:psychometrics_laajaj_2019; paper:persona-dialogue-chen-2023-orig; paper:synthetic-replacements-2024)
7. **Promotion and abstention gate.** Do not promote one observation to a durable construct when reliability, criterion validity, context transfer, or confound checks are absent; preserve unknown and competing explanations. (paper:psychometrics_epstein_1983; paper:psychometrics_rieman_2017; paper:linguistic-calibration-2022)
8. **Owner-correction layer.** Support typed corrections such as wrong, no longer true, relationship-specific, temporary, do not infer, do not personalize, and delete; preview impact, version changes, and retain rollback while honoring owner authority. (paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles; paper:bostandjiev-2012-tasteweights; paper:bakalov-2013-controllable-user-models)
9. **Sensitive-inference prohibition.** Prevent protected or sensitive attributes from becoming durable inferred fields, and audit whether embeddings, summaries, graphs, or generated answers reconstruct suppressed attributes. (paper:kosinski-2013-private-traits; paper:gong-2016-attribute-inference; paper:jia-2018-attriguard)
10. **Generation/write-back boundary.** Generated answers, rationales, summaries, reflections, persona expansions, and predictions cannot become owner evidence without an external observation or explicit confirmation. (paper:generative-agents-2023; paper:persona-dialogue-gao-2023-peacok; paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026)
11. **Inspectable decision trace.** A response should identify the evidence, measurement view, active state, context key, confounds, uncertainty, and generation choice that influenced it; plausibility alone is not provenance. (paper:generative-agents-2023; paper:connected-memories-comet-2022; paper:turing-experiments-2023)

## Evaluation tests

| Test | Required procedure and interpretation | Evidence basis |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Chronological behavioral holdout** | Build only from earlier evidence; predict later real responses and actions with probabilities. Compare generic-LLM, demographic, recency-only, retrieval-only, and full-system baselines by domain. | paper:generative-agents-1000-2024; paper:twin-2k-500-2025; paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026 |
| **Aggregation curve** | Estimate performance and uncertainty as occasions, messages, and relationships accumulate; report saturation and leave-one-episode-out sensitivity rather than assuming a universal evidence count. | paper:psychometrics_epstein_1983; paper:psychometrics_borkenau_2004; paper:psychometrics_yarkoni_2010 |
| **Test-retest reliability** | Repeat owner instruments, open-ended probes, and model runs across dates, seeds, prompts, and deployment versions; report trait- and context-specific ICC or equivalent agreement. | paper:psychometrics_park_2015; paper:incharacter-2024; paper:personality-temporal-stability-2024 |
| **Convergent and discriminant validity** | Compare trace estimates separately with owner self-report, owner correction, informant judgment where consented, and later behavior; verify that nominally different constructs remain distinguishable. | paper:psychometrics_park_2015; paper:psychometrics_mairesse_2007; paper:emulate-personality-2025 |
| **Factor and measurement invariance** | Test factor structure, cross-loadings, metric and scalar invariance, and item or feature bias across language, relationship, platform, age period, and elicitation mode. | paper:psychometrics_nye_2008; paper:psychometrics_soto_2008; paper:psychometrics_laajaj_2019; paper:psychometrics_mezquita_2019 |
| **State-trait decomposition** | Use repeated sampling to estimate within-person state distributions, situation effects, medium-term phases, and slow central tendencies; score whether the model preserves variability and exceptions. | paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001; paper:psychometrics_sherman_2015; paper:kuppens-2010-dynaffect |
| **Cross-context transport** | Leave out complete relationships, languages, platforms, or roles; require recalibration and known-null checks before transfer, and report unsupported contexts as unknown. | paper:psychometrics_rieman_2017; paper:tice-et-al-1995-when-modesty-prevails; paper:doyle-frank-2016-sources-linguistic-alignment |
| **Temporal validity and leakage** | Test current, superseded, not-yet-known, and never-known claims at historical cutoffs; measure whether later evidence leaks into earlier simulated states. | paper:timechara-2024; paper:longmemeval-2025; paper:kober-habermas-2017-personal-past-stability |
| **Confound ablations** | Remove or balance age, gender, topic, partner, message volume, prompt order, role labels, and base-model knowledge; compare coefficients, calibration, and errors before and after. | paper:psychometrics_schwartz_2013; paper:psychometrics_montag_2015; paper:persona-dialogue-chen-2023-orig; paper:turing-experiments-2023 |
| **Distributional fidelity** | Compare means, variance, covariance, subgroup errors, repeated-choice noise, and excess rationality; a mean match with variance collapse is a failure. | paper:synthetic-replacements-2024; paper:turing-experiments-2023; paper:llms-more-rational-2025 |
| **Frame and option robustness** | Re-ask decisions under equivalent frames, altered option sets, and reordered elicitation; distinguish stable preference from measurement-created change. | paper:tversky-kahneman-1981-framing; paper:tversky-simonson-1993-context-preferences; paper:chen-risen-2010-free-choice |
| **Elicitation reactivity** | Measure before and after owner interviews or narrative prompts, and avoid treating prompt-produced self-description as pre-existing state. | paper:dargembeau-garcia-jimenez-2024-working-self; paper:habermas-de-silveira-2008-global-coherence; paper:adler-2012-living-into-story |
| **Calibration and abstention** | Produce reliability curves by claim type for facts, memories, preferences, traits, and future behavior; penalize confident unsupported claims and test cue-reduced retrieval. | paper:linguistic-calibration-2022; paper:cognitive-memory-koriat-bjork-2005; paper:cognitive-memory-metcalfe-finn-2008 |

## WhatsApp observability gaps

No private WhatsApp data was accessed. Even with explicit owner authorization, an export is a selected communication trace rather than a criterion-complete self-model. (paper:psychometrics_schwartz_2013; paper:litt-hargittai-2016-imagined-audience; paper:bardi-schwartz-2003-values-behavior)

## Highest-value owner questions

Questions should be selected by expected decision impact and answerability, not by curiosity or entropy alone. A skip or not-sure response updates the query policy rather than the owner's inferred trait. (paper:boutilier-2003-active-cf; paper:harpale-2008-personalized-active-learning; paper:rashid-2002-getting-to-know-you)

1. **What should this estimate mean: how you describe yourself, how this person likely sees you, or what you are likely to do?** This resolves criterion ambiguity before self-report, reputation, and prediction are collapsed. (paper:psychometrics_mairesse_2007; paper:psychometrics_youyou_2015)
2. **Was this expression a temporary state, a recurring response to this situation, a current phase, or something you consider broadly stable?** This supplies the missing state-trait label and time scale. (paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001; paper:psychometrics_sherman_2015; paper:kuppens-2010-dynaffect)
3. **In which relationships, roles, languages, or channels does this pattern hold, and where does it not?** Counterexamples are needed before cross-context promotion. (paper:andersen-chen-2002-relational-self; paper:tice-et-al-1995-when-modesty-prevails; paper:psychometrics_rieman_2017)
4. **When did this become true, and is it still true now?** The answer supplies valid-from, valid-to, and supersession rather than treating message time as truth time. (paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics; paper:longmemeval-2025; paper:jardmo-et-al-2023-repeated-narratives)
5. **Was the message literal, playful, quoted, aspirational, strategic, or written for a particular audience?** This distinguishes content from self-presentation and prevents first-person extraction from becoming an identity fact. (paper:persona-dialogue-mazare-2018; paper:tice-et-al-1995-when-modesty-prevails; paper:litt-hargittai-2016-imagined-audience)
6. **What options, frame, constraints, norms, and competing goals were active when you chose or said this?** This is required before a choice becomes a preference, value, or policy update. (paper:tversky-kahneman-1981-framing; paper:bardi-schwartz-2003-values-behavior; paper:hofmann-2012-everyday-temptations)
7. **How did you actually feel, and how did you intend the message to be read?** Perceived tone and experienced affect are distinct criteria. (paper:demszky-2020-goemotions; paper:carroll-russell-1996-face-context; paper:smith-ellsworth-1985-appraisal)
8. **What happened afterward offline, and would you make the same choice again under the same conditions?** This supplies a prospective behavioral criterion and repeated-choice evidence. (paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026; paper:llms-more-rational-2025)
9. **Which source claim is wrong, stale, relationship-specific, or forbidden for personalization, and should it be suppressed, superseded, or deleted?** This turns correction into a typed governance action rather than an opaque profile edit. (paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles; paper:bostandjiev-2012-tasteweights; paper:bakalov-2013-controllable-user-models)
10. **Which inferences should the system never make even if they appear statistically predictable?** This establishes protected and sensitive no-inference boundaries. (paper:kosinski-2013-private-traits; paper:gong-2016-attribute-inference; paper:buolamwini-2018-gender-shades)
11. **What evidence would make you trust this estimate, and when should the system simply say it does not know?** This supplies the owner's criterion, risk threshold, and abstention preference. (paper:linguistic-calibration-2022; paper:cognitive-memory-koriat-bjork-2005)
