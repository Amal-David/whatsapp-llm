# Values, Goals, Affect, and Decisions

## Evidence scope

This synthesis uses the deduplicated `corpus.jsonl`: 187 unique, cross-reviewed primary-paper records, of which 100 were inspected at full-text depth and 87 at abstract depth. The focal `values_decisions_emotion` slice contributes 20 records, but only `paper:demszky-2020-goemotions`, `paper:trampe-2015-everyday-emotions`, and `paper:wu-2018-emotion-mental-states` were inspected in full; the other 17 focal records support only claims visible in their primary abstracts. Classic findings on values, framing, appraisal, and mentalizing are therefore used as bounded constraints, not as permission to invent effect sizes, populations, mechanisms, or WhatsApp-specific validity (`paper:schwartz-2012-refined-values`, `paper:tversky-kahneman-1981-framing`, `paper:smith-ellsworth-1985-appraisal`, `paper:baker-2017-bayesian-mentalizing`).

The synthesis also uses cross-seat evidence where it changes the interpretation of the focal literature: state and situation effects from psychometrics; relationship, audience, and norm effects from social research; temporal update and owner-control evidence; prospective memory; and direct failures of human simulation. Those complementary records range from abstract-only foundational work to full-text system evaluations, so confidence is attached to the claim and setting rather than inherited from a familiar paper title (`paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001`, `paper:psychometrics_sherman_2015`, `paper:postmes-spears-sakhel-degroot-2001-social-influence`, `paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics`, `paper:llms-more-rational-2025`, `paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026`).

No private WhatsApp content, owner profile, message database, pseudonym key, or credential was accessed. The observability section describes what a future consented messaging source could and could not support; it does not report findings about any owner.

### Construct boundaries

| Construct | Representation target | Boundary that must remain explicit | Evidence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Declared values | Owner-endorsed priorities, tradeoffs, and compatibility or conflict relations, versioned by time and scope | A declaration is evidence about self-concept and endorsement, not proof of behavior in every setting | `paper:schwartz-2012-refined-values`, `paper:bardi-schwartz-2003-values-behavior` |
| Observed behavior | A time-, opportunity-, relationship-, and situation-bound action or communication event | An action is not a direct sensor for a value, norm endorsement, motive, or stable policy | `paper:bardi-schwartz-2003-values-behavior`, `paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001`, `paper:psychometrics_sherman_2015` |
| Norms | Group, role, audience, or relationship expectations that make conduct locally appropriate or costly | Compliance, accommodation, or self-presentation does not establish private endorsement | `paper:postmes-spears-sakhel-degroot-2001-social-influence`, `paper:youssef-1993-childrens-linguistic-choices`, `paper:trefalt-2013-between-you-and-me` |
| Motives | Competing explanations for why an action was selected under assumed options, costs, beliefs, and desires | A motive is a posterior hypothesis, not a biographical fact or a generated rationale | `paper:baker-2017-bayesian-mentalizing`, `paper:wu-2018-emotion-mental-states`, `paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026` |
| Active goals | Currently pursued desired states, including commitment, hierarchy, competition, facilitation, triggers, and completion status | Temporary inaccessibility of another goal is not abandonment; desire, conflict, resistance, and enactment are different variables | `paper:shah-2002-goal-shielding`, `paper:hofmann-2012-everyday-temptations`, `paper:cognitive-memory-einstein-et-al-2005` |
| Constructed preferences | Context-bound valuations inferred from choices, reports, and outcomes | Coherence does not imply an unanchored utility, and a post-choice report does not by itself prove preference change | `paper:ariely-2003-coherent-arbitrariness`, `paper:chen-risen-2010-free-choice` |
| Decision frames | Exact wording, gain or loss reference, option set, comparison attributes, anchors, order, and elicitation conditions | The frame is part of the evidence-generating situation, not noise to discard after storing the choice | `paper:tversky-kahneman-1981-framing`, `paper:tversky-simonson-1993-context-preferences`, `paper:ariely-2003-coherent-arbitrariness` |
| Appraisal | The person's interpretation of certainty, control, responsibility, effort, attention, and pleasantness in an episode | Appraisal is not interchangeable with valence or a categorical emotion label | `paper:smith-ellsworth-1985-appraisal`, `paper:lerner-keltner-2001-fear-anger-risk` |
| Affect dynamics | Time-indexed, potentially mixed affect with baseline, variability, reversion, intensity, and observation uncertainty | The latest detected label is neither a stable trait nor privileged access to felt experience | `paper:kuppens-2010-dynaffect`, `paper:trampe-2015-everyday-emotions`, `paper:demszky-2020-goemotions` |
| Theory-of-mind uncertainty | A distribution over another person's beliefs, desires, percepts, knowledge, and likely policies | Agreement with observer judgments does not verify the hidden state, and explicit perspective knowledge may fail to guide online interpretation | `paper:baker-2017-bayesian-mentalizing`, `paper:wu-2018-emotion-mental-states`, `paper:keysar-2003-tom-limits`, `paper:epley-2004-perspective-taking` |

## VALIDATED constraints

Here, **VALIDATED** means directly supported within the cited study settings. It does not mean that an end-to-end digital-brain implementation or WhatsApp-derived inference has been validated.

1. **Keep declarations, behavior, and norms separate.** Values can form a structured motivational continuum, but value-behavior associations vary by domain and can be weak or obscured by normative pressure. A repeated action may support or contradict an owner declaration, but it cannot silently replace it (`paper:schwartz-2012-refined-values`, `paper:bardi-schwartz-2003-values-behavior`).
2. **Bind behavior to situation and relationship.** People express broad ranges of state-like behavior, situation characteristics independently predict behavior and emotion, and relationship or group cues can activate different self-presentations and local norms. Context-conditioned conduct must not be flattened into a global owner trait or value (`paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001`, `paper:psychometrics_sherman_2015`, `paper:andersen-chen-2002-relational-self`, `paper:postmes-spears-sakhel-degroot-2001-social-influence`, `paper:tice-et-al-1995-when-modesty-prevails`).
3. **Represent goal activation rather than only a timeless goal list.** A committed focal goal can suppress access to alternatives without deleting them, while everyday desire episodes separate wanting, conflict, resistance, and enactment. Intentions can also surface through deliberate monitoring or spontaneous event cues (`paper:shah-2002-goal-shielding`, `paper:hofmann-2012-everyday-temptations`, `paper:cognitive-memory-einstein-et-al-2005`).
4. **Store the decision-producing context.** Equivalent gain/loss formulations can reverse choice, third options can change the relative appeal of existing options, arbitrary anchors can create coherent valuations, and noisy pre-choice measurement can manufacture apparent post-choice change. One isolated choice is not a context-free utility observation (`paper:tversky-kahneman-1981-framing`, `paper:tversky-simonson-1993-context-preferences`, `paper:ariely-2003-coherent-arbitrariness`, `paper:chen-risen-2010-free-choice`).
5. **Model a repertoire of decision policies, not one universal style.** Feedback can change which strategy is selected in a bounded environment, whereas rational, intuitive, dependent, avoidant, and spontaneous questionnaire scales are self-report priors rather than causal explanations of every decision (`paper:rieskamp-otto-2006-strategy-selection`, `paper:scott-bruce-1995-decision-style`).
6. **Use appraisal, not valence alone, when affect changes a decision.** Fear and anger share negative valence yet were associated with opposite risk judgments in the studied tasks, partly through certainty and control appraisals. Such effects are transient and domain-bounded, not stable risk-tolerance traits (`paper:smith-ellsworth-1985-appraisal`, `paper:lerner-keltner-2001-fear-anger-risk`).
7. **Allow mixed and dynamic affect, and keep observer labels subordinate to self-report.** Core affect can be modeled with an individual home base, variability, and reversion; everyday reports frequently mixed positive and negative states. Fine-grained text classification remains error-prone and represents how decontextualized text was labeled by readers, not what its author necessarily felt (`paper:kuppens-2010-dynaffect`, `paper:trampe-2015-everyday-emotions`, `paper:demszky-2020-goemotions`, `paper:carroll-russell-1996-face-context`).
8. **Treat mentalizing as uncertain inverse inference.** Formal models can fit human judgments in constrained tasks, but belief recovery degrades when actions and reactions are inconsistent or evidence is sparse. Humans also misuse privileged knowledge and anchor on their own perspective, especially under time pressure (`paper:baker-2017-bayesian-mentalizing`, `paper:wu-2018-emotion-mental-states`, `paper:keysar-2003-tom-limits`, `paper:epley-2004-perspective-taking`).
9. **Separate temporal change from recency.** Stable, slowly changing, and transient components can improve prediction, but indiscriminate age-based decay discards durable signal and some domains show little sequential structure. A timestamp changes evidential scope; it does not identify why a preference changed (`paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics`, `paper:li-2020-tisasrec`).
10. **Evaluate realized behavior, not plausible rationale or aggregate resemblance alone.** Human simulators can be too rational, popularity-biased, internally inconsistent, under-dispersed, or poor at exact next actions even when their answers look reasonable or their average survey scores look strong (`paper:llms-more-rational-2025`, `paper:llm-user-sim-crs-2024`, `paper:synthetic-replacements-2024`, `paper:generative-agents-1000-2024`, `paper:twin-2k-500-2025`, `paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026`).

## PLAUSIBLE design hypotheses

1. **Versioned value graph.** A graph can store owner-declared values, compatibility and conflict edges, priority by domain, historical versions, and links to supporting or countervailing behavior. Norm evidence should attach as a separate explanation rather than becoming the value itself (`paper:schwartz-2012-refined-values`, `paper:bardi-schwartz-2003-values-behavior`, `paper:postmes-spears-sakhel-degroot-2001-social-influence`).
2. **Active-goal workspace plus intention manager.** A request-time workspace can activate a focal goal, retain shielded alternatives, encode competing and facilitative edges, and hand event- or time-based commitments to a prospective-intention component. This architecture follows the evidence but has not been evaluated as a digital-brain module (`paper:shah-2002-goal-shielding`, `paper:hofmann-2012-everyday-temptations`, `paper:cognitive-memory-einstein-et-al-2005`).
3. **Decision-episode ledger.** Each consequential choice can preserve the considered and unavailable options, wording, reference point, anchor, order, social context, current goals, appraisal, selected action, outcome, and later owner interpretation. Preference updates should be proposed from episodes rather than written directly from the latest choice (`paper:tversky-kahneman-1981-framing`, `paper:tversky-simonson-1993-context-preferences`, `paper:ariely-2003-coherent-arbitrariness`, `paper:chen-risen-2010-free-choice`).
4. **Domain-conditioned policy repertoire.** The system can maintain multiple strategy hypotheses with scope, expected performance, and outcome history, while keeping learned procedure separate from an invented declarative reason. Policy weights may update locally without rewriting global decision style (`paper:rieskamp-otto-2006-strategy-selection`, `paper:scott-bruce-1995-decision-style`, `paper:cognitive-memory-cohen-squire-1980`).
5. **Two-level affect model.** Direct or owner-confirmed affect observations can feed a time-indexed latent state with appraisal dimensions, mixed labels, baseline, volatility, and reversion. Text classifiers can offer low-authority observation likelihoods with calibration and abstention, never an internal-state fact (`paper:smith-ellsworth-1985-appraisal`, `paper:kuppens-2010-dynaffect`, `paper:trampe-2015-everyday-emotions`, `paper:demszky-2020-goemotions`).
6. **Motive and other-mind hypothesis store.** The runtime can preserve alternative belief-desire explanations, the options and observability assumptions behind them, and the action or affect evidence that changed their posterior. Consequential uncertainty should generate a clarification question rather than a confident backstory (`paper:baker-2017-bayesian-mentalizing`, `paper:wu-2018-emotion-mental-states`, `paper:keysar-2003-tom-limits`, `paper:epley-2004-perspective-taking`).
7. **Typed, reversible owner correction.** Corrections can distinguish factually wrong, changed over time, relationship-specific, temporary suppression, do-not-use, and delete. Visibility and control are valuable, but broad edits can hurt objective utility or conflate novelty complaints with durable preference changes, so previews and rollback are warranted (`paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles`, `paper:bostandjiev-2012-tasteweights`).
8. **Evidence-first situation model.** Before generation, the runtime can assemble relevant values, active goals, norms, relationship context, decision frame, appraisal, affect, and motive hypotheses as separate inputs, then expose which evidence and assumptions influenced the selected response. The separation is supported; fidelity gains from this exact orchestration remain to be tested (`paper:psychometrics_sherman_2015`, `paper:sotopia-2024`, `paper:linguistic-calibration-2022`).

## SPECULATIVE claims

1. It is speculative that messaging alone can recover a stable value hierarchy. Text supplies selected expressions under particular audiences and norms, while value-behavior links are uneven and first-person profile sentences can be temporary, contradictory, or performative (`paper:bardi-schwartz-2003-values-behavior`, `paper:tice-et-al-1995-when-modesty-prevails`, `paper:persona-dialogue-mazare-2018`).
2. It is speculative that sparse text-derived affect observations are sufficient to estimate an owner's baseline, volatility, or reversion. The dynamics evidence used repeated self-report, while text labels are reader judgments with substantial fine-grained error (`paper:kuppens-2010-dynaffect`, `paper:demszky-2020-goemotions`).
3. It is speculative that inferred motives improve owner fidelity more than calibrated abstention and clarification. Inverse-planning models assume a known task and approximately rational policy, while everyday options, coercion, habits, and information are often missing (`paper:baker-2017-bayesian-mentalizing`, `paper:keysar-2003-tom-limits`, `paper:epley-2004-perspective-taking`).
4. It is speculative that a model trained on conversational choices will transfer to offline or consequential decisions. Survey-conditioned agents show bounded gains, but economic-game transfer was unclear, causal effects failed in some twins, exact shopping-action prediction remained low, and chain errors accumulated (`paper:generative-agents-1000-2024`, `paper:twin-2k-500-2025`, `paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026`, `paper:behaviorchain-2025`).
5. It is speculative that repeated owner interviews reveal a pre-existing stable self without changing the measurement target. Reflection and retelling can alter immediate self-description or later meaning, so elicitation provenance and delayed confirmation are necessary (`paper:dargembeau-garcia-jimenez-2024-working-self`, `paper:jardmo-et-al-2023-repeated-narratives`, `paper:chen-risen-2010-free-choice`).
6. It is speculative that a coherent generated explanation reveals cognition. Learned procedures can exist without declarative recall, and synthetic reasoning traces in behavioral agents are not human explanations (`paper:cognitive-memory-cohen-squire-1980`, `paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026`).

## Contradictions and boundaries

| Tension | Supporting evidence | Negative or conflicting evidence | Synthesis boundary |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Values have structure | A 19-value motivational continuum had cross-sample structural and predictive support (`paper:schwartz-2012-refined-values`) | Several value-behavior links were marginal and norms could obscure them (`paper:bardi-schwartz-2003-values-behavior`) | Store declared structure and behavioral evidence separately; do not infer behavioral fidelity from structural validity. |
| Preferences look coherent | Later valuations can remain ordered (`paper:ariely-2003-coherent-arbitrariness`) | Arbitrary anchors shift their level, frames and option sets reverse choice, and measurement can create apparent change (`paper:tversky-kahneman-1981-framing`, `paper:tversky-simonson-1993-context-preferences`, `paper:chen-risen-2010-free-choice`) | Coherence is a within-context property unless anchor and frame robustness are demonstrated. |
| People report decision styles | Five self-reported style factors were recoverable (`paper:scott-bruce-1995-decision-style`) | Feedback changed strategy selection in bounded tasks (`paper:rieskamp-otto-2006-strategy-selection`) | Treat style as a prior and observed policy as domain- and outcome-conditioned evidence. |
| A person has stable tendencies | Average state distributions can be stable (`paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001`) | Situations, significant others, groups, and audiences independently alter behavior (`paper:psychometrics_sherman_2015`, `paper:andersen-chen-2002-relational-self`, `paper:postmes-spears-sakhel-degroot-2001-social-influence`) | Represent stable priors plus relationship- and situation-scoped states, not one persona vector. |
| Emotion categories and appraisal are informative | Appraisal dimensions distinguish emotions and explain divergent risk effects (`paper:smith-ellsworth-1985-appraisal`, `paper:lerner-keltner-2001-fear-anger-risk`) | Mixed affect is common, situational context can dominate expressive cues, and text classification leaves substantial error (`paper:trampe-2015-everyday-emotions`, `paper:carroll-russell-1996-face-context`, `paper:demszky-2020-goemotions`) | Keep category, appraisal, mixture, source, and uncertainty as separate fields. |
| Formal mentalizing can fit judgments | Bayesian models closely tracked human ratings in constrained tasks (`paper:baker-2017-bayesian-mentalizing`, `paper:wu-2018-emotion-mental-states`) | Hidden-state truth was not observed, evidence conflicts caused failures, and humans made egocentric online errors (`paper:wu-2018-emotion-mental-states`, `paper:keysar-2003-tom-limits`, `paper:epley-2004-perspective-taking`) | Use posterior hypotheses to select questions or actions, never to create owner facts. |
| Temporal personalization can help | Stable and changing components improved future prediction (`paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics`) | Indiscriminate decay removed durable signal and one domain showed no obvious sequential pattern (`paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics`, `paper:li-2020-tisasrec`) | Test temporal signal per domain and make updates explicit. |
| Owner control can improve experience | Interactive weighting increased reported satisfaction and relevance (`paper:bostandjiev-2012-tasteweights`) | Profile editing reduced objective precision and recall in a small study (`paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles`) | Owner authority is non-negotiable, but edits need typed scope, preview, and rollback rather than opaque latent mutation. |
| Person simulators can predict some responses | Self-report agents and broad survey twins beat weaker baselines on selected held-out items (`paper:generative-agents-1000-2024`, `paper:twin-2k-500-2025`) | Gains varied by domain; covariance and causal effects failed, synthetic distributions compressed, risky choices became too rational, and real next actions remained hard (`paper:synthetic-replacements-2024`, `paper:llms-more-rational-2025`, `paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026`) | Report domain-specific individual, distributional, causal, and temporal tests; no aggregate digital-twin score. |
| Persona consistency is useful | Explicit profiles improved consistency (`paper:persona-dialogue-zhang-2018`) | Own-profile access did not improve engagement, and assigned short personas are not real evolving owners (`paper:persona-dialogue-zhang-2018`) | Keep consistency, engagement, owner recognition, and behavioral prediction as separate outcomes. |

## Architecture consequences

| Component | Minimum state | Update and use rule |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `value_assertion` | Content, owner wording, source type, endorsement status, domain, priority range, compatibility/conflict edges, valid interval, supporting and contradicting evidence | Only owner declaration or correction changes canonical endorsement; behavior updates evidence, not the declaration (`paper:schwartz-2012-refined-values`, `paper:bardi-schwartz-2003-values-behavior`). |
| `behavior_episode` | Action or message, time, opportunity set, relationship, audience, situation, outcome, source, uncertainty | Preserve the event before inferring value, norm, goal, motive, or policy; aggregate only within a declared domain (`paper:psychometrics_epstein_1983`, `paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001`, `paper:psychometrics_sherman_2015`). |
| `norm_context` | Group or relationship, role, audience, expected conduct, potential sanction or relational cost, source, owner endorsement unknown/accepted/rejected | Apply as a request-time contextual factor and never merge compliance into a durable value (`paper:postmes-spears-sakhel-degroot-2001-social-influence`, `paper:trefalt-2013-between-you-and-me`, `paper:youssef-1993-childrens-linguistic-choices`). |
| `goal_state` | Goal hierarchy, activation, commitment, conflict/facilitation, trigger, deadline, monitoring mode, completion evidence, expiry | Focal activation may suppress retrieval of alternatives but cannot delete them; uncompleted or unmentioned goals remain unresolved (`paper:shah-2002-goal-shielding`, `paper:hofmann-2012-everyday-temptations`, `paper:cognitive-memory-einstein-et-al-2005`). |
| `decision_episode` | Considered and unavailable options, exact wording, frame, reference, anchor, order, active goals, appraisal, affect, social context, action, outcome, confidence, later interpretation | Update a preference only through a versioned proposal that can distinguish frame sensitivity, revealed information, and genuine change (`paper:tversky-kahneman-1981-framing`, `paper:tversky-simonson-1993-context-preferences`, `paper:ariely-2003-coherent-arbitrariness`, `paper:chen-risen-2010-free-choice`). |
| `policy_hypothesis` | Domain, situation, candidate strategy, outcome history, expected performance, temporal layer, source, confidence | Maintain a repertoire and update local weights from outcomes; do not invent an explicit motive for a learned routine (`paper:rieskamp-otto-2006-strategy-selection`, `paper:scott-bruce-1995-decision-style`, `paper:cognitive-memory-cohen-squire-1980`). |
| `affect_observation` and `affect_state` | Observation source, labels and probabilities, appraisal, valence/arousal, intensity, timestamp, mixture, baseline, volatility, reversion, valid interval | Owner report or correction has greater authority than reader or model classification; no single observation updates a durable trait (`paper:smith-ellsworth-1985-appraisal`, `paper:kuppens-2010-dynaffect`, `paper:trampe-2015-everyday-emotions`, `paper:demszky-2020-goemotions`). |
| `motive_hypothesis` and `other_mind` | Actor, observability, candidate options, costs, belief/desire/percept hypotheses, action and reaction evidence, rationality assumptions, posterior, alternatives | Use for probabilistic planning and clarification only; never serialize the top hypothesis as a fact about the owner or another person (`paper:baker-2017-bayesian-mentalizing`, `paper:wu-2018-emotion-mental-states`, `paper:keysar-2003-tom-limits`, `paper:epley-2004-perspective-taking`). |
| `owner_correction` | Target component, correction type, requested scope, effective time, preview, accepted delta, rollback pointer, deletion and do-not-use state | Preserve the historical version while making owner authority effective; validate downstream effects without vetoing the owner's requested correction (`paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles`, `paper:bostandjiev-2012-tasteweights`, `paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics`). |
| `generation_trace` | Retrieved evidence, active goals, applicable norms, frame, appraisal/affect state, motive alternatives, confidence, omitted evidence, selected policy | Separate evidence, inference, assumption, and generation choice; confidence wording must be tested against correctness by claim type (`paper:linguistic-calibration-2022`, `paper:sotopia-2024`). |
| `safety_boundary` | Sensitive-inference block, subject/recipient/purpose permissions, third-party status, abstention reason | Do not infer protected traits as owner-model features and do not let a language model infer disclosure authorization from context alone (`paper:kosinski-2013-private-traits`, `paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy`, `paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide`). |

## Evaluation tests

1. **Declaration-behavior-norm separation:** present matched episodes where the same action can reflect endorsement, role duty, group pressure, or convenience. The system should preserve alternatives, request owner adjudication when consequential, and avoid converting compliance into a value (`paper:bardi-schwartz-2003-values-behavior`, `paper:postmes-spears-sakhel-degroot-2001-social-influence`, `paper:trefalt-2013-between-you-and-me`).
2. **Frame and anchor robustness:** repeat owner-approved low-stakes choices under equivalent gain/loss frames, reordered wording, changed reference points, added decoy options, and neutral versus arbitrary anchors. Report preference reversal, probability calibration, and uncertainty rather than forcing consistency (`paper:tversky-kahneman-1981-framing`, `paper:tversky-simonson-1993-context-preferences`, `paper:ariely-2003-coherent-arbitrariness`).
3. **Preference-update validity:** retain noisy pre-choice reports, the choice, and later reports, then test whether an update model distinguishes revealed information from genuine post-choice change. A free-choice spread alone must not count as learning (`paper:chen-risen-2010-free-choice`).
4. **Goal competition and completion:** test whether the system prioritizes a focal goal without losing competing or facilitative goals, distinguishes desire from conflict and enactment, and marks intentions complete only from evidence or owner confirmation (`paper:shah-2002-goal-shielding`, `paper:hofmann-2012-everyday-temptations`, `paper:cognitive-memory-einstein-et-al-2005`).
5. **Prospective policy prediction:** use chronological held-out owner decisions and outcomes, compare value-only, recency-only, generic-model, self-report-style, and full-context baselines, and report domain-specific probabilities, repeated-choice noise, and excess rationality (`paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics`, `paper:scott-bruce-1995-decision-style`, `paper:llms-more-rational-2025`, `paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026`).
6. **Appraisal and affect calibration:** test same-valence states with different certainty/control appraisals, permit mixed labels, compare model estimates with sparse direct owner reports, and score calibration, temporal error, and abstention. Reader agreement is not an internal-state gold standard (`paper:smith-ellsworth-1985-appraisal`, `paper:lerner-keltner-2001-fear-anger-risk`, `paper:kuppens-2010-dynaffect`, `paper:trampe-2015-everyday-emotions`, `paper:demszky-2020-goemotions`).
7. **Theory-of-mind uncertainty:** include false-belief and privileged-knowledge tasks, hidden options, habitual or coerced actions, conflicting action/emotion evidence, and unknown rationality. Score posterior calibration, alternative coverage, clarification quality, and abstention, not only top-hypothesis accuracy (`paper:baker-2017-bayesian-mentalizing`, `paper:wu-2018-emotion-mental-states`, `paper:keysar-2003-tom-limits`, `paper:epley-2004-perspective-taking`).
8. **Temporal update and correction:** use future-time holdouts, stable and drifting domains, model-version pins, owner corrections with scoped previews, and rollback tests. Report new-period utility, old-period retention, correction success, and false durable updates separately (`paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics`, `paper:li-2020-tisasrec`, `paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles`, `paper:synthetic-replacements-2024`).
9. **Multiaxis owner fidelity:** keep owner judgment, behavioral prediction, variance/covariance, causal-effect replication, relationship appropriateness, confidence calibration, and privacy gates separate. Survey agreement or dialogue plausibility cannot compensate for action failure, distributional collapse, or sensitive inference (`paper:generative-agents-1000-2024`, `paper:twin-2k-500-2025`, `paper:synthetic-replacements-2024`, `paper:sotopia-2024`, `paper:kosinski-2013-private-traits`).
10. **Question-policy evaluation:** compare uncertainty-only questions with expected-decision-change and answerability-aware selection; treat skip and `not sure` as query-policy evidence, not a negative preference (`paper:rashid-2002-getting-to-know-you`, `paper:boutilier-2003-active-cf`, `paper:harpale-2008-personalized-active-learning`).

## WhatsApp observability gaps

| Potentially observable in a consented archive | Not established by that observation | Required treatment | Evidence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| An explicit value statement | Its cross-domain priority, persistence, tradeoff weight, or behavioral enactment | Store as a scoped declaration and ask for conflicts or exceptions | `paper:schwartz-2012-refined-values`, `paper:bardi-schwartz-2003-values-behavior` |
| A sent choice, recommendation, or refusal | The rejected and unavailable options, exact reference point, anchor, coercion, or offline outcome | Mark the decision episode incomplete until frame and outcome are confirmed | `paper:tversky-kahneman-1981-framing`, `paper:tversky-simonson-1993-context-preferences`, `paper:ariely-2003-coherent-arbitrariness` |
| A stated desire, plan, or reminder | Goal conflict, resistance, continuing commitment, completion, or abandonment | Store desire, intention, trigger, and completion as separate statuses | `paper:hofmann-2012-everyday-temptations`, `paper:shah-2002-goal-shielding`, `paper:cognitive-memory-einstein-et-al-2005` |
| Emotive words, emoji, reaction, or tone | Felt emotion, intensity, mixture, appraisal, masking, sarcasm, or bodily state | Store a low-authority perceived-expression estimate with context and owner correction | `paper:carroll-russell-1996-face-context`, `paper:demszky-2020-goemotions`, `paper:trampe-2015-everyday-emotions` |
| Message timing, response delay, or silence | Disinterest, avoidance, forgetting, goal deactivation, or affective state | Treat as missing and multiply confounded evidence, never as a negative preference | `paper:li-2020-tisasrec`, `paper:psychometrics_montag_2015` |
| Direct-chat or group membership | The imagined audience, overhearers, local norm, role pressure, or who may receive a forward or screenshot | Keep intended, imagined, permitted, and observed audiences separate and ask before sensitive use | `paper:bell-1984-audience-design`, `paper:litt-hargittai-2016-imagined-audience`, `paper:bernstein-et-al-2013-invisible-audience` |
| A reason supplied in conversation | The causal motive, latent utility, or policy that actually produced the action | Store as a declared explanation and preserve alternative motive hypotheses | `paper:baker-2017-bayesian-mentalizing`, `paper:cognitive-memory-cohen-squire-1980`, `paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026` |
| Repeated behavior or wording | Whether repetition reflects a stable value, opportunity, habit, audience design, or one relationship's norm | Aggregate only within a declared context and retain event-level counterexamples | `paper:psychometrics_epstein_1983`, `paper:psychometrics_sherman_2015`, `paper:tice-et-al-1995-when-modesty-prevails` |
| A message mentioning another person's state or sensitive identity | That person's belief, consent, preferred audience, or the accuracy and appropriateness of the inferred trait | Keep third-party information compartmentalized; suppress protected-trait inference and require subject-purpose permissions | `paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy`, `paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide`, `paper:kosinski-2013-private-traits` |
| Rich self-report across chats | Natural behavior outside messaging or generalization across decisions | Validate on held-out real actions and preserve domain-specific failure | `paper:generative-agents-1000-2024`, `paper:llm-user-sim-crs-2024`, `paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026` |

## Highest-value owner questions

Question selection should prioritize expected change to a consequential decision, answerability, interaction effort, and coverage rather than raw uncertainty alone (`paper:rashid-2002-getting-to-know-you`, `paper:boutilier-2003-active-cf`, `paper:harpale-2008-personalized-active-learning`).

1. **Which values matter most in your current life period, and which tradeoffs would make you choose one over another?** This establishes an owner-declared structure without inferring priorities from behavior (`paper:schwartz-2012-refined-values`, `paper:bardi-schwartz-2003-values-behavior`).
2. **Where does your behavior reflect duty, politeness, group norms, or a relationship boundary rather than personal endorsement?** This distinguishes values from locally appropriate conduct (`paper:postmes-spears-sakhel-degroot-2001-social-influence`, `paper:trefalt-2013-between-you-and-me`, `paper:tice-et-al-1995-when-modesty-prevails`).
3. **What goals are active now, which goals compete or help each other, and what evidence would mark each one paused, completed, or abandoned?** This protects shielded alternatives and clarifies prospective state (`paper:shah-2002-goal-shielding`, `paper:hofmann-2012-everyday-temptations`, `paper:cognitive-memory-einstein-et-al-2005`).
4. **For one recent consequential decision, what options did you consider, what was unavailable, how was the choice framed, what served as the reference point, and what happened afterward?** This fills the largest preference-construction gaps (`paper:tversky-kahneman-1981-framing`, `paper:tversky-simonson-1993-context-preferences`, `paper:ariely-2003-coherent-arbitrariness`).
5. **Which decision approaches do you use in which domains, and when do you deliberately switch between analysis, intuition, dependence, avoidance, or spontaneity?** This scopes style and policy rather than assigning one global score (`paper:scott-bruce-1995-decision-style`, `paper:rieskamp-otto-2006-strategy-selection`).
6. **Which preferences or commitments have genuinely changed, and which recent differences were temporary, situational, or relationship-specific?** This separates drift from recency and context (`paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics`, `paper:li-2020-tisasrec`, `paper:psychometrics_sherman_2015`).
7. **When uncertainty, control, responsibility, or anticipated effort changes, how does your risk judgment or action usually change?** This elicits appraisal-linked contingencies rather than a generic emotion-to-policy rule (`paper:smith-ellsworth-1985-appraisal`, `paper:lerner-keltner-2001-fear-anger-risk`).
8. **How should the system ask about current affect, and which expressions are commonly jokes, masks, venting, or audience-specific for you?** This calibrates text interpretation against owner report and relationship context (`paper:demszky-2020-goemotions`, `paper:carroll-russell-1996-face-context`, `paper:tice-et-al-1995-when-modesty-prevails`).
9. **Which kinds of motive or belief inference should always trigger a question or abstention, especially about you or another person?** This sets a boundary around uncertain mentalizing (`paper:baker-2017-bayesian-mentalizing`, `paper:wu-2018-emotion-mental-states`, `paper:keysar-2003-tom-limits`).
10. **When you correct the model, do you mean wrong, changed, context-only, temporarily hidden, do-not-use, or delete, and should the system preview affected behavior?** This makes owner control precise and reversible (`paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles`, `paper:bostandjiev-2012-tasteweights`).
