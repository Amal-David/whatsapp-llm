# Safety Governance: Privacy, Consent, and Identity

## Evidence scope

This draft uses the deduplicated merged corpus as its citation authority. The corpus contains 187 reviewed primary papers: 100 inspected at full-text depth and 87 at abstract depth. The most direct safety evidence covers training-data leakage, contextual disclosure, interdependent privacy, attribute inference, identity classification, synthetic-media provenance, and posthumous stewardship [paper:abadi-2016-deep-learning-dp; paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide; paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy; paper:kosinski-2013-private-traits; paper:san-roman-2024-audioseal; paper:brubaker-2016-legacy-contact].

The strongest technical privacy findings are full-text studies of differential privacy, membership inference, extraction, deduplication, PII leakage, machine unlearning, and attacks against aligned production models [paper:abadi-2016-deep-learning-dp; paper:shokri-2017-membership-inference; paper:carlini-2021-training-data-extraction; paper:kandpal-2022-dedup-privacy; paper:lukas-2023-pii-leakage; paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning; paper:aligned-production-extraction-2025]. They establish concrete attack and mitigation behavior in the studied systems, but they do not establish a universal privacy budget, deletion guarantee, or safe configuration for relationship-dense private messaging [paper:abadi-2016-deep-learning-dp; paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning; paper:lukas-2023-pii-leakage].

Consent and third-party evidence is narrower. A live-style vignette study measured hypothetical disclosure intent rather than archive-import behavior, a contextual-integrity benchmark used partly synthetic scenarios, and the web-permission audit studied mutable domain signals rather than person-level consent [paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy; paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide; paper:longpre-2024-consent-crisis]. These papers validate the risk of interdependent and purpose-sensitive information flows, while the exact consent ledger below is a normative product design [paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy; paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide; paper:longpre-2024-consent-crisis].

Identity and posthumous evidence has important depth limits. The Legacy Contact and delegate-expectation records are abstract-depth studies of one platform, while the griefbot record is a full-text qualitative study of anticipated use rather than longitudinal benefit or harm [paper:brubaker-2016-legacy-contact; paper:gach-2021-facebook-affairs; paper:lu-2026-griefbot-perceptions]. The gender-schema record is also abstract-depth, so it supports a bounded warning about restrictive labels and contestability, not a general error estimate for every identity system [paper:scheuerman-2019-computers-see-gender].

Cross-seat evidence supplies additional boundaries. Owner controls are supported by small, short studies with mixed objective results; relational privacy evidence is often abstract-depth or platform-specific; and person simulators remain weak on real action, longitudinal consistency, temporal knowledge, or some held-out domains [paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles; paper:bostandjiev-2012-tasteweights; paper:lampinen-et-al-2011-were-in-it-together; paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026; paper:behaviorchain-2025; paper:timechara-2024; paper:generative-agents-1000-2024]. This draft therefore treats an integrated safety-governance system as an engineering hypothesis constrained by evidence, not as an architecture already validated end to end [paper:personalization-legitimizes-risks-2026; paper:twinvoice-2026].

Status words are used strictly:

- **VALIDATED** means directly supported in the cited study settings, not universally true.
- **PLAUSIBLE** means a convergent design hypothesis that the reviewed evidence motivates but does not test end to end.
- **SPECULATIVE** means useful to investigate but not established by this corpus.
- **NORMATIVE REQUIREMENT** means a product rule chosen to prevent unacceptable use; cited papers motivate the risk but do not prove that the exact rule is uniquely correct.

No WhatsApp content, owner profile, private database, pseudonym key, or credential was accessed for this synthesis.

## VALIDATED constraints

### 1. Model artifacts can disclose source participation and content

**Evidence-backed constraint.** Black-box outputs can reveal training membership, language models can regenerate memorized text and PII, longer true prefixes and duplication can increase extractability, and ordinary alignment can hide rather than remove leakage [paper:shokri-2017-membership-inference; paper:carlini-2021-training-data-extraction; paper:quantifying-memorization-2023; paper:kandpal-2022-dedup-privacy; paper:lukas-2023-pii-leakage; paper:aligned-production-extraction-2025].

**Normative requirement.** Raw private evidence MUST remain outside model weights by default; any exception MUST be capability-specific, owner-approved, versioned, deduplicated, privacy-accounted, and gated by extraction, reconstruction, inference, and membership attacks [paper:abadi-2016-deep-learning-dp; paper:kandpal-2022-dedup-privacy; paper:lukas-2023-pii-leakage; paper:shokri-2017-membership-inference]. Passing PII scrubbing, deduplication, differential privacy, or ordinary refusal tests MUST NOT be represented as a complete privacy guarantee [paper:kandpal-2022-dedup-privacy; paper:lukas-2023-pii-leakage; paper:aligned-production-extraction-2025].

### 2. Consent is recipient-, purpose-, capability-, subject-, and time-sensitive

**Evidence-backed constraint.** Models violated contextual privacy in multi-party disclosure and summarization tasks, including after privacy reminders; web permission signals changed and conflicted across mechanisms; and a third-party salience intervention changed intended disclosure of address-book data [paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide; paper:longpre-2024-consent-crisis; paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy].

**Normative requirement.** Every data use MUST resolve a machine-readable ConsentGrant before ingestion, derivation, retrieval, training, simulation, export, or disclosure. A grant MUST name the data subject, author or source, capability, purpose, audience or recipient class, modality, allowed transformations, validity period, retention rule, and grant evidence [paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide; paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy; paper:longpre-2024-consent-crisis]. Unknown, ambiguous, expired, denied, revoked, or disputed consent MUST fail closed; prose prompting MUST NOT substitute for policy evaluation [paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide].

### 3. Third-party messages are contextual evidence, not identity inventory

**Evidence-backed constraint.** Disclosure decisions can expose other people, participants use collaborative boundary work, imagined and actual audiences diverge, and prior disclosure does not reliably encode a stable privacy preference [paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy; paper:lampinen-et-al-2011-were-in-it-together; paper:litt-hargittai-2016-imagined-audience; paper:bernstein-et-al-2013-invisible-audience; paper:acquisti-gross-2006-imagined-communities; paper:vitak-2012-context-collapse-privacy].

**Normative requirement.** Third-party-authored content MUST be isolated from the owner's identity model, excluded from tuning, excluded from contact-persona construction, and omitted from exports unless a separate permission authorizes the exact use [paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy; paper:lampinen-et-al-2011-were-in-it-together]. Relationship summaries MAY retain a minimal, source-linked statement about the owner's own behavior, but MUST redact or quarantine another person's sensitive facts and MUST preserve that the surrounding context is incomplete [paper:lampinen-et-al-2011-were-in-it-together; paper:cognitive-memory-hashtroudi-johnson-chrosniak-1989].

### 4. Sensitive inference is feasible, proxy-rich, and unevenly valid

**Evidence-backed constraint.** Likes, social edges, and behavioral joins predicted sensitive or hidden attributes in the studied datasets; prediction confidence did not make the inference authorized or individually true [paper:kosinski-2013-private-traits; paper:jernigan-2009-gaydar; paper:gong-2016-attribute-inference]. Identity classifiers also embedded restrictive schemas and large intersectional error disparities, while behavioral personality prediction varied sharply by trait and setting [paper:buolamwini-2018-gender-shades; paper:scheuerman-2019-computers-see-gender; paper:psychometrics_stachl_2020; paper:psychometrics_montag_2015].

**Normative requirement.** The product MUST NOT infer, rank, persist, or expose protected or highly sensitive attributes from messages, contacts, voice, face, relationship graphs, embeddings, or usage behavior [paper:kosinski-2013-private-traits; paper:jernigan-2009-gaydar; paper:gong-2016-attribute-inference; paper:buolamwini-2018-gender-shades]. Explicit owner self-description MAY be stored only in the owner's wording, marked self_reported, scoped to a declared purpose, and made independently correctable and deletable; a removed field MUST NOT be silently reconstructed from proxies [paper:scheuerman-2019-computers-see-gender; paper:jia-2018-attriguard; paper:gong-2016-attribute-inference].

### 5. Personalization can weaken safety behavior

**Evidence-backed constraint.** In PS-Bench, benign memories and persona information increased harmful-query attack success relative to stateless baselines, while a post-retrieval detection-reflection intervention reduced much of the measured degradation [paper:personalization-legitimizes-risks-2026]. The benchmark is text-only, partly synthetic, and does not validate tool-using or long-term write-back safety [paper:personalization-legitimizes-risks-2026].

**Normative requirement.** Safety policy MUST run after retrieval and inspect the same personal context presented to the generator; a pre-retrieval refusal classifier alone is insufficient [paper:personalization-legitimizes-risks-2026]. Retrieved personal evidence MUST NOT relax a safety boundary, and proposed unsafe or unverified outputs MUST NOT enter durable memory merely because the assistant generated them [paper:personalization-legitimizes-risks-2026; paper:cognitive-memory-hupbach-et-al-2007].

### 6. Likeness detection and provenance are complementary, bounded controls

**Evidence-backed constraint.** AudioSeal supports watermarking and localization for generators integrated with its scheme, but white-box access weakens removal resistance; FaceForensics++ shows strong bounded in-domain detection and substantial degradation under compression, without proving future-generator generalization [paper:san-roman-2024-audioseal; paper:rossler-2019-faceforensics].

**Normative requirement.** Synthetic voice or face generation MUST require explicit subject and modality authorization, an always-visible synthetic disclosure, a signed generation record, and a compatible embedded watermark when the system controls generation [paper:san-roman-2024-audioseal; paper:rossler-2019-faceforensics]. Detector confidence or watermark presence MUST NOT be treated as proof of consent, and watermark absence MUST NOT be treated as proof of authenticity [paper:san-roman-2024-audioseal; paper:rossler-2019-faceforensics].

### 7. Posthumous stewardship does not establish posthumous personation

**Evidence-backed constraint.** Legacy Contact separates narrow stewardship from logging in as the deceased, delegate expectations can diverge from actual feature powers, and griefbot participants anticipated both comfort and harms such as dependency, inauthenticity, and misrepresentation [paper:brubaker-2016-legacy-contact; paper:gach-2021-facebook-affairs; paper:lu-2026-griefbot-perceptions]. The first two records are abstract-depth and the griefbot evidence does not establish therapeutic benefit or longitudinal harm [paper:brubaker-2016-legacy-contact; paper:gach-2021-facebook-affairs; paper:lu-2026-griefbot-perceptions].

**Normative requirement.** Posthumous text, voice, and face simulation MUST default off and MUST require a specific advance directive for each modality, purpose, audience, duration, steward power, and revocation path [paper:brubaker-2016-legacy-contact; paper:gach-2021-facebook-affairs; paper:lu-2026-griefbot-perceptions]. Account inheritance, kinship, inactivity, archive possession, or a general preservation setting MUST NOT activate personation; disputed or absent directives MUST place simulation in a hard-pause state [paper:brubaker-2016-legacy-contact; paper:gach-2021-facebook-affairs].

### 8. Auditability requires source and version boundaries

**Evidence-backed constraint.** Content can be available while its source remains confusable, reactivation can introduce delayed source intrusions, confidence can reflect visible cue-target fluency rather than later retrievability, and identical synthetic-survey prompts can drift after a model update [paper:cognitive-memory-hashtroudi-johnson-chrosniak-1989; paper:cognitive-memory-hupbach-et-al-2007; paper:cognitive-memory-koriat-bjork-2005; paper:synthetic-replacements-2024].

**Normative requirement.** Every derived claim and generated output MUST carry source IDs, source class, direct-versus-inferred status, relationship and audience scope, observation and validity times, model and policy versions, retrieved evidence, denied evidence classes, uncertainty, and the final guard decision [paper:cognitive-memory-hashtroudi-johnson-chrosniak-1989; paper:cognitive-memory-hupbach-et-al-2007; paper:timechara-2024; paper:synthetic-replacements-2024]. Audit logs MUST record policy decisions without retaining unnecessary raw third-party text, because auditability cannot become a second ungoverned copy of sensitive evidence [paper:lukas-2023-pii-leakage; paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning].

### 9. High-stakes simulation requires abstention and owner authority

**Evidence-backed constraint.** Linguistic confidence is often miscalibrated, person simulators can be too rational, use future information, fail to reproduce real next actions, or perform unevenly across survey, game, and experimental domains [paper:linguistic-calibration-2022; paper:llms-more-rational-2025; paper:timechara-2024; paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026; paper:generative-agents-1000-2024]. Fluent recognition of a persona also does not guarantee faithful generation [paper:twinvoice-2026].

**Normative requirement.** For consequential medical, legal, financial, physical-safety, reputational, relationship-boundary, or external-commitment decisions, the system MUST switch from owner simulation to evidence-only assistance, identify missing evidence, and ask the living owner or abstain [paper:linguistic-calibration-2022; paper:llms-more-rational-2025; paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026]. It MUST NOT claim to be the owner, send or commit on the owner's behalf, or present a simulated preference as authority; this prohibition is a program safety choice motivated by observed fidelity and calibration failures, not a paper-validated threshold [paper:behaviorchain-2025; paper:twinvoice-2026; paper:generative-agents-1000-2024].

## PLAUSIBLE design hypotheses

1. **Capability-scoped consent ledger.** A deterministic policy engine over ConsentGrant records is likely safer and more auditable than asking an LLM to infer permission from conversational context, because prompting did not reliably enforce contextual integrity and permission signals are purpose-sensitive [paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide; paper:longpre-2024-consent-crisis; paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy].
2. **Retrieval-first private memory.** Keeping mutable personal evidence in a local, access-controlled retrieval store rather than weights should make purpose checks, correction, revocation, and provenance more executable, although the corpus does not directly compare complete retrieval-first and fine-tuned private digital brains [paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning; paper:carlini-2021-training-data-extraction; paper:persona-dialogue-salemi-2024-lamp; paper:persona-dialogue-wang-2024-emg-rag].
3. **Relationship-scoped third-party vaults.** Isolating third-party-authored content by relationship and audience should reduce cross-contact leakage and context collapse without flattening the owner's relational behavior into one persona [paper:lampinen-et-al-2011-were-in-it-together; paper:litt-hargittai-2016-imagined-audience; paper:bernstein-et-al-2013-invisible-audience; paper:andersen-chen-2002-relational-self].
4. **Two-phase revocation.** An immediate authorization deny followed by a lineage-driven purge and adversarial verification should prevent new use while slower deletion propagates through summaries, indexes, caches, adapters, checkpoints, and backups [paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning; paper:shokri-2017-membership-inference; paper:lukas-2023-pii-leakage]. The exact verification suite and acceptable residual risk are not established for generative personal models [paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning; paper:aligned-production-extraction-2025].
5. **Sensitive-inference firewall.** Combining forbidden-output rules, feature-deny policies, owner-self-report provenance, and proxy-recovery red teams should be more robust than deleting only explicit sensitive fields [paper:kosinski-2013-private-traits; paper:jernigan-2009-gaydar; paper:gong-2016-attribute-inference; paper:jia-2018-attriguard].
6. **Identity output envelope.** Binding each text, voice, or face simulation to a subject authorization, disclosed synthetic status, model version, purpose, audience, expiry, watermark where compatible, and signed generation log should make authorized synthesis distinguishable from impersonation attempts [paper:san-roman-2024-audioseal; paper:rossler-2019-faceforensics].
7. **Posthumous state machine.** A state machine with explicit directive, verified death status, steward scope, dispute, pause, expiry, and deletion transitions should be safer than inheriting the living account's ordinary settings [paper:brubaker-2016-legacy-contact; paper:gach-2021-facebook-affairs; paper:lu-2026-griefbot-perceptions].
8. **Calibrated high-stakes gate.** A deterministic stakes classifier plus evidence sufficiency, authority, relationship-boundary, and recipient checks should reduce confident overreach, but exact categories and thresholds require prospective evaluation [paper:linguistic-calibration-2022; paper:llms-more-rational-2025; paper:personalization-legitimizes-risks-2026; paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide].
9. **Typed owner corrections.** Separate controls for wrong, stale, context-only, do-not-use, hide, revoke, and delete should preserve owner authority while avoiding the broad model damage observed when narrow interface problems caused profile removals [paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles; paper:bostandjiev-2012-tasteweights; paper:bakalov-2013-controllable-user-models].
10. **Evidence-derived identity, never generated identity.** Preventing generated replies, commonsense expansions, and roleplay outputs from becoming owner facts should reduce self-reinforcing identity drift and false source attribution [paper:persona-dialogue-majumder-2020; paper:persona-dialogue-gao-2023-peacok; paper:persona-dialogue-welleck-2019; paper:cognitive-memory-hupbach-et-al-2007].

## SPECULATIVE claims

1. It is speculative that a practical product can obtain meaningful affirmative consent from every person represented in a large historical group archive; the available third-party study measured hypothetical intent and does not validate a deployed correspondent-consent system [paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy; paper:lampinen-et-al-2011-were-in-it-together].
2. It is speculative that any current process can prove complete forgetting from a generative model and every downstream export; SISA demonstrates bounded unlearning speedups for classifiers, while extraction studies show that residual model behavior can be difficult to observe [paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning; paper:aligned-production-extraction-2025; paper:lukas-2023-pii-leakage].
3. It is speculative that watermarking and detection can survive all editing, re-recording, unseen generators, or white-box attacks; both primary mechanisms report bounded robustness and access-model limitations [paper:san-roman-2024-audioseal; paper:rossler-2019-faceforensics].
4. It is speculative that cooldowns, session limits, or steward oversight improve bereavement outcomes; the griefbot study reports anticipated perceptions and cannot establish therapeutic benefit or causal harm [paper:lu-2026-griefbot-perceptions].
5. It is speculative that a digital brain can learn a reliable owner-specific high-stakes abstention threshold from WhatsApp behavior, because confidence calibration was tested on factual QA and simulation failures vary by domain [paper:linguistic-calibration-2022; paper:generative-agents-1000-2024; paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026].
6. It is speculative that deleting an explicit sensitive field can remove all proxy information from embeddings, graphs, summaries, or generated behavior; graph and behavior attacks recover attributes from correlated traces, while AttriGuard provides only a bounded empirical defense [paper:jernigan-2009-gaydar; paper:gong-2016-attribute-inference; paper:jia-2018-attriguard].
7. It is speculative that more audit detail monotonically improves safety or owner trust; small control studies show demand for transparency but mixed objective effects, and retained logs can themselves enlarge the sensitive-data surface [paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles; paper:bostandjiev-2012-tasteweights; paper:bakalov-2013-controllable-user-models; paper:lukas-2023-pii-leakage].
8. It is speculative that WhatsApp-only evidence can support a faithful, integrated, consented replica. Message-conditioned systems and self-report agents show partial success, while real-action, cross-context, temporal, and cumulative fidelity remain weak or uneven [paper:generative-agents-1000-2024; paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026; paper:twinvoice-2026; paper:behaviorchain-2025; paper:timechara-2024].

## Contradictions and boundaries

1. **Personal evidence can improve prediction and increase exposure.** Rich interviews and surveys improved some held-out survey predictions, but gains did not clearly transfer to economic games and the same reusable agents create privacy, deanonymization, withdrawal, and deletion risks [paper:generative-agents-1000-2024].
2. **Retrieval can improve memory and weaken safety.** Long-memory and retrieval systems improve access or consistency, while personalized retrieved context can legitimize harmful intent and contextual summarization can disclose another person's facts [paper:memgpt-2023; paper:persona-dialogue-xu-2022-lemon; paper:secom-2025; paper:personalization-legitimizes-risks-2026; paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide].
3. **Owner control is authoritative even when prediction worsens.** Users preferred visibility and control, yet direct profile editing reduced objective recommendation performance in one small study and interactive gains were confounded with extra feedback in another [paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles; paper:bostandjiev-2012-tasteweights; paper:bakalov-2013-controllable-user-models]. Product legitimacy therefore cannot be optimized as recommendation accuracy alone [paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles; paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning].
4. **Disclosure history is not permission.** Concern, settings, imagined audience, actual reach, and disclosure diverge; intimate content also changes meaning and perceived appropriateness across private and public contexts [paper:acquisti-gross-2006-imagined-communities; paper:vitak-2012-context-collapse-privacy; paper:litt-hargittai-2016-imagined-audience; paper:bernstein-et-al-2013-invisible-audience; paper:bazarova-2012-public-intimacy].
5. **Privacy defenses reduce measured risk without eliminating it.** Differential privacy gives a formal bound under chosen assumptions, deduplication sharply lowers measured regeneration, and PII scrubbing helps, but unique data, contextual disclosure, residual PII, and adaptive attacks remain [paper:abadi-2016-deep-learning-dp; paper:kandpal-2022-dedup-privacy; paper:lukas-2023-pii-leakage; paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide; paper:aligned-production-extraction-2025].
6. **Provenance and deletion pull in opposite directions unless stores are separated.** Source and version history are needed to detect confusion and reconstruct decisions, but raw evidence and derived artifacts must remain deletable; a minimally identifying policy log should survive only where its retention is itself authorized [paper:cognitive-memory-hashtroudi-johnson-chrosniak-1989; paper:cognitive-memory-hupbach-et-al-2007; paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning; paper:lukas-2023-pii-leakage].
7. **Synthetic-media controls are not authorization.** Watermarks can provide generation-path evidence and detectors can flag known manipulation artifacts, but neither establishes identity, intent, or consent [paper:san-roman-2024-audioseal; paper:rossler-2019-faceforensics].
8. **Posthumous presence can be experienced as comfort and harm.** The same simulated continuity motivated anticipated comfort, dependency, inauthenticity, prolonged grief, and misrepresentation concerns; engagement duration is therefore not a defensible benefit metric [paper:lu-2026-griefbot-perceptions].
9. **Aggregate plausibility can hide individual failure.** Population or survey means can look human-like while variance, covariance, causal effects, next actions, or cumulative trajectories fail [paper:out-of-one-many-2023; paper:synthetic-replacements-2024; paper:turing-experiments-2023; paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026; paper:behaviorchain-2025].
10. **Stability is not truth, and change is not error.** Repeated narratives can preserve event identity while meaning changes, model outputs can drift after updates, and retrieval can contaminate source memory; audit must preserve versions rather than selecting the most repeated statement as ground truth [paper:kober-habermas-2017-personal-past-stability; paper:jardmo-et-al-2023-repeated-narratives; paper:synthetic-replacements-2024; paper:cognitive-memory-hupbach-et-al-2007].

## Architecture consequences

### Control plane

| Component | Product-enforceable behavior | Evidence basis and status |
|---|---|---|
| SourceManifest | Records source, author, subjects, direct/quoted/forwarded status, collection time, original audience, retention, duplicate group, and allowed uses before any derivation. Missing subject or permission metadata causes quarantine [paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy; paper:kandpal-2022-dedup-privacy; paper:cognitive-memory-hashtroudi-johnson-chrosniak-1989]. | PLAUSIBLE design implementing validated interdependent-privacy, duplication, and source-confusion constraints [paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy; paper:kandpal-2022-dedup-privacy; paper:cognitive-memory-hashtroudi-johnson-chrosniak-1989]. |
| Consent PDP | Evaluates subject x source x capability x purpose x audience x modality x time. States are UNKNOWN, GRANTED, DENIED, REVOKED, EXPIRED, and DISPUTED; only an authenticated human or imported directive can grant authority [paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide; paper:longpre-2024-consent-crisis; paper:gach-2021-facebook-affairs]. | NORMATIVE REQUIREMENT motivated by validated context and permission failures [paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide; paper:longpre-2024-consent-crisis]. |
| Evidence vaults | Separates owner-authored evidence, third-party-authored context, owner self-report, derived claims, sensitive self-description, and synthetic output. Cross-vault retrieval requires an explicit policy decision [paper:lampinen-et-al-2011-were-in-it-together; paper:scheuerman-2019-computers-see-gender; paper:persona-dialogue-xu-2022-lemon]. | PLAUSIBLE; no reviewed paper evaluates this complete partition [paper:lampinen-et-al-2011-were-in-it-together; paper:persona-dialogue-xu-2022-lemon]. |
| Derivation registry | Maps each summary, embedding, graph edge, adapter, checkpoint, cache, and export to source IDs, policy version, model version, and transformation [paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning; paper:cognitive-memory-hupbach-et-al-2007; paper:synthetic-replacements-2024]. | PLAUSIBLE implementation of deletion lineage and version boundaries [paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning; paper:synthetic-replacements-2024]. |
| Revocation orchestrator | On revocation, denies new access synchronously, marks dependent claims unusable, starts purge jobs, rebuilds affected indexes or artifacts, runs post-deletion attacks, and reports ENFORCED_AT_GATE, PURGE_PENDING, VERIFIED, or EXTERNAL_RESIDUAL [paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning; paper:shokri-2017-membership-inference; paper:lukas-2023-pii-leakage]. | NORMATIVE state machine; complete generative forgetting remains speculative [paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning; paper:aligned-production-extraction-2025]. |
| Sensitive inference firewall | Blocks protected-trait objectives, graph propagation, proxy-based output, contact profiling, and reconstitution of deleted labels; preserves only purpose-scoped owner self-description [paper:kosinski-2013-private-traits; paper:jernigan-2009-gaydar; paper:gong-2016-attribute-inference; paper:scheuerman-2019-computers-see-gender]. | NORMATIVE REQUIREMENT motivated by validated inference and classification harms [paper:kosinski-2013-private-traits; paper:buolamwini-2018-gender-shades]. |
| Retrieval and output guard | Applies authorization before context assembly, reruns safety and recipient checks after retrieval, detects contradiction and temporal leakage, and refuses unauthorized quoting or disclosure [paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide; paper:personalization-legitimizes-risks-2026; paper:persona-dialogue-welleck-2019; paper:timechara-2024]. | PLAUSIBLE combination of directly tested but separate mechanisms [paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide; paper:personalization-legitimizes-risks-2026; paper:persona-dialogue-welleck-2019; paper:timechara-2024]. |
| Identity output gateway | Requires subject authorization and creates a GenerationRecord with disclosed synthetic status, purpose, audience, model, policy, source set, expiry, signature, and compatible watermark. It blocks hidden simulation and third-party likeness generation [paper:san-roman-2024-audioseal; paper:rossler-2019-faceforensics; paper:twinvoice-2026]. | NORMATIVE REQUIREMENT; provenance mechanisms are validated only in bounded media settings [paper:san-roman-2024-audioseal; paper:rossler-2019-faceforensics]. |
| Posthumous controller | Uses LIVING, DEATH_UNVERIFIED, DIRECTIVE_ACTIVE, DISPUTED, PAUSED, EXPIRED, and DELETED states. A steward can administer only enumerated directive powers and cannot create undisclosed statements in the person's voice [paper:brubaker-2016-legacy-contact; paper:gach-2021-facebook-affairs; paper:lu-2026-griefbot-perceptions]. | NORMATIVE REQUIREMENT motivated by limited stewardship and perception evidence [paper:brubaker-2016-legacy-contact; paper:gach-2021-facebook-affairs; paper:lu-2026-griefbot-perceptions]. |
| Audit and owner console | Shows sources, denied inputs, claim versions, policy decisions, corrections, revocations, model changes, generation records, and unresolved conflicts without exposing unauthorized third-party text [paper:bakalov-2013-controllable-user-models; paper:bostandjiev-2012-tasteweights; paper:cognitive-memory-hupbach-et-al-2007; paper:lukas-2023-pii-leakage]. | PLAUSIBLE; control evidence is small and mixed [paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles; paper:bostandjiev-2012-tasteweights; paper:bakalov-2013-controllable-user-models]. |
| High-stakes gate | Returns ALLOW_EVIDENCE_ONLY, ASK_OWNER, ABSTAIN, or BLOCK after checking authority, evidence sufficiency, uncertainty, recipient, relationship boundary, and action consequences. It never returns autonomous owner representation [paper:linguistic-calibration-2022; paper:llms-more-rational-2025; paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026; paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide]. | NORMATIVE REQUIREMENT motivated by validated calibration, simulation, and disclosure failures [paper:linguistic-calibration-2022; paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026; paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide]. |

### Hard product invariants

1. No source may be retrieved, transformed, trained on, simulated from, exported, or disclosed without a current affirmative policy decision for that exact operation [paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide; paper:longpre-2024-consent-crisis].
2. No third-party-authored message may become an owner fact or a contact persona by default [paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy; paper:lampinen-et-al-2011-were-in-it-together].
3. No inferred protected or highly sensitive attribute may enter durable state or generated output [paper:kosinski-2013-private-traits; paper:jernigan-2009-gaydar; paper:gong-2016-attribute-inference].
4. No generated text, roleplay inference, or synthetic rationale may become source evidence without a separate owner-confirmed event [paper:persona-dialogue-welleck-2019; paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026; paper:cognitive-memory-hupbach-et-al-2007].
5. No text, voice, or face simulation may hide its synthetic status or bypass subject authorization [paper:san-roman-2024-audioseal; paper:rossler-2019-faceforensics; paper:twinvoice-2026].
6. No system output may claim to be the living owner or send, approve, sign, purchase, publish, or commit on the owner's behalf [paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026; paper:llms-more-rational-2025; paper:behaviorchain-2025].
7. No posthumous simulation may activate from silence, inactivity, kinship, account inheritance, or generic retention settings [paper:brubaker-2016-legacy-contact; paper:gach-2021-facebook-affairs].
8. No deletion may be reported complete until in-scope derived artifacts are enumerated and verification runs finish; unreachable external copies must be disclosed as residuals [paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning; paper:aligned-production-extraction-2025].
9. No average fidelity or utility score may offset an unauthorized disclosure, sensitive inference, deceptive impersonation, or posthumous-directive violation [paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide; paper:kosinski-2013-private-traits; paper:san-roman-2024-audioseal; paper:lu-2026-griefbot-perceptions].

| Personalized safety | Recreate stateless and memory-grounded harmful requests, semantically aligned benign memories, persona cues, multi-turn escalation, and tool requests [paper:personalization-legitimizes-risks-2026]. | Post-retrieval safety must not underperform the stateless baseline on severe categories; limitations for modalities and tools remain explicit [paper:personalization-legitimizes-risks-2026]. |

## Highest-value owner questions

1. **Which capabilities do you authorize for each source: local search, summarization, profile claims, retrieval, evaluation, tuning, text simulation, voice, face, export, or sharing?** A single archive-level choice cannot represent purpose- and capability-specific consent [paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide; paper:longpre-2024-consent-crisis].
2. **What should happen to messages from people who have not separately consented: exclude, retain locally as quarantined context, redact into owner-only events, or ask before each use?** Owner authority alone does not resolve interdependent privacy [paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy; paper:lampinen-et-al-2011-were-in-it-together].
3. **Which topics, people, groups, and relationship contexts must never cross into another audience or generated response?** Intended and actual audiences can diverge, and disclosure meaning changes by visibility [paper:litt-hargittai-2016-imagined-audience; paper:bernstein-et-al-2013-invisible-audience; paper:bazarova-2012-public-intimacy].
4. **Which identity fields, if any, do you want to self-describe for a specific purpose, and which fields must never be inferred even privately?** Behavioral and graph data can expose sensitive labels, while classifier schemas can misrepresent identity [paper:kosinski-2013-private-traits; paper:jernigan-2009-gaydar; paper:scheuerman-2019-computers-see-gender].
5. **When you revoke data, do you mean stop future use, hide from the interface, delete source evidence, rebuild derivatives, invalidate trained artifacts, or all of these?** Deletion scope and technical forgetting are distinct operations [paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning; paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles].
6. **What residuals are acceptable when deletion cannot recall an external export or prove complete generative forgetting, and what evidence must the product show before saying "verified"?** Available unlearning and extraction evidence does not justify an absolute guarantee [paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning; paper:aligned-production-extraction-2025].
7. **May the system ever produce text in your first-person voice, and must every such output stay local, be watermarked or signed, expire, or require per-use confirmation?** Fluent persona generation does not establish authority, and provenance controls have bounded robustness [paper:twinvoice-2026; paper:san-roman-2024-audioseal].
8. **Are voice or face generation categorically prohibited, or allowed for named purposes and audiences under fresh confirmation?** Detection does not prove consent, and integrated watermarking covers only controlled generators [paper:san-roman-2024-audioseal; paper:rossler-2019-faceforensics].
9. **Which decisions are high-stakes for you, and should the system always ask, provide evidence only, or refuse to simulate your answer?** Calibration and behavioral fidelity vary by task and can fail despite plausible output [paper:linguistic-calibration-2022; paper:llms-more-rational-2025; paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026].
10. **May the system ever draft an external message or action, and what must remain impossible even after confirmation?** Multi-turn simulators reproduce real actions poorly and personalization can weaken safety behavior [paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026; paper:personalization-legitimizes-risks-2026].
11. **What posthumous preservation, deletion, memorial access, text, voice, face, audience, duration, and steward powers do you choose, and how often should they be reconfirmed?** Stewardship, simulation, and bereavement outcomes are distinct and incompletely evidenced [paper:brubaker-2016-legacy-contact; paper:gach-2021-facebook-affairs; paper:lu-2026-griefbot-perceptions].
12. **What should an audit show, who may inspect it, and how much third-party detail may it retain?** Visibility can improve perceived control, but logs and explanations can also preserve sensitive data or degrade model behavior if controls are too coarse [paper:bakalov-2013-controllable-user-models; paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles; paper:lukas-2023-pii-leakage].
13. **When evidence conflicts, should the system preserve multiple relationship- or time-scoped versions, ask you immediately, or abstain until a consequential use arises?** Repeated narratives change meaning, source confusion is real, and future or superseded knowledge can leak into simulation [paper:jardmo-et-al-2023-repeated-narratives; paper:cognitive-memory-hupbach-et-al-2007; paper:timechara-2024].
14. **Which safety controls are non-overridable even by the owner: protected-trait inference, deceptive impersonation, third-party simulation, autonomous commitment, or disputed posthumous use?** The evidence shows that personalization, confidence, and prior disclosure are not reliable substitutes for these boundaries [paper:personalization-legitimizes-risks-2026; paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide; paper:llms-more-rational-2025].
