# Relationship-Scoped Selves

## Evidence Scope

Corpus metadata: this synthesis uses the deduplicated, cross-reviewed corpus of 187 unique papers. The merged inspection-depth mix is 100 full-text and 87 abstract records, with no metadata-only record. The core social-relationships slice contributes 20 papers, of which 6 are full-text and 14 are abstract inspections.

No WhatsApp content, private profile, local database, message export, credential, or key was accessed. Claims below concern what a future consented system could and could not infer.

Status terms are used narrowly:

- **VALIDATED** means the claim is directly supported in the included paper's studied setting. It does not mean that an end-to-end digital brain has been validated.
- **PLAUSIBLE** means multiple records motivate a concrete design hypothesis, but the proposed architecture itself has not been tested.
- **SPECULATIVE** means the claim is useful enough to test but is not established by the corpus.

The strongest evidence for relationship scope comes from relational-self theory, laboratory audience and common-ground studies, naturalistic or platform communication studies, and qualitative boundary-management research. Much of the foundational social evidence is abstract-depth, and many studies are narrow by platform, culture, age, relationship type, or task, so it supports bounded constraints rather than prevalence estimates or universal social rules (paper:andersen-chen-2002-relational-self; paper:bell-1984-audience-design; paper:youssef-1993-childrens-linguistic-choices; paper:lampinen-et-al-2011-were-in-it-together).

Cross-seat evidence strengthens the boundaries around those social findings. Psychometrics separates broad dispositions from occasion- and situation-specific states; privacy work shows that relationship graphs and multi-party information flows expose people other than the owner; narrative work shows that telling and meaning vary by audience; and evaluation work shows that short roleplay or recognition performance does not establish persistent relationship fidelity (paper:psychometrics_epstein_1983; paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001; paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy; paper:mclean-2005-meaning-telling; paper:persona-dialogue-arimoto-2024; paper:twinvoice-2026).

Negative and null evidence is retained rather than averaged away: WhatsApp-use duration had no clear openness or agreeableness association; own-profile conditioning improved persona consistency but not engagement; aggressive profile editing reduced objective recommendation metrics in a small study; audience adaptation was not immediate; and category-level linguistic alignment was limited (paper:psychometrics_montag_2015; paper:persona-dialogue-zhang-2018; paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles; paper:horton-gerrig-2002-speakers-experiences; paper:doyle-frank-2016-sources-linguistic-alignment).

No included paper validates reconstruction of a consenting person's relationship-scoped selves from WhatsApp alone. The closest benchmarks use short social scenarios, acted chat, public archives, synthetic histories, or distinct corpora that do not form one person's integrated relational life (paper:sotopia-2024; paper:persona-dialogue-arimoto-2024; paper:twinvoice-2026; paper:synthetic-replacements-2024).

## VALIDATED Constraints

### 1. Context-conditioned behavior is not automatically global identity

People can show wide within-person ranges of trait-relevant states, and situation characteristics independently contribute to behavior and emotion. Relationship cues, friend-versus-stranger audiences, and salient group norms can also change self-evaluation, presentation, and conduct. A single message or one relationship's recurring pattern is therefore insufficient evidence for a context-free owner trait (paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001; paper:psychometrics_sherman_2015; paper:andersen-chen-2002-relational-self; paper:tice-et-al-1995-when-modesty-prevails; paper:postmes-spears-sakhel-degroot-2001-social-influence).

Broad owner estimates require aggregation across occasions and intended domains, but aggregation can erase stable contingencies when contexts are mixed. Global summaries must preserve the event, relationship, audience, role, and time slices from which they were derived (paper:psychometrics_epstein_1983; paper:psychometrics_mairesse_2007; paper:psychometrics_rieman_2017).

Observed behavior is also an unreliable direct sensor for values or motives because value-behavior links vary by domain and can be obscured by normative pressure. The same constraint applies to message volume: WhatsApp-use duration showed small and uneven trait associations, with age and gender variation stronger than most reported trait links (paper:bardi-schwartz-2003-values-behavior; paper:psychometrics_montag_2015).

### 2. Roles and group norms can activate local behavior

Work-nonwork boundary tactics varied by counterpart, and the combination of relationship and tactic affected both substantive and relational outcomes. In controlled computer-mediated groups, a salient group identity transmitted a primed local norm under anonymity. Role obligations and group norms must therefore remain contextual explanations, not be silently promoted to owner-global preferences (paper:trefalt-2013-between-you-and-me; paper:postmes-spears-sakhel-degroot-2001-social-influence).

Relationship memories can contain closeness, separation, conflict, and meaning at the same time. A single relationship-health label discards coexisting dimensions and converts an owner-centered memory into an unsupported claim about the other person (paper:mclean-thorne-2003-relationship-memories).

### 3. Audience is plural, message-specific, and imperfectly known

Audience design distinguishes direct addressees, auditors, overhearers, and absent reference groups, but a fixed hierarchy is not universal: wider social norms and the personal significance of an audience member can outweigh simple role ordering. Speakers may also need experience before adapting effectively to a partner's knowledge (paper:bell-1984-audience-design; paper:youssef-1993-childrens-linguistic-choices; paper:horton-gerrig-2002-speakers-experiences).

An imagined audience can change from one post to the next even when technical permissions remain stable. Actual reach can exceed the owner's estimate by several-fold, and likes, comments, friend counts, and prior reach are unreliable estimators of exposure. Intended, imagined, permitted, and observed audiences are therefore distinct evidence fields (paper:litt-hargittai-2016-imagined-audience; paper:bernstein-et-al-2013-invisible-audience).

### 4. Interaction history can create partner-specific common ground

Repeated interaction can establish shared labels that persist, simplify, and later be renegotiated. A changed label from the original partner delayed comprehension in a constrained task, while the same change from a new partner did not; however, the paper's own limitation preserves a later alternative mechanism involving recovery from partner-independent preemption. Partner-specific wording is supported, but early partner-specific mental lookup is not settled (paper:brennan-clark-1996-conceptual-pacts; paper:metzing-brennan-2003-conceptual-pacts-broken).

Partner-specific common ground is therefore evidence about a dyad and an episode, not a permanent language rule or a property of either person alone. A repeated phrase can still be quotation, task language, coincidence, or a stale pact (paper:brennan-clark-1996-conceptual-pacts; paper:metzing-brennan-2003-conceptual-pacts-broken; paper:cognitive-memory-hashtroudi-johnson-chrosniak-1989).

### 5. Accommodation exists, but similarity is not adaptation or relationship health

Function-word matching was associated with mutual interest and three-month romantic stability in modest observational samples. Turn-contingent Twitter accommodation remained after dyadic and topic controls, but it varied by feature and direction and was only weakly related to crude status proxies. A later conditional analysis found alignment mainly at the individual-word level and strongly dependent on discourse act (paper:ireland-et-al-2011-language-style-matching; paper:danescu-niculescu-mizil-et-al-2011-mark-my-words; paper:doyle-frank-2016-sources-linguistic-alignment).

Aggregate similarity is symmetric and cannot identify who adapted to whom. Directional accommodation requires temporally ordered replies against a dyadic baseline with topic, lexical item, and discourse-act controls, and even then it does not establish motive, closeness, approval, power, attraction, or relationship quality (paper:ireland-et-al-2011-language-style-matching; paper:danescu-niculescu-mizil-et-al-2011-mark-my-words; paper:doyle-frank-2016-sources-linguistic-alignment).

### 6. Audience-conditioned self-presentation is not simply true or false

Participants presented themselves more modestly to friends and more favorably to strangers, while relationship-incongruent presentation imposed a later recall cost. Online daters balanced favorable or aspirational presentation against credibility and anticipated offline verification. Audience-dependent differences can reflect learned presentation policy, aspiration, selective emphasis, politeness, or strategic credibility rather than deception (paper:tice-et-al-1995-when-modesty-prevails; paper:ellison-heino-gibbs-2006-managing-impressions).

Stories and identity meanings are also selected for an audience and purpose. Memories told for self-explanation carried meaning more often than memories told for entertainment, and later retellings can revise identity meaning without changing the underlying event. Message content must therefore preserve telling purpose, audience, and dated interpretation (paper:mclean-2005-meaning-telling; paper:jardmo-et-al-2023-repeated-narratives; paper:libby-eibach-2002-looking-back).

### 7. Disclosure depends on audience, channel, purpose, and other people

The same intimate content was judged differently in private and public conditions, with public intimate disclosure judged less appropriate. People also used audience zones, private channels, negotiated rules, approval requests, untagging, and removal requests to manage disclosures that affected others (paper:bazarova-2012-public-intimacy; paper:lampinen-et-al-2011-were-in-it-together).

Past disclosure, privacy-tool use, or stated concern does not establish current permission. Concerned users still disclosed substantial information while misunderstanding visibility, and audience composition, concern, settings, and disclosure did not behave as one stable latent privacy preference (paper:acquisti-gross-2006-imagined-communities; paper:vitak-2012-context-collapse-privacy).

Strong language models violated contextual privacy expectations in multi-party tasks, and reminders or reasoning prompts did not reliably prevent disclosure. Recipient, source, subject, purpose, and allowed transformation must be enforced outside the generator rather than inferred from a long prompt (paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide).

### 8. Third-party privacy is a first-class relationship constraint

An archive owner's access does not erase another participant's stake in their messages, identifiers, images, or relationship data. Interdependent-privacy salience materially reduced intended disclosure, and collaborative boundary-management studies show that unspoken expectations leave people vulnerable to disclosure by others (paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy; paper:lampinen-et-al-2011-were-in-it-together).

Relationship edges and innocuous behavior can jointly expose sensitive attributes. Friendship structure revealed information hidden from a focal profile, and graph-plus-behavior attacks outperformed either source alone. Relationship modeling must not become a route to infer or persist contact traits (paper:jernigan-2009-gaydar; paper:gong-2016-attribute-inference; paper:kosinski-2013-private-traits).

Public accessibility, collection permission, personalization, training, and replica generation are separate decisions. Permission signals can be mutable or conflicting, so ambiguity cannot be converted into blanket consent (paper:longpre-2024-consent-crisis; paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy).

### 9. Relationship state is temporal and versioned

Predictive models benefit when stable, slowly changing, and transient components are separated, and simply downweighting older evidence can discard durable signal. Immediate transition context can help prediction without becoming a durable preference or identity claim (paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics; paper:rendle-2010-fpmc).

Later retellings can add agency, relevance, or new identity meaning, while current self-concept can alter recall perspective. A relationship role, boundary, common-ground pact, or narrative meaning needs a validity interval and supersession history rather than destructive overwrite (paper:jardmo-et-al-2023-repeated-narratives; paper:libby-eibach-2002-looking-back; paper:timechara-2024).

## PLAUSIBLE Design Hypotheses

### Global identity versus scoped state

The following partition is an evidence-informed architecture, not a directly tested representation:

| State layer | Proposed contents | Scope rule and evidence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Owner-global identity** | Owner-confirmed facts and self-identifications; durable values; slowly updated cross-context trait distributions; global safety, consent, and non-representation rules | Promote only from explicit owner authority or repeated cross-context evidence. One relationship's style, disclosure, or role behavior cannot overwrite this layer (paper:psychometrics_epstein_1983; paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001; paper:bardi-schwartz-2003-values-behavior; paper:tice-et-al-1995-when-modesty-prevails). |
| **Relationship state** | Counterpart or group key; owner-confirmed relationship and role labels; shared history; relationship-specific self-schema hypotheses; boundary negotiations; current relationship phase | Store owner-side evidence without inferring the counterpart's traits. Keep conflict, closeness, obligation, and role as separable dimensions with temporal validity (paper:andersen-chen-2002-relational-self; paper:trefalt-2013-between-you-and-me; paper:mclean-thorne-2003-relationship-memories; paper:jernigan-2009-gaydar). |
| **Role state** | Active role, obligations, authority, task, institutional norms, and owner-approved role-specific behavior | A role can recur across relationships, but its activation and boundary consequences remain relationship- and situation-bound (paper:trefalt-2013-between-you-and-me; paper:postmes-spears-sakhel-degroot-2001-social-influence; paper:youssef-1993-childrens-linguistic-choices). |
| **Audience state** | Intended, imagined, permitted, and observed audiences; addressee, auditor, overhearer, and reference-group roles; confidence and mismatch flags | Audience state belongs to a message or episode, not permanently to a platform or thread (paper:bell-1984-audience-design; paper:litt-hargittai-2016-imagined-audience; paper:bernstein-et-al-2013-invisible-audience). |
| **Partner common ground** | Shared aliases, nicknames, shorthand, referents, repair history, establishment evidence, last confirmation, and expiry | Retrieve by relationship and episode; preserve old and new pacts without treating either as global language (paper:brennan-clark-1996-conceptual-pacts; paper:metzing-brennan-2003-conceptual-pacts-broken). |
| **Communication policy** | Observed register, code choice, presentation strategy, humor conventions, symmetric matching, and directional accommodation hypotheses | Keep observation separate from cause. Condition directional features on ordered replies, dyadic baseline, topic, lexical item, and discourse act (paper:tice-et-al-1995-when-modesty-prevails; paper:danescu-niculescu-mizil-et-al-2011-mark-my-words; paper:doyle-frank-2016-sources-linguistic-alignment). |
| **Disclosure boundary** | Information category, subject, source, audience, channel, purpose, allowed transformation, consent state, retention, expiry, and conflict status | Authorization is evaluated before retrieval and again before output. Relationship familiarity or prior disclosure cannot substitute for current permission (paper:bazarova-2012-public-intimacy; paper:lampinen-et-al-2011-were-in-it-together; paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide; paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy). |
| **Episode-local state** | Current task, recent interaction, temporary mood hypothesis, active goal, short-lived presentation choice, and request-local assumptions | Expire or supersede without promoting to global identity unless the owner confirms broader scope (paper:rendle-2010-fpmc; paper:psychometrics_sherman_2015; paper:carroll-russell-1996-face-context). |

### Scope and promotion rules

New behavior should first enter as a source-linked observation with relationship, audience, role, channel, time, and uncertainty. Repetition can strengthen a scoped pattern, but only explicit owner confirmation or evidence sampled across relevant contexts should propose a global update (paper:psychometrics_epstein_1983; paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001; paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics).

Contradictions across relationships should coexist until their scope is understood. The system should distinguish factually wrong, no longer true, true only here, presentation choice, do not use, and delete; owner controls should preview affected scopes and remain reversible because broad editing can reduce objective utility or conflate correction with novelty management (paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles; paper:bostandjiev-2012-tasteweights; paper:bakalov-2013-controllable-user-models).

A globally true fact is not globally disclosable. Disclosure authority is an independent relation among information, subject, source, recipient, purpose, channel, and time, and global safety or consent rules override any local style or rapport objective (paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide; paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy; paper:personalization-legitimizes-risks-2026).

### Context-collapse handling

When audiences with incompatible boundaries are present, a plausible safe policy is to segment the audience, narrow the content, ask the owner, or abstain. It is not supported to synthesize one average persona or assume that a larger mixed audience will automatically cause less disclosure (paper:marwick-boyd-2011-context-collapse; paper:vitak-2012-context-collapse-privacy; paper:litt-hargittai-2016-imagined-audience).

An audience mismatch should be explicit: intended versus imagined, imagined versus permitted, and permitted versus observed. The system should explain which mismatch triggered a confirmation gate rather than silently inferring intent from membership or engagement (paper:litt-hargittai-2016-imagined-audience; paper:bernstein-et-al-2013-invisible-audience; paper:acquisti-gross-2006-imagined-communities).

### Accommodation and generation

A plausible generator should select content and relationship-appropriate style separately. It may retrieve established common ground and weak accommodation cues, but it should never copy a contact's distinctive style, infer relationship quality, or let local matching override owner-global voice and safety rules (paper:brennan-clark-1996-conceptual-pacts; paper:ireland-et-al-2011-language-style-matching; paper:doyle-frank-2016-sources-linguistic-alignment; paper:persona-dialogue-li-2016).

The relationship model should expose whether a style feature is a stable owner pattern, a dyadic convention, a response to the immediately preceding turn, a role norm, or an unexplained correlation. This separation is motivated by state-trait, audience, and alignment evidence but has not been validated as a complete generative control scheme (paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001; paper:bell-1984-audience-design; paper:danescu-niculescu-mizil-et-al-2011-mark-my-words).

## SPECULATIVE Claims

- Relationship-scoped retrieval may improve owner-rated fidelity over a single global persona, but no included paper tests one consenting owner across natural long-term relationships with both systems (paper:persona-dialogue-li-2016; paper:sotopia-2024; paper:twinvoice-2026).
- Explicit intended, imagined, permitted, and observed audience objects may reduce context-collapse errors, but the corpus does not provide a deployed comparison against thread-membership-only routing (paper:litt-hargittai-2016-imagined-audience; paper:bernstein-et-al-2013-invisible-audience; paper:marwick-boyd-2011-context-collapse).
- Partner-scoped common-ground retrieval may improve naturalness, but stale, ironic, quoted, or ambiguous phrases could create false familiarity unless the system asks for repair (paper:brennan-clark-1996-conceptual-pacts; paper:metzing-brennan-2003-conceptual-pacts-broken).
- Discourse-act-conditioned accommodation may sound more authentic than unconditional style matching, but it could still caricature the owner or reproduce unhealthy interaction dynamics (paper:danescu-niculescu-mizil-et-al-2011-mark-my-words; paper:doyle-frank-2016-sources-linguistic-alignment; paper:ireland-et-al-2011-language-style-matching).
- Automated induction of roles, relationship phases, and disclosure policies from message histories may be useful for proposing owner questions, but it is not validated as an authority for durable state or contact modeling (paper:trefalt-2013-between-you-and-me; paper:persona-dialogue-arimoto-2024; paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy).
- A small set of high-value owner questions may close more consequential gaps than broad passive inference, but active-learning evidence comes from recommendation tasks rather than intimate social boundaries (paper:boutilier-2003-active-cf; paper:rashid-2002-getting-to-know-you; paper:harpale-2008-personalized-active-learning).

## Architecture Consequences

### Required state contract

Every owner-side claim should carry source event, source speaker, subject, relationship scope, role scope, audience scope, channel, observation time, valid time, confidence, owner-confirmation state, disclosure policy, and supersession links. This is a plausible synthesis of source-memory, relationship, temporal, and contextual-privacy evidence rather than a schema directly evaluated by one paper (paper:cognitive-memory-hashtroudi-johnson-chrosniak-1989; paper:andersen-chen-2002-relational-self; paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics; paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide).

Third-party material should remain a source-linked reference needed to understand the owner, not a durable profile of the other person. Relationship edges, quotes, forwarded content, inferred contact properties, and summaries must retain separate subject and permission scopes, and sensitive contact inference should be prohibited (paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy; paper:jernigan-2009-gaydar; paper:gong-2016-attribute-inference; paper:lampinen-et-al-2011-were-in-it-together).

### Scope-resolution order

1. **Apply hard consent, third-party, safety, and no-representation rules before retrieval.** A relationship-scoped style preference cannot override a global prohibition or another subject's boundary (paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide; paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy; paper:personalization-legitimizes-risks-2026).
2. **Construct a situation model.** Identify owner, counterpart or group, current role, task, channel, time, intended audience, permitted audience, and uncertainty before selecting personal evidence (paper:bell-1984-audience-design; paper:trefalt-2013-between-you-and-me; paper:litt-hargittai-2016-imagined-audience).
3. **Retrieve layers separately.** Fetch owner-global claims, relationship state, role state, audience state, common ground, and episode-local state as distinct candidate sets (paper:andersen-chen-2002-relational-self; paper:brennan-clark-1996-conceptual-pacts; paper:rendle-2010-fpmc).
4. **Resolve by authority, scope, time, and provenance.** Explicit current owner instructions outrank inferred patterns; a scoped observation outranks a global prior only inside its valid scope; conflicting versions remain visible (paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics; paper:jardmo-et-al-2023-repeated-narratives; paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles).
5. **Generate content and style as separate choices.** Common ground and accommodation can shape wording only after factual grounding and disclosure authorization are fixed (paper:persona-dialogue-li-2016; paper:brennan-clark-1996-conceptual-pacts; paper:doyle-frank-2016-sources-linguistic-alignment).
6. **Run post-retrieval guards.** Check recipient and purpose authorization, third-party exposure, contradiction, temporal leakage, unsupported relationship inference, and safety in the same context seen by the generator (paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide; paper:persona-dialogue-welleck-2019; paper:timechara-2024; paper:personalization-legitimizes-risks-2026).
7. **Expose a trace or abstain.** The trace should distinguish source evidence, scoped inference, owner rule, uncertainty, and generation choice; unresolved high-impact scope conflicts become owner questions (paper:bostandjiev-2012-tasteweights; paper:bakalov-2013-controllable-user-models; paper:linguistic-calibration-2022).

### Write and correction rules

- Generated text, predictions, and roleplay must never become owner evidence merely because the model produced them. Retrieval-driven updates can contaminate source attribution, so consolidation must preserve the old version, cue, new evidence, and resulting proposal (paper:cognitive-memory-hupbach-et-al-2007; paper:persona-dialogue-welleck-2019).
- A relationship-specific correction changes only that relationship unless the owner explicitly broadens it. A global correction should not automatically disclose the corrected fact to every audience (paper:tice-et-al-1995-when-modesty-prevails; paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide).
- Deletion and no-use controls must propagate through evidence, summaries, embeddings, common-ground entries, inferred policies, caches, and evaluation fixtures; graph-derived contact inferences must not survive deletion of their source edge (paper:jernigan-2009-gaydar; paper:gong-2016-attribute-inference).
- Owner interfaces should separate inspect, edit, override, suppress, expire, and delete, with a preview of affected relationships and behaviors and an auditable rollback path (paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles; paper:bostandjiev-2012-tasteweights; paper:bakalov-2013-controllable-user-models).

### Rejected alternatives

- Reject one flat persona vector as the canonical identity store because latent personalization can improve short dialogue while hiding evidence, confounds, temporal validity, and relationship scope (paper:persona-dialogue-li-2016; paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001; paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics).
- Reject contact profiles inferred from owner messages or the social graph; predictive capability is a privacy risk, not authorization or individual truth (paper:jernigan-2009-gaydar; paper:gong-2016-attribute-inference; paper:kosinski-2013-private-traits).
- Reject a single closeness, power, intimacy, or relationship-health score derived from language matching, message frequency, or disclosure volume (paper:ireland-et-al-2011-language-style-matching; paper:doyle-frank-2016-sources-linguistic-alignment; paper:bazarova-2012-public-intimacy; paper:psychometrics_montag_2015).
- Reject thread membership, public availability, prior disclosure, or archive possession as blanket consent for retrieval, training, summarization, or simulation (paper:acquisti-gross-2006-imagined-communities; paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy; paper:longpre-2024-consent-crisis).
- Reject prompt-only privacy and a compromise persona for mixed audiences; enforce authorization outside the model and segment, clarify, narrow, or abstain under context collapse (paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide; paper:marwick-boyd-2011-context-collapse; paper:vitak-2012-context-collapse-privacy).

## Evaluation Tests

Evaluation must use held-out relationships and future time slices, preserve model and prompt versions, include owner judgments, and report safety and fidelity as separate vectors. Mean resemblance or one automatic judge cannot establish relationship fidelity (paper:sotopia-2024; paper:synthetic-replacements-2024; paper:generative-agents-1000-2024).

| **Post-retrieval safety** | Replay benign relationship memories beside harmful or manipulative requests | Run the guard after retrieval, report personalization utility separately, and block cases where context improperly legitimizes harm (paper:personalization-legitimizes-risks-2026). |

## Highest-Value Owner Questions

Owner questions should be asked when the answer can change a consequential retrieval, disclosure, correction, or simulation decision, not merely because the model is uncertain. Query value must be balanced with answerability, burden, coverage, and disclosure cost; this transfer from recommender active learning remains a plausible design choice (paper:boutilier-2003-active-cf; paper:rashid-2002-getting-to-know-you; paper:harpale-2008-personalized-active-learning).

1. **Which facts, values, self-descriptions, and safety rules should remain global across relationships, and which are true only in particular roles or contexts?** This directly controls promotion from scoped evidence into owner-global identity (paper:psychometrics_epstein_1983; paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001; paper:andersen-chen-2002-relational-self).
2. **What role does this person or group play for you now, and when did that role begin or change?** Role labels and boundaries are relationship- and time-dependent rather than permanent contact traits (paper:trefalt-2013-between-you-and-me; paper:mclean-thorne-2003-relationship-memories; paper:timechara-2024).
3. **Which topics may be known, retrieved, summarized, or disclosed in this relationship, through which channel, and for what purpose?** Knowledge and permission must be represented separately (paper:bazarova-2012-public-intimacy; paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide).
4. **Who else might see, receive, or hear material from this chat, and should the system segment, narrow, or abstain for mixed audiences?** Thread membership does not reveal imagined or actual audience (paper:litt-hargittai-2016-imagined-audience; paper:bernstein-et-al-2013-invisible-audience; paper:marwick-boyd-2011-context-collapse).
5. **May information about the other person be retained, summarized, reused, or used only transiently for understanding this exchange?** The owner's archive access does not settle another subject's privacy stake (paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy; paper:lampinen-et-al-2011-were-in-it-together).
6. **Which nicknames, shorthand, shared references, or avoided terms are current with this person, and which are stale or private?** Partner-specific pacts can help retrieval but must be repairable and time-bounded (paper:brennan-clark-1996-conceptual-pacts; paper:metzing-brennan-2003-conceptual-pacts-broken).
7. **When your language changes with this person, is that an authentic preference, politeness, safety, obligation, role performance, humor, or accommodation you do not want copied?** Similar text can arise from distinct causes and should not become an imitation policy without owner authority (paper:tice-et-al-1995-when-modesty-prevails; paper:doyle-frank-2016-sources-linguistic-alignment; paper:psychometrics_sherman_2015).
8. **When two chats appear contradictory, is one statement wrong, outdated, relationship-specific, aspirational, playful, or a deliberate presentation choice?** Contradictions should be typed and scoped instead of resolved by frequency or favorability (paper:ellison-heino-gibbs-2006-managing-impressions; paper:jardmo-et-al-2023-repeated-narratives; paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles).
9. **What must the system never infer from your social graph or interaction patterns, including closeness, power, sexuality, health, politics, or another person's traits?** Predictability from traces is a privacy threat, not consent or individual truth (paper:jernigan-2009-gaydar; paper:gong-2016-attribute-inference; paper:kosinski-2013-private-traits).
10. **Which relationship-sensitive actions always require confirmation, and what should happen when you are unavailable: narrow, abstain, use a safe global default, or refuse?** Explicit fallback policy is safer than letting a generator infer permission or optimize rapport (paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide; paper:personalization-legitimizes-risks-2026).
