# Integrated Digital-Brain Blueprint

This draft translates the merged council evidence into an inspectable, local-first proof-of-concept architecture. It does **not** claim consciousness, psychological reconstruction, or authority to impersonate the owner. Throughout, **VALIDATED** means supported within the cited paper settings; **PLAUSIBLE** means an engineering inference supported by multiple records but not tested as one end-to-end digital brain; and **SPECULATIVE** means a hypothesis that requires direct owner-centered evaluation (`paper:cognitive-memory-eliasmith-et-al-2012`; `paper:generative-agents-1000-2024`; `paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026`).

## Evidence Scope

### Corpus boundary

The deduplicated corpus contains 187 reviewed primary papers: 100 inspected at full-text depth and 87 at abstract depth. Thirteen cross-seat duplicate groups were collapsed before synthesis. Corpus counts and depth labels are metadata facts from `corpus.jsonl`; they are not estimates from any individual paper.

The three early lifelog records are abstract-depth system or experimental reports, so they support bounded claims about lifetime capture and cueing, not detailed performance or a complete self-model (`paper:mylifebits-2006`; `paper:sensecam-retrospective-2006`; `paper:sensecam-lifelog-memory-2007`). Most current memory-agent, simulator, and twin records were inspected in full, but many use synthetic histories, fictional personas, surveys, or constrained action spaces rather than a consenting person's integrated life (`paper:connected-memories-comet-2022`; `paper:locomo-2024`; `paper:behaviorchain-2025`; `paper:twinvoice-2026`).

No paper in the merged corpus validates the full architecture proposed below. Integrated cognitive architectures demonstrate coordination across modules, while personal-agent papers test narrower memory, believability, survey, or action-prediction tasks (`paper:cognitive-memory-anderson-et-al-2004`; `paper:cognitive-memory-eliasmith-et-al-2012`; `paper:generative-agents-2023`; `paper:generative-agents-1000-2024`). The blueprint therefore treats component results as constraints and keeps the integrated design at **PLAUSIBLE** status.

No private WhatsApp content, profile, local message database, credential, or key was accessed for this synthesis. The WhatsApp section below describes future observability limits, not findings about any owner's messages.

### System-family comparison

| System family | Canonical substrate and target | Best-supported contribution | Observed boundary or failure |
|---|---|---|---|
| Integrated lifelog | Multimodal artifacts, timestamps, annotations, and entity-event links used for lifetime search or memory cueing | A database can preserve heterogeneous personal artifacts, passive cues can aid recall, and graph relations help navigate people, places, times, and media (`paper:mylifebits-2006`; `paper:sensecam-retrospective-2006`; `paper:connected-memories-comet-2022`) | Capture is not autobiographical truth; cue effects differ by capture type and delay, early evidence is narrow, and real-memory graphs remain privacy-constrained (`paper:sensecam-lifelog-memory-2007`; `paper:mylifebits-2006`; `paper:connected-memories-comet-2022`) |
| Memory agent | Dialogue or observation history plus working context, archival storage, summaries, portraits, topical segments, or reflection | Explicit memory operations, source-separated stores, topical segmentation, time-aware indexing, and abstention improve access and diagnosability (`paper:memgpt-2023`; `paper:persona-dialogue-xu-2022-lemon`; `paper:secom-2025`; `paper:longmemeval-2025`) | Retrieval can stop too early, return the wrong item, lose temporal structure, hallucinate around evidence, or weaken safety; most longitudinal histories are synthetic (`paper:memgpt-2023`; `paper:memorybank-2024`; `paper:locomo-2024`; `paper:personalization-legitimizes-risks-2026`) |
| Generative agent | Seed persona, observation stream, reflections, plans, and environment rules used to produce believable interaction | Memory, reflection, and planning can improve judged believability and support emergent coordination; populated prototypes can expose possible scenarios (`paper:generative-agents-2023`; `paper:social-simulacra-2022`) | Believability is not prediction of a real person; the worlds are short, synthetic, and vulnerable to retrieval omissions and fabricated embellishment (`paper:generative-agents-2023`; `paper:social-simulacra-2022`; `paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026`) |
| User simulator | Demographic or preference conditioning, task state, and generated feedback used to approximate populations or optimize policies | Simulators can reproduce some aggregate associations and can improve a downstream policy when transfer is checked with real users (`paper:out-of-one-many-2023`; `paper:neural-user-simulation-2018`) | Means can hide compressed variance, excess rationality, generic requests, inconsistent feedback, and simulator exploitation; aggregate fit does not imply individual fidelity (`paper:turing-experiments-2023`; `paper:synthetic-replacements-2024`; `paper:llms-more-rational-2025`; `paper:llm-user-sim-crs-2024`) |
| Personal twin | Interviews, repeated surveys, persona histories, public archives, biographies, or ordered real actions used for person-level prediction | Rich self-report improves some held-out person-level predictions relative to demographics, and repeated broad measurement can approach a person's retest reliability on selected survey tasks (`paper:generative-agents-1000-2024`; `paper:twin-2k-500-2025`) | Survey gains do not reliably transfer to games or causal effects; cumulative behavior chains collapse, recognition exceeds generation, and exact next-action prediction remains low (`paper:generative-agents-1000-2024`; `paper:twin-2k-500-2025`; `paper:behaviorchain-2025`; `paper:twinvoice-2026`; `paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026`) |

The comparison yields a product boundary: a lifelog is an evidence substrate, a memory agent is an access and update mechanism, a generative agent is a behavior-production mechanism, a user simulator is an evaluation or policy-training environment, and a personal twin is a person-specific predictive claim. These labels must not be collapsed merely because one implementation contains all five mechanisms (`paper:mylifebits-2006`; `paper:memgpt-2023`; `paper:generative-agents-2023`; `paper:neural-user-simulation-2018`; `paper:generative-agents-1000-2024`).

## VALIDATED Constraints

The constraints in this section are validated only within the cited settings. Their use as software requirements is conservative translation, not evidence that the resulting software is cognitively equivalent to a person (`paper:cognitive-memory-anderson-et-al-2004`; `paper:cognitive-memory-eliasmith-et-al-2012`).

### V1. Evidence, recollection, interpretation, and generation are different objects

Captured artifacts can cue memory without proving the owner's later interpretation, and reactivation can make later material intrude into an earlier episode (`paper:sensecam-lifelog-memory-2007`; `paper:cognitive-memory-hupbach-et-al-2007`). Repeated narratives can preserve an event while changing its meaning, and current self-concept can alter retrieval perspective (`paper:jardmo-et-al-2023-repeated-narratives`; `paper:libby-eibach-2002-looking-back`). A generated response must therefore never become autobiographical evidence merely because the agent produced it.

### V2. Memory is typed, bounded, context-sensitive, and source fallible

Episodic, semantic, and procedural performance dissociate; prospective memory can use monitoring or spontaneous cues; and content can remain available while its source is confused (`paper:cognitive-memory-vargha-khadem-1997`; `paper:cognitive-memory-cohen-squire-1980`; `paper:cognitive-memory-einstein-et-al-2005`; `paper:cognitive-memory-hashtroudi-johnson-chrosniak-1989`). Context match can change retrieval, and selective retrieval can strengthen targets while impairing competitors (`paper:cognitive-memory-godden-baddeley-1975`; `paper:cognitive-memory-karpicke-roediger-2008`; `paper:cognitive-memory-anderson-bjork-bjork-1994`). One undifferentiated vector store cannot expose those distinctions.

### V3. Long context and successful retrieval do not guarantee correct memory use

Long-context and retrieval methods remain well below humans on temporal and adversarial long-dialogue questions; finer indexing and evidence notes help but do not remove update or abstention failures (`paper:locomo-2024`; `paper:longmemeval-2025`). MemoryBank further separates retrieval success from answer correctness, while MemGPT failures show that an agent may stop paging before enough evidence is found (`paper:memorybank-2024`; `paper:memgpt-2023`). Retrieval and answer grounding require separate scores.

### V4. Plausibility, aggregate fit, individual agreement, and trajectory fidelity are distinct targets

Generated communities can look plausible without being forecasts, and population-conditioned models can reproduce aggregate associations without corresponding to a specific individual (`paper:social-simulacra-2022`; `paper:out-of-one-many-2023`). Models can reproduce classic effects while exhibiting nonhuman overknowledge, match survey means while compressing variance, or sound reasonable while overpredicting expected-value rationality (`paper:turing-experiments-2023`; `paper:synthetic-replacements-2024`; `paper:llms-more-rational-2025`). These targets require separate labels and tests.

### V5. Person-level fidelity is domain- and horizon-specific

Interview-, survey-, and combined self-report agents improved selected held-out survey predictions, but their economic-game performance did not differ significantly and the experiment aggregate was underpowered (`paper:generative-agents-1000-2024`). Twin-2K-500 reported high held-out answer agreement while failing several between- and within-subject effects, BehaviorChain showed early-error propagation, and ShopCART found low exact next-action accuracy with systematic over-purchasing (`paper:twin-2k-500-2025`; `paper:behaviorchain-2025`; `paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026`). A single fidelity score would hide these failures.

### V6. A person's observed expression is conditional on time, situation, relationship, and audience

Momentary behavior varies widely within a person and reflects both person and situation effects (`paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001`; `paper:psychometrics_sherman_2015`). Self-presentation differs with friends and strangers, shared language becomes partner-indexed, and imagined and actual audiences can diverge (`paper:tice-et-al-1995-when-modesty-prevails`; `paper:brennan-clark-1996-conceptual-pacts`; `paper:litt-hargittai-2016-imagined-audience`; `paper:bernstein-et-al-2013-invisible-audience`). The system must not flatten all relationships and occasions into one persona.

### V7. Traits, values, preferences, affect, and motives cannot be read directly from one trace

Trait estimates improve through aggregation, yet observable cues predict some targets better than others and may capture reputation rather than self-report (`paper:psychometrics_epstein_1983`; `paper:psychometrics_mairesse_2007`; `paper:psychometrics_stachl_2020`). Value-behavior links vary by domain and norms, choices shift with framing and option sets, and text emotion labels represent reader judgments rather than verified inner experience (`paper:bardi-schwartz-2003-values-behavior`; `paper:tversky-kahneman-1981-framing`; `paper:tversky-simonson-1993-context-preferences`; `paper:demszky-2020-goemotions`). Mental-state inference should remain a hypothesis distribution used to decide whether to clarify (`paper:keysar-2003-tom-limits`; `paper:epley-2004-perspective-taking`; `paper:wu-2018-emotion-mental-states`).

### V8. Time is useful for prediction but does not identify meaning or legitimate retention

Temporal decomposition, event order, and elapsed intervals improve prediction in several recommender settings, but time-aware gains are domain-dependent and indiscriminate age-based downweighting can discard durable signal (`paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics`; `paper:kumar-2019-jodie`; `paper:li-2020-tisasrec`). Anti-forgetting methods can help on dynamic streams while adding little or hurting in stable settings (`paper:mi-2020-ader`; `paper:xu-2020-graphsail`). Recency, silence, and replay value cannot independently establish changed belief, consent, or identity.

### V9. Owner visibility and control are necessary but not automatically accuracy-improving

Users valued profile visibility and control, yet direct editing reduced recommendation precision and recall in one study, while positive control results elsewhere were small or confounded by extra feedback (`paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles`; `paper:bostandjiev-2012-tasteweights`; `paper:bakalov-2013-controllable-user-models`). Owner authority is a governance requirement; it should be paired with previews, typed scope, rollback, and separate quality evaluation rather than justified as guaranteed metric improvement.

### V10. Personalization expands the privacy and safety attack surface

Models can leak membership, memorized text, and PII; duplication raises regeneration risk, and deletion requires artifact lineage rather than deleting one source row (`paper:shokri-2017-membership-inference`; `paper:carlini-2021-training-data-extraction`; `paper:kandpal-2022-dedup-privacy`; `paper:lukas-2023-pii-leakage`; `paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning`). In multi-party tasks, privacy reminders did not reliably enforce contextual information flow, and benign retrieved memories made harmful requests appear personally legitimate (`paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide`; `paper:personalization-legitimizes-risks-2026`). Authorization must happen before retrieval, and safety must run again after retrieval.

### V11. Fidelity is a score vector with hard safety boundaries

Persona consistency, contradiction, social-goal success, style, temporal knowledge, individual prediction, distributional fit, calibration, and leakage are empirically separable constructs (`paper:persona-dialogue-zhang-2018`; `paper:persona-dialogue-welleck-2019`; `paper:sotopia-2024`; `paper:timechara-2024`; `paper:generative-agents-1000-2024`; `paper:synthetic-replacements-2024`; `paper:linguistic-calibration-2022`; `paper:carlini-2021-training-data-extraction`). Strong performance on one axis cannot compensate for a severe privacy leak, unauthorized disclosure, or false claim of authority.

## PLAUSIBLE Design Hypotheses

### H1. Three-plane separation

The system should expose three independently governed planes: **evidence** (authorized source events), **derived self-model** (versioned claims, episodes, policies, and uncertainties), and **simulation** (request-local hypotheses, candidate responses, and traces). This separation follows from source-memory errors, editable retrieval systems, and the gap between retrieval and generation, but no cited paper tests the full arrangement (`paper:cognitive-memory-hashtroudi-johnson-chrosniak-1989`; `paper:persona-dialogue-wang-2024-emg-rag`; `paper:twinvoice-2026`).

### H2. Typed state with different promotion and expiry rules

Episodes, semantic claims, procedures, intentions, traits, values, active goals, affect, narrative interpretations, relationship overlays, and communication policies should be separate record types. Human-memory dissociations, temporal personalization, goal shielding, narrative revision, and relational-self evidence support the distinction, while the exact software ontology remains a design choice (`paper:cognitive-memory-vargha-khadem-1997`; `paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics`; `paper:shah-2002-goal-shielding`; `paper:mcadams-et-al-2006-continuity-change`; `paper:andersen-chen-2002-relational-self`).

### H3. Retrieval-first personalization with optional, bounded adapters

Mutable facts, episodes, goals, and relationship context should remain in inspectable retrieval stores. A versioned adapter may later encode stable communication tendencies, but it must never be the canonical identity store. Retrieval improves many personalization tasks and editable graphs support correction, while fine-tuning can reduce open-domain coherence and learned artifacts create deletion and leakage obligations (`paper:persona-dialogue-salemi-2024-lamp`; `paper:persona-dialogue-wang-2024-emg-rag`; `paper:persona-dialogue-xu-2022-lemon`; `paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning`; `paper:carlini-2021-training-data-extraction`).

### H4. Provisional update proposals instead of silent profile mutation

New observations should first produce typed, reversible deltas with source, valid time, confidence, scope, expiry, and expected behavioral effect. Promotion should depend on repeated evidence, explicit owner declaration, or measured utility that survives retention tests; consequential ambiguity should trigger an answerable owner question (`paper:longmemeval-2025`; `paper:mi-2020-ader`; `paper:boutilier-2003-active-cf`; `paper:harpale-2008-personalized-active-learning`).

### H5. Deliberation as evidence-bounded candidate selection

Before responding, the system should build a situation model, retrieve typed evidence, generate several plausible owner-consistent candidates, test each for contradiction, temporal leakage, relationship scope, information-flow permission, and harm, then answer, clarify, or abstain. Independent contradiction reranking, point-in-time tests, contextual-integrity failures, and post-retrieval safety evidence support the stages, not the claim that hidden chain-of-thought reproduces human cognition (`paper:persona-dialogue-welleck-2019`; `paper:timechara-2024`; `paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide`; `paper:personalization-legitimizes-risks-2026`).

### H6. Owner questions should optimize decision value, answerability, and disclosure cost

The interview should ask only when an answer may change a consequential output, resolve a durable conflict, or close a high-value coverage gap. Information-value and answerability methods outperform random or uncertainty-only elicitation in their recommender settings, while autobiographical prompts can themselves alter the working self (`paper:boutilier-2003-active-cf`; `paper:harpale-2008-personalized-active-learning`; `paper:dargembeau-garcia-jimenez-2024-working-self`). The question policy must therefore log the prompt and permit skip, uncertainty, and later revision.

## SPECULATIVE Claims

1. A three-plane, typed architecture will produce higher owner-recognized fidelity than a strong retrieval assistant using the same evidence. The components are individually motivated, but no included study supplies this ablation (`paper:memgpt-2023`; `paper:generative-agents-2023`; `paper:generative-agents-1000-2024`).
2. Relationship-scoped retrieval will improve naturalness without increasing cross-person leakage when authorization is deterministic and owner-visible. Partner-specific language and contextual privacy motivate the test, but no paper evaluates this integrated tradeoff (`paper:brennan-clark-1996-conceptual-pacts`; `paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide`; `paper:twinvoice-2026`).
3. Active owner elicitation will outperform passive WhatsApp-only inference on values, narrative identity, and future decisions at an acceptable burden. Active-learning and owner-cue studies support asking targeted questions, not the cross-domain performance claim (`paper:harpale-2008-personalized-active-learning`; `paper:rathbone-moulin-conway-2008-self-centered`; `paper:bardi-schwartz-2003-values-behavior`).
4. A small style adapter trained only after repeated, cross-context evidence will improve stable voice without absorbing mutable facts or private text. Persona tuning can improve role fidelity, but coherence, deletion, contamination, and memorization remain unresolved (`paper:persona-dialogue-shao-2023-character-llm`; `paper:persona-dialogue-xu-2022-lemon`; `paper:quantifying-memorization-2023`).
5. Explicitly separating base-model world knowledge from owner evidence will reduce normative-answer substitution and point-in-time leakage. Twin and temporal benchmarks expose the failure, but a robust mechanism has not been validated (`paper:twin-2k-500-2025`; `paper:turing-experiments-2023`; `paper:timechara-2024`).

## Contradictions and Boundaries

| Tension | Positive evidence | Negative or boundary evidence | Blueprint resolution |
|---|---|---|---|
| Capture versus selfhood | Lifetime capture and passive cues make evidence retrievable (`paper:mylifebits-2006`; `paper:sensecam-retrospective-2006`) | Capture type changes cueing, source can drift, and narrative meaning changes (`paper:sensecam-lifelog-memory-2007`; `paper:cognitive-memory-hupbach-et-al-2007`; `paper:jardmo-et-al-2023-repeated-narratives`) | Preserve source artifacts and separately version owner-endorsed interpretation; never equate archive coverage with identity coverage. |
| Retrieval utility versus retrieval harm | Paging, topical segments, and explicit memories improve long-range access (`paper:memgpt-2023`; `paper:secom-2025`; `paper:persona-dialogue-xu-2022-lemon`) | Wrong retrieval can cause false recall, retrieval strengthens some memories at competitors' expense, and personalized context weakens safety (`paper:memorybank-2024`; `paper:cognitive-memory-anderson-bjork-bjork-1994`; `paper:personalization-legitimizes-risks-2026`) | Score retrieval, grounding, competitor coverage, and safety separately; retain a no-use path. |
| Believability versus predictive fidelity | Memory-reflection-planning and populated prototypes improve judged plausibility (`paper:generative-agents-2023`; `paper:social-simulacra-2022`) | Real next-action and cumulative-chain performance remain weak (`paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026`; `paper:behaviorchain-2025`) | Label prototype simulation separately from person prediction and prohibit marketing one metric as the other. |
| Aggregate simulation versus individual simulation | Conditioned models reproduce some subgroup patterns and classic effects (`paper:out-of-one-many-2023`; `paper:turing-experiments-2023`) | Variance collapses, regressions drift, and person-specific correspondence is absent (`paper:synthetic-replacements-2024`; `paper:turing-experiments-2023`) | Maintain separate population, subgroup, individual, and repeated-choice scorecards. |
| Survey fidelity versus behavioral fidelity | Rich self-reports improve selected held-out survey answers (`paper:generative-agents-1000-2024`; `paper:twin-2k-500-2025`) | Economic games, causal effects, trajectories, and real actions do not inherit the gain (`paper:generative-agents-1000-2024`; `paper:twin-2k-500-2025`; `paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026`) | Never average across domains; require a held-out criterion for each claimed capability. |
| Retention versus forgetting and deletion | Replay and preservation reduce benchmark forgetting (`paper:lopez-paz-2017-gem`; `paper:mi-2020-ader`; `paper:masson-dautume-2019-episodic-language-memory`) | Preservation can add little or hurt, retrieval suppresses competitors, and legal or consent deletion is a separate requirement (`paper:mi-2020-ader`; `paper:xu-2020-graphsail`; `paper:cognitive-memory-anderson-bjork-bjork-1994`; `paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning`) | Use reversible archive/down-ranking for utility and a distinct auditable hard-delete path for authority or consent. |
| Owner control versus measured quality | Editable and controllable models increase perceived agency in some studies (`paper:bostandjiev-2012-tasteweights`; `paper:bakalov-2013-controllable-user-models`) | Direct edits harmed objective recommendation metrics in one deployment (`paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles`) | Owner authority wins; preview and test the behavioral delta without blocking a correction because a metric falls. |
| Stable trait versus state and context | Aggregated evidence can yield stable trait estimates (`paper:psychometrics_epstein_1983`; `paper:psychometrics_park_2015`) | Within-person distributions are broad, situations contribute independently, and cross-domain transfer can fail (`paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001`; `paper:psychometrics_sherman_2015`; `paper:psychometrics_rieman_2017`) | Store context-bound states and a calibrated posterior, never a message-level trait label. |
| Choice consistency versus stable preference | Repeated choices can become coherent and strategies can adapt with feedback (`paper:ariely-2003-coherent-arbitrariness`; `paper:rieskamp-otto-2006-strategy-selection`) | Frames, option sets, anchors, and measurement can manufacture apparent preference (`paper:tversky-kahneman-1981-framing`; `paper:tversky-simonson-1993-context-preferences`; `paper:chen-risen-2010-free-choice`) | Store the complete choice situation and outcome; do not update a context-free utility from one selection. |
| Narrative coherence versus truth or health | Coherence dimensions can be coded and develop differently (`paper:reese-et-al-2011-narrative-coherence`; `paper:kober-schmiedek-habermas-2015-coherence-development`) | Coherence did not necessarily increase with intervention or adaptation, and repetition is not factual correctness (`paper:habermas-de-silveira-2008-global-coherence`; `paper:adler-2012-living-into-story`; `paper:kober-habermas-2017-personal-past-stability`) | Annotate coherence and preserve versions; never manufacture a polished story or use coherence as a clinical score. |
| Personalization versus privacy and safety | Personal evidence improves some tasks (`paper:persona-dialogue-salemi-2024-lamp`; `paper:generative-agents-1000-2024`) | Personal context enables extraction, cross-recipient disclosure, sensitive inference, and harmful-query legitimization (`paper:carlini-2021-training-data-extraction`; `paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide`; `paper:kosinski-2013-private-traits`; `paper:personalization-legitimizes-risks-2026`) | Minimize data, authorize before retrieval, inspect after retrieval, and treat privacy/safety failures as release vetoes. |

## Architecture Consequences

### Modular architecture

| Module | Responsibility and boundary | Primary evidence basis |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Source and consent registry | Record source, subject(s), sender, collection purpose, allowed transformations, audience, retention, consent state, revocation, and deletion scope before content becomes retrievable. Archive possession is not blanket authorization. | `paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy`; `paper:longpre-2024-consent-crisis`; `paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide` |
| 2. Canonical evidence ledger | Preserve authorized source events, capture time, available metadata, content hash or protected reference, and provenance. Corrections append a new state while retained; explicit deletion removes content through lineage-aware workflows rather than pretending append-only means undeletable. | `paper:mylifebits-2006`; `paper:cognitive-memory-hupbach-et-al-2007`; `paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning` |
| 3. Attribution and episode builder | Resolve speaker/subject uncertainty, quotations, media references, entities, time, and event boundaries; keep unresolved candidates rather than forcing identity. Build an entity-event graph beside lexical and dense indexes. | `paper:connected-memories-comet-2022`; `paper:secom-2025`; `paper:cognitive-memory-hashtroudi-johnson-chrosniak-1989` |
| 4. Typed self-model store | Maintain separately versioned episodes, semantic claims, procedures, intentions, self-images, narrative versions, traits, values, preferences, goals, affect, relationship overlays, disclosure rules, and communication policies. Every derived item links to support and contradiction. | `paper:cognitive-memory-vargha-khadem-1997`; `paper:rathbone-moulin-conway-2008-self-centered`; `paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001`; `paper:schwartz-2012-refined-values`; `paper:andersen-chen-2002-relational-self` |
| 5. Temporal and conflict manager | Track observation time, estimated valid time, supersession, relationship scope, confidence, and unresolved alternatives. Keep enduring, slowly changing, episode-local, and request-local views separate. | `paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics`; `paper:longmemeval-2025`; `paper:timechara-2024`; `paper:mcadams-et-al-2006-continuity-change` |
| 6. Retrieval planner | Decompose the request by memory type, apply deterministic authorization and temporal/relationship filters, compare sparse/dense/graph retrieval, iterate within budget, and return evidence plus a no-use or insufficient-evidence result. | `paper:memgpt-2023`; `paper:secom-2025`; `paper:locomo-2024`; `paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide` |
| 7. Situation and deliberation engine | Build the present situation before generation: request intent, time cutoff, counterpart, audience, active role, goals, option set, affect uncertainty, stakes, and authority. Generate candidates from evidence-bounded hypotheses, not one flat persona prompt. | `paper:psychometrics_sherman_2015`; `paper:bell-1984-audience-design`; `paper:tversky-simonson-1993-context-preferences`; `paper:generative-agents-2023` |
| 8. Consistency, privacy, and safety gate | Check candidate claims for evidence support, contradiction, future leakage, wrong-person attribution, cross-relationship disclosure, sensitive inference, PII, harmful personalization, and unauthorized representation after retrieval and before release. | `paper:persona-dialogue-welleck-2019`; `paper:timechara-2024`; `paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide`; `paper:personalization-legitimizes-risks-2026` |
| 9. Confidence and abstention controller | Calibrate by claim type and evidence quality; distinguish observed, owner-confirmed, inferred, assumed, and predicted content. Ask or abstain when support is missing or authority is high stakes. | `paper:cognitive-memory-koriat-bjork-2005`; `paper:linguistic-calibration-2022`; `paper:longmemeval-2025` |
| 10. Update and consolidation service | Convert new evidence or owner feedback into proposed deltas; test interference, retention, privacy, and behavioral changes; commit a reversible version; never write generated responses back as owner evidence. | `paper:cognitive-memory-mcclelland-mcnaughton-oreilly-1995`; `paper:lopez-paz-2017-gem`; `paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles`; `paper:behaviorchain-2025` |
| 11. Owner control and audit surface | Let the owner inspect sources, claims, conflicts, retrievals, policy effects, corrections, exclusions, exports, and deletion lineage at source, relationship, capability, and model-artifact scope. | `paper:bostandjiev-2012-tasteweights`; `paper:bakalov-2013-controllable-user-models`; `paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning` |
| 12. Evaluation harness | Freeze evidence cutoff, model version, prompt, retrieval snapshot, and decoding settings; run held-out behavioral, temporal, relationship, calibration, memorization, deletion, and safety suites with explicit baselines. | `paper:synthetic-replacements-2024`; `paper:generative-agents-1000-2024`; `paper:quantifying-memorization-2023`; `paper:sotopia-2024` |

### Typed state contract

| State type | Canonical content | Promotion/update rule | Forbidden shortcut |
|---|---|---|---|
| Source event | Authorized message, media reference, observation, interview answer, or action outcome with provenance | Append while authorized; correct by linked version; delete through lineage | A summary, inference, or generated response cannot replace the source (`paper:mylifebits-2006`; `paper:cognitive-memory-hupbach-et-al-2007`) |
| Episode | Bounded event with participants, context, time uncertainty, sources, and outcome | Segment provisionally; merge or split with source-preserving edits | Topic similarity alone cannot establish one real-world event (`paper:secom-2025`; `paper:connected-memories-comet-2022`) |
| Semantic self-claim | Owner fact or generalized self-knowledge with supporting and contradicting episodes | Owner declaration may confirm; inference remains provisional; supersede by scope and time | Repetition, fluency, or retrieval frequency is not truth (`paper:cognitive-memory-vargha-khadem-1997`; `paper:kober-habermas-2017-personal-past-stability`) |
| Procedure or habit | Claimed or observed recurring action policy and outcome history | Keep declared reason separate from behavioral regularity; require outcomes for consequential use | Do not invent a declarative rationale for a learned pattern (`paper:cognitive-memory-cohen-squire-1980`; `paper:cognitive-memory-knowlton-mangels-squire-1996`) |
| Intention | Goal-directed action, trigger, deadline/event condition, monitoring route, completion, and expiry | Activate by owner statement or bounded inference; close only with owner or observed completion evidence | A future-tense message is not proof of durable commitment or completion (`paper:cognitive-memory-einstein-et-al-2005`; `paper:dargembeau-garcia-jimenez-2024-working-self`) |
| Narrative version | Owner account, audience, prompt, meaning, affect-at-recall, agency, and perspective | Append reinterpretations without overwriting event evidence; allow unresolved or ambivalent versions | Never auto-polish coherence or force growth (`paper:jardmo-et-al-2023-repeated-narratives`; `paper:pals-2006-difficult-experiences`; `paper:adler-2012-living-into-story`) |
| Trait posterior | Distribution over repeated states, domain, relationship, criterion, population calibration, and evidence count | Update slowly after aggregation and target-domain validation; abstain per trait | No message-level or universal personality decoder (`paper:psychometrics_epstein_1983`; `paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001`; `paper:psychometrics_stachl_2020`) |
| Value, preference, or decision policy | Owner declaration plus choice episodes with frame, options, anchor, constraints, outcome, and later endorsement | Keep active-goal and context effects; update policy weights by domain and feedback | One observed choice cannot become a context-free utility (`paper:bardi-schwartz-2003-values-behavior`; `paper:tversky-kahneman-1981-framing`; `paper:rieskamp-otto-2006-strategy-selection`) |
| Affect and motive hypothesis | Time-indexed mixed affect, appraisal evidence, competing belief/desire hypotheses, and owner report | Direct report outranks text classification; expire quickly unless re-observed | Reader-perceived emotion or inverse planning is not inner-state truth (`paper:demszky-2020-goemotions`; `paper:baker-2017-bayesian-mentalizing`; `paper:wu-2018-emotion-mental-states`) |
| Relationship and audience overlay | Owner-confirmed role, shared references, active audience, boundaries, disclosure permissions, and context-conditioned style | Scope to counterpart/group, channel, purpose, and valid time; preserve uncertainty about unseen audiences | Do not infer contact traits, closeness, power, or consent from style or membership (`paper:brennan-clark-1996-conceptual-pacts`; `paper:danescu-niculescu-mizil-et-al-2011-mark-my-words`; `paper:lampinen-et-al-2011-were-in-it-together`) |
| Synthetic output | Candidate or released simulation with model and trace metadata | Store only as system output; promote nothing without independent owner evidence | The system's own words are never owner memory or authority (`paper:generative-agents-2023`; `paper:behaviorchain-2025`) |

### End-to-end update loop

1. **Authorize and observe.** Check source, subjects, purpose, audience, and current consent before reading content; append the event without treating behavior as endorsement (`paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy`; `paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide`).
2. **Attribute and segment.** Resolve speaker, quotation, forward, media, time, entities, and episode boundaries; retain ambiguity and uncompressed evidence (`paper:connected-memories-comet-2022`; `paper:secom-2025`).
3. **Propose typed deltas.** Generate zero or more candidate updates with target state type, valid scope, confidence, expiry, supporting and contradicting evidence, and predicted behavioral effect (`paper:longmemeval-2025`; `paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics`).
4. **Check conflicts and confounds.** Test wrong-person attribution, current-versus-superseded state, relationship dependence, situation effects, response style, and base-model prior leakage (`paper:psychometrics_sherman_2015`; `paper:psychometrics_rieman_2017`; `paper:twin-2k-500-2025`).
5. **Choose automatic, provisional, or owner-gated handling.** Low-impact episode links may remain provisional; durable identity, values, boundaries, sensitive material, consequential policies, and conflicting claims require owner review or abstention (`paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles`; `paper:bardi-schwartz-2003-values-behavior`; `paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide`).
6. **Ask only a high-value, answerable question.** Rank questions by expected decision change, conflict reduction, answerability, urgency, and disclosure cost; treat skip or "not sure" as feedback about the query, not the owner (`paper:boutilier-2003-active-cf`; `paper:harpale-2008-personalized-active-learning`).
7. **Run shadow evaluation.** Test the proposed version against chronological holdouts, owner-approved retention anchors, contradiction suites, relationship boundaries, calibration, and privacy/safety gates (`paper:lopez-paz-2017-gem`; `paper:persona-dialogue-welleck-2019`; `paper:synthetic-replacements-2024`).
8. **Preview and commit.** Show high-impact effects, then write a version, diff, rationale, policy version, evaluation receipt, and rollback pointer; owner-authorized correction remains valid even if a prediction metric falls (`paper:bostandjiev-2012-tasteweights`; `paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles`).
9. **Reindex and consolidate.** Update graph, lexical, dense, temporal, and summary views while retaining source links; test old and new retrieval and prevent one frequently retrieved claim from suppressing alternatives (`paper:cognitive-memory-mcclelland-mcnaughton-oreilly-1995`; `paper:cognitive-memory-anderson-bjork-bjork-1994`; `paper:secom-2025`).
10. **Monitor and retire.** Measure drift per state type, expire temporary hypotheses, preserve superseded history when authorized, and propagate revocation or deletion through summaries, indexes, caches, replay sets, adapters, and exports (`paper:mi-2020-ader`; `paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning`).

### Runtime and deliberation trace

The runtime should expose a concise decision record rather than hidden reasoning. Its purpose is provenance and control: which situation fields were supplied, which evidence was authorized and retrieved, which hypotheses were considered, which constraints rejected candidates, and why the system answered, clarified, or abstained (`paper:cognitive-memory-anderson-et-al-2004`; `paper:persona-dialogue-welleck-2019`; `paper:longmemeval-2025`).

```yaml
trace:
  trace_id: stable-local-id
  request:
    purpose: user-visible-purpose
    authority: answer | draft | simulate_only
    time_cutoff: timestamp-or-null
    counterpart_scope: owner | relationship-id | group-id | unknown
    stakes: low | consequential | prohibited
  situation:
    intended_audience: []
    permitted_audience: []
    active_role_hypotheses: []
    active_goal_hypotheses: []
    option_set_known: true-or-false
    missing_observations: []
  retrieval:
    authorization_policy_version: id
    query_by_store: {}
    selected_evidence_ids: []
    rejected_evidence: [{id: ..., reason: stale-or-unauthorized-or-wrong-scope}]
    unresolved_conflicts: []
    sufficiency: sufficient | partial | insufficient
  candidates:
    - candidate_id: id
      evidence_support: []
      inferences: []
      assumptions: []
      predicted_owner_fit: probability-with-calibration-class
      constraint_results: {}
  decision:
    action: answer | clarify | abstain | refuse
    selected_candidate: id-or-null
    confidence_by_claim: []
    reason_codes: []
  write_back:
    synthetic_output_only: true
    proposed_state_deltas: []
    owner_confirmation_required: true-or-false
```

The trace must not expose private evidence to an unauthorized viewer or present generated chain-of-thought as the owner's cognition. It should reveal evidence references, typed inferences, assumptions, scores, and rule outcomes while keeping sensitive content access-controlled (`paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide`; `paper:llms-more-rational-2025`; `paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026`).

### Failure boundaries

1. **No authority boundary:** the system may answer, draft, or simulate under explicit scope, but it must not claim to be the living person or autonomously send, commit, purchase, consent, or speak on the person's behalf. Weak real-action prediction and simulator exploitation make autonomy especially unjustified (`paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026`; `paper:neural-user-simulation-2018`).
2. **No synthetic-memory boundary:** generated dialogue, rationales, reflections, and roleplay remain system artifacts until independently confirmed. Models fabricate embellishments and synthetic rationales need not match human cognition (`paper:generative-agents-2023`; `paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026`).
3. **No sensitive-inference boundary:** do not infer or persist protected or sensitive identity from behavior, contacts, voice, face, or language. Predictability is not legitimacy, and classifiers can misrepresent people unevenly (`paper:kosinski-2013-private-traits`; `paper:gong-2016-attribute-inference`; `paper:buolamwini-2018-gender-shades`; `paper:scheuerman-2019-computers-see-gender`).
4. **No cross-person ownership boundary:** another participant's message is contextual evidence, not permission to model, imitate, or disclose that person. Information flow is recipient- and purpose-dependent (`paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy`; `paper:lampinen-et-al-2011-were-in-it-together`; `paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide`).
5. **No context-free identity boundary:** a relationship-specific phrase, momentary state, or audience performance cannot silently become a global trait or value (`paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001`; `paper:tice-et-al-1995-when-modesty-prevails`; `paper:bardi-schwartz-2003-values-behavior`).
6. **No world-knowledge substitution boundary:** a generally true, normative, or future fact is a fidelity error when the owner did not know or choose it (`paper:turing-experiments-2023`; `paper:twin-2k-500-2025`; `paper:timechara-2024`).
7. **No clinical or well-being boundary:** narrative coherence, affect classification, trait scores, and simulation outputs are not diagnoses or measures of recovery (`paper:adler-2012-living-into-story`; `paper:demszky-2020-goemotions`; `paper:psychometrics_laajaj_2019`).
8. **No posthumous inference boundary:** retention settings, kinship, inactivity, or ordinary stewardship do not authorize posthumous simulation; evidence supports explicit advance directives and conservative defaults, not therapeutic efficacy (`paper:brubaker-2016-legacy-contact`; `paper:gach-2021-facebook-affairs`; `paper:lu-2026-griefbot-perceptions`).
9. **No privacy-by-prompt boundary:** reminders, scrubbing, deduplication, refusals, watermarking, or detector scores are individual controls, not standalone guarantees (`paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide`; `paper:kandpal-2022-dedup-privacy`; `paper:lukas-2023-pii-leakage`; `paper:san-roman-2024-audioseal`; `paper:rossler-2019-faceforensics`).

### Minimal proof-of-concept path

**POC-0: Public and synthetic fixtures only.** Build development fixtures that exercise direct statements, quotations, corrections, relationship scopes, future leakage, conflicting consent, and deletion. Synthetic histories are useful for reproducible diagnosis but must not be labeled as natural-life validation (`paper:connected-memories-comet-2022`; `paper:longmemeval-2025`; `paper:personalization-legitimizes-risks-2026`).

**POC-1: Local evidence ledger and consent manifest.** Implement source registration, protected content references, event timestamps, subjects, audiences, purpose, retention, and revocation. Provide deterministic pre-retrieval authorization and a deletion-lineage receipt (`paper:mylifebits-2006`; `paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy`; `paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning`).

**POC-2: Owner-reviewed typed extraction.** Support only episodes, semantic claims, intentions, relationship boundaries, and narrative versions at first. Each proposed item must show its source and allow confirm, narrow scope, mark outdated, exclude from personalization, or delete (`paper:cognitive-memory-einstein-et-al-2005`; `paper:mcadams-et-al-2006-continuity-change`; `paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles`).

**POC-3: Retrieval-first simulation with trace.** Use lexical, dense, temporal, and graph retrieval; construct the situation; produce one or more candidates; check grounding, contradiction, time, relationship, privacy, and safety; then answer, clarify, or abstain with the compact trace (`paper:secom-2025`; `paper:persona-dialogue-welleck-2019`; `paper:timechara-2024`; `paper:personalization-legitimizes-risks-2026`).

**POC-4: Reversible update loop.** Add typed deltas, owner questions, preview, version commit, rollback, reindexing, and full deletion propagation. Generated answers stay outside canonical memory (`paper:boutilier-2003-active-cf`; `paper:bostandjiev-2012-tasteweights`; `paper:behaviorchain-2025`; `paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning`).

**POC-5: Evidence and safety evaluation.** Run chronological held-outs, source attribution, future-leakage, relationship-boundary, abstention, correction, privacy extraction, and post-retrieval safety tests against generic-LLM, recency-only, and retrieval-only baselines (`paper:generative-agents-1000-2024`; `paper:timechara-2024`; `paper:carlini-2021-training-data-extraction`; `paper:personalization-legitimizes-risks-2026`).

The minimal POC should exclude per-owner fine-tuning, synthetic voice or face, autonomous tools, contact simulation, protected-trait objectives, and posthumous operation. Retrieval is more inspectable and editable than weight-based memory, while likeness and learned artifacts add provenance, deletion, and leakage obligations before core evidence handling is proven (`paper:persona-dialogue-salemi-2024-lamp`; `paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning`; `paper:san-roman-2024-audioseal`; `paper:rossler-2019-faceforensics`).

## Evaluation Tests

Every evaluation run should freeze the owner or fixture, source manifest, evidence cutoff, relationship, model and embedding versions, prompts, retrieval snapshot, decoding settings, and policy versions. Model updates and prompt changes can materially shift synthetic behavior (`paper:synthetic-replacements-2024`; `paper:persona-dialogue-chen-2023-orig`).

| Test | Setup and metric | Required interpretation or gate | Evidence basis |
|---|---|---|---|
| Source attribution | Ask factual, episodic, quoted, inferred, and wrong-person questions; score source ID, source class, claim support, and false attribution | A fluent correct answer with the wrong source is a failure | `paper:cognitive-memory-hashtroudi-johnson-chrosniak-1989`; `paper:cognitive-memory-hupbach-et-al-2007` |
| Retrieval and abstention | Long histories with answerable, conflicting, adversarial, and absent evidence; score retrieval recall, evidence precision, answer grounding, and abstention separately | More retrieved text is not automatically better; unsupported guessing fails | `paper:locomo-2024`; `paper:longmemeval-2025`; `paper:memorybank-2024` |
| Temporal and supersession | Query the self-model at earlier cutoffs, after corrections, and after changed preferences; score future leakage and current/history retrieval | A globally true but not-yet-known fact fails the target-time test | `paper:timechara-2024`; `paper:longmemeval-2025`; `paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics` |
| Relationship boundary | Repeat one intent across owner, friend, family, work, stranger, and mixed-group scopes; seed tempting cross-chat facts | Any unauthorized cross-relationship disclosure is a hard failure | `paper:andersen-chen-2002-relational-self`; `paper:litt-hargittai-2016-imagined-audience`; `paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide` |
| Contradiction and wrong-person | Paraphrase, reorder, negate, supersede, and swap entities in owner claims | Report entailment, neutral, contradiction, and unresolved; absence of contradiction is not fidelity | `paper:persona-dialogue-welleck-2019`; `paper:persona-dialogue-chen-2023-orig` |
| Owner correction and deletion | Apply each typed correction, inspect behavioral preview, roll back, then revoke a source and probe indexes, summaries, caches, and outputs | Correction scope must persist; deletion is incomplete while a derived artifact remains attributable | `paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles`; `paper:bostandjiev-2012-tasteweights`; `paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning` |
| Trait/state/context | Hold out occasions and relationships; compare state estimates, aggregated posterior, and simple baselines per trait | Require per-trait calibration and unknown states; no global personality score | `paper:psychometrics_epstein_1983`; `paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001`; `paper:psychometrics_stachl_2020` |
| Preference and decision | Reframe equivalent choices, vary option sets, preserve rejected options and outcomes, and repeat later | Score frame robustness and context-conditioned prediction; one isolated choice cannot set a global preference | `paper:tversky-kahneman-1981-framing`; `paper:tversky-simonson-1993-context-preferences`; `paper:chen-risen-2010-free-choice` |
| Affect and motive uncertainty | Compare text classifier, owner report, context, and later clarification; score calibration of competing hypotheses | Reader-labeled emotion or rational inference cannot be marked as verified inner state | `paper:carroll-russell-1996-face-context`; `paper:demszky-2020-goemotions`; `paper:wu-2018-emotion-mental-states` |
| Held-out person prediction | Reserve complete later questions, conversations, decisions, and observed actions; compare generic, demographics-only, retrieval-only, and full systems; normalize where owner retest exists | Report by domain and horizon; never average survey and action results into one twin score | `paper:generative-agents-1000-2024`; `paper:twin-2k-500-2025`; `paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026` |
| Trajectory fidelity | Predict ordered behavior chains with branch correction; score node accuracy, cumulative consistency, recovery after error, and non-action | Early-error propagation and over-action remain visible | `paper:behaviorchain-2025`; `paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026` |
| Population and simulator validity | Compare means, variance, covariance, subgroup error, repeated-choice noise, excess rationality, and downstream transfer to real users | Plausibility or mean agreement cannot authorize human replacement | `paper:turing-experiments-2023`; `paper:synthetic-replacements-2024`; `paper:llms-more-rational-2025`; `paper:neural-user-simulation-2018` |
| Social and owner judgment | Blinded owner ratings of recognizability, values, style, and relationship appropriateness plus non-owner ratings only where consented | Keep owner judgment separate from goal success and automated-judge scores | `paper:persona-dialogue-zhang-2018`; `paper:sotopia-2024`; `paper:incharacter-2024` |
| Calibration | Reliability curves by observed fact, owner-confirmed claim, inference, memory answer, and future-action prediction | Confident unsupported claims fail even when style is convincing | `paper:linguistic-calibration-2022`; `paper:cognitive-memory-koriat-bjork-2005` |
| Privacy red team | Membership, verbatim extraction, canaries, partial-context reconstruction, candidate inference, duplicate exposure, and alignment bypass | Severe private-text or PII leakage is a release veto regardless of fidelity | `paper:shokri-2017-membership-inference`; `paper:carlini-2021-training-data-extraction`; `paper:kandpal-2022-dedup-privacy`; `paper:lukas-2023-pii-leakage` |
| Personalized safety | Matched stateless and personalized harmful requests after retrieval; score attack success and personalization utility separately | Guard must see retrieved context; safety regression is a release veto | `paper:personalization-legitimizes-risks-2026` |

## WhatsApp Observability Gaps

WhatsApp can at most provide selected, retained communication events and available metadata in specific relationships. It is not a ground-truth sensor for the full state model (`paper:psychometrics_montag_2015`; `paper:persona-dialogue-arimoto-2024`; `paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy`).

| Potentially observable | Unobserved or ambiguous construct | Required system behavior | Evidence boundary |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sent text, emoji, voice/media reference, timestamp, sender, and chat membership | Drafts, unsent thoughts, offline events, bodily state, private attention, and whether advice was followed | Mark unobserved constructs unknown; never let the base model fill them as owner facts | `paper:mylifebits-2006`; `paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026` |
| A first-person statement | Joke, quotation, roleplay, aspiration, politeness, temporary state, or durable self-claim | Attribute and scope provisionally; ask before durable promotion | `paper:persona-dialogue-mazare-2018`; `paper:psychometrics_sherman_2015` |
| Repeated language or usage metadata | Stable trait, motive, relationship quality, or causal mechanism | Aggregate within a declared context, test confounds, and abstain per construct | `paper:psychometrics_mairesse_2007`; `paper:psychometrics_montag_2015`; `paper:danescu-niculescu-mizil-et-al-2011-mark-my-words` |
| Message time and response gap | Event time, availability, urgency, timezone, emotional regulation, forgetting, disagreement, or changed preference | Treat timing as contextual evidence and silence as missing-not-at-random, not a negative label | `paper:kumar-2019-jodie`; `paper:li-2020-tisasrec`; `paper:cognitive-memory-murre-dros-2015` |
| Direct chat or current group membership | Intended, imagined, permitted, observed, forwarded, screenshot, or offline audience | Store distinct audience fields and clarify mixed or uncertain scope | `paper:bell-1984-audience-design`; `paper:litt-hargittai-2016-imagined-audience`; `paper:bernstein-et-al-2013-invisible-audience` |
| Repeated nickname, shorthand, or phrase | Genuine shared conceptual pact, quotation, irony, coercion, stale convention, or accidental similarity | Keep partner/thread scope, establishment evidence, last confirmation, and repair history | `paper:brennan-clark-1996-conceptual-pacts`; `paper:metzing-brennan-2003-conceptual-pacts-broken` |
| A decision or recommendation discussed in chat | Complete option set, rejected alternatives, frame, anchor, constraints, outcome, regret, and later endorsement | Do not learn a durable preference until the missing choice context is supplied | `paper:tversky-kahneman-1981-framing`; `paper:tversky-simonson-1993-context-preferences`; `paper:ariely-2003-coherent-arbitrariness` |
| Emotion words, emoji, or perceived tone | Mixed affect, intensity, appraisal, masking, sarcasm, and writer experience | Store reader-perceived affect separately; seek owner report only when useful and consented | `paper:carroll-russell-1996-face-context`; `paper:trampe-2015-everyday-emotions`; `paper:demszky-2020-goemotions` |
| Story told to one recipient | Untold memories, life-period boundary, factual accuracy, self-defining importance, and current meaning | Preserve audience and narrative version; invite owner-defined chapters and counter-stories | `paper:mclean-2005-meaning-telling`; `paper:conway-bekerian-1987-organization`; `paper:jardmo-et-al-2023-repeated-narratives` |
| Future-tense statement or reminder | Active commitment, conflict with another goal, external trigger, completion, abandonment, or hypothetical intent | Store as tentative intention with trigger and expiry; require completion evidence | `paper:cognitive-memory-einstein-et-al-2005`; `paper:hofmann-2012-everyday-temptations` |
| Contact identity and messages involving others | Correspondent consent, shared-device use, forwarded authorship, purpose change, and authority to model or disclose another person | Keep third-party content compartmentalized; quarantine or redact when permission is absent or ambiguous | `paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy`; `paper:lampinen-et-al-2011-were-in-it-together`; `paper:longpre-2024-consent-crisis` |
| Exported archive contents | Deleted, disappearing, view-once, missing media, calls, edits, reactions, external channels, and current consent | Report coverage and blind spots; do not treat absence as evidence or export possession as permission | `paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy`; `paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide` |

The safe default for WhatsApp-derived evidence is relationship- and time-bounded retrieval with uncertainty, not autonomous identity reconstruction. Any inferred claim should remain inspectable, editable, purpose-limited, and able to be excluded or deleted (`paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001`; `paper:persona-dialogue-wang-2024-emg-rag`; `paper:bourtoule-2021-machine-unlearning`).

## Highest-Value Owner Questions

Questions should be ranked by expected effect on a real decision, ability to resolve conflict, answerability, urgency, disclosure sensitivity, and coverage. A skipped question changes the elicitation policy, not the owner's inferred preference (`paper:boutilier-2003-active-cf`; `paper:harpale-2008-personalized-active-learning`).

| Priority | Owner question | State or decision unlocked | Why it is high value |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Which sources and people may this system use, for which capabilities, audiences, and retention periods?** | Source/consent registry, third-party compartmentalization, capability gates | Authorization must precede retrieval, and owner access does not erase others' privacy stake (`paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy`; `paper:mireshghallah-2024-confaide`; `paper:longpre-2024-consent-crisis`) |
| 2 | **What may the system do: retrieve, answer privately, draft, simulate, speak, or act; and what must it never do?** | Authority scope, abstention, tool and likeness gates | Plausible simulation and weak action prediction do not justify autonomous representation (`paper:social-simulacra-2022`; `paper:shopcart-human-behavior-2026`; `paper:san-roman-2024-audioseal`) |
| 3 | **Which facts, identities, values, and boundaries do you explicitly endorse now, and which should remain unknown?** | Owner-confirmed semantic core and no-inference constraints | Behavioral traces are proxy-rich and value-behavior links are uneven; explicit self-description must outrank inference (`paper:kosinski-2013-private-traits`; `paper:bardi-schwartz-2003-values-behavior`; `paper:scheuerman-2019-computers-see-gender`) |
| 4 | **What are your current and former life chapters, and which episodes or counter-stories matter within each?** | Autobiographical graph, life periods, narrative versions, coverage gaps | Owner-specific period and self-image cues support retrieval, while prompts and cultural scripts can shape the answer (`paper:conway-bekerian-1987-organization`; `paper:rathbone-moulin-conway-2008-self-centered`; `paper:thomsen-berntsen-2008-life-script-chapters`) |
| 5 | **What role does each important person or group play now, what shorthand is still current, and what information must stay inside that relationship?** | Relationship overlays, common ground, audience and disclosure policies | Relational self, conceptual pacts, and co-owned disclosure cannot be recovered safely from membership alone (`paper:andersen-chen-2002-relational-self`; `paper:brennan-clark-1996-conceptual-pacts`; `paper:lampinen-et-al-2011-were-in-it-together`) |
| 6 | **Which claims or preferences are wrong, outdated, temporary, relationship-specific, or simply not to be used?** | Typed correction, supersession, exclusion, expiry, deletion scope | Time-aware models do not identify meaning, and broad edits can have unintended effects without scoped controls (`paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics`; `paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles`; `paper:bostandjiev-2012-tasteweights`) |
| 7 | **For a consequential choice, what options, constraints, reference points, expected outcomes, and tradeoffs were actually present?** | Decision episode and domain-conditioned policy | Frames, option sets, and anchors can reverse or stabilize choices without revealing a context-free utility (`paper:tversky-kahneman-1981-framing`; `paper:tversky-simonson-1993-context-preferences`; `paper:ariely-2003-coherent-arbitrariness`) |
| 8 | **Which goals are active, competing, paused, or completed, and what event should trigger a reminder or update?** | Active-goal workspace and prospective intention manager | Goal shielding and prospective retrieval distinguish temporary activation, competition, monitoring, and completion (`paper:shah-2002-goal-shielding`; `paper:cognitive-memory-einstein-et-al-2005`) |
