# Human Representation Taxonomy

This taxonomy maps the reviewed research to state that a digital-self system can
represent without claiming that the representation is the person. “Observable”
means potentially available in a consented message source; it does not mean the
signal is complete, unbiased, or sufficient for inference.

| Layer | Potentially observable in messaging | Usually missing or ambiguous | Canonical representation | Update rule | Evidence anchors |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Source evidence | Owner-authored text, timestamp, channel, chat, reply structure, attachment metadata | Offline context, intent, truth, omitted alternatives, third-party consent | Immutable source ID, content hash, scope, sensitivity, owner/third-party target, consent state | Append; redact or tombstone on revocation; never rewrite provenance | `paper:carlini-2021-training-data-extraction`, `paper:franz-2022-interdependent-privacy`, `paper:longpre-2024-consent-crisis` |
| Events and episodes | Mentioned actions, plans, locations, participants, outcomes, retellings | Whether an event occurred as stated, unmentioned episodes, exact boundaries, private meaning | Event claims plus episode identity, time range, participants, source links, factual uncertainty | Link corroborating or conflicting claims; owner correction supersedes without deleting history | `paper:conway-bekerian-1987-organization`, `paper:kober-habermas-2017-personal-past-stability`, `paper:libby-eibach-2002-looking-back` |
| Life periods and autobiographical organization | Recurrent roles, places, projects, relationships, transitions | Owner-endorsed chapter boundaries, cultural script bias, importance | Overlapping owner-editable periods and general events used as retrieval paths | Propose tentatively; require owner confirmation; permit overlap and retirement | `paper:chen-mcanally-reese-2013-memory-organization`, `paper:thomsen-berntsen-2008-life-script-chapters`, `paper:rathbone-moulin-conway-2008-self-centered` |
| Semantic self-knowledge | Repeated explicit self-statements and stable factual claims | Current endorsement, scope, exceptions, source reliability | Versioned propositions with provenance, confidence, temporal and relationship scope | Promote only through explicit evidence policy; preserve contradictions | `paper:haslam-et-al-2011-self-knowledge-identity`, `paper:cognitive-memory-vargha-khadem-1997`, `paper:ahn-2007-open-user-profiles` |
| World knowledge | Facts the owner states or relies on | Whether knowledge is current, deeply understood, or merely quoted | Source-linked owner knowledge distinct from general model knowledge | Version and expire; never infer identity from generic knowledge alone | `paper:cognitive-memory-hashtroudi-johnson-chrosniak-1989`, `paper:memgpt-2023` |
| Procedural habits and policies | Recurrent message routines, planning patterns, recurring choices | Offline skills, causal policy, alternatives, success criteria | Context-conditioned procedure with trigger, action, outcome, frequency, and exceptions | Update from repeated episodes and owner feedback; never generalize across domains silently | `paper:cognitive-memory-cohen-squire-1980`, `paper:rieskamp-otto-2006-strategy-selection`, `paper:behaviorchain-2025` |
| Working and prospective state | Current conversation goals, promised follow-ups, near-term plans | Competing priorities, completion, cancellation, private reminders | Bounded active workspace and prospective intentions with deadlines and authority | Expire or resolve explicitly; do not promote abandoned plans to identity | `paper:cognitive-memory-baddeley-2000`, `paper:cognitive-memory-einstein-mcdaniel-1990`, `paper:cognitive-memory-einstein-et-al-2005` |
| Self-schema, roles, and traits | “I am” claims, role language, repeated behavior tendencies | Trait validity, situational variance, role performance, protected traits | Owner-authored self-images plus candidate context-conditioned tendencies | Separate confirmed self-schema from behavioral inference; require temporal scope and exceptions | `paper:rathbone-moulin-conway-2008-self-centered`, `paper:psychometrics_fleeson_2001`, `paper:psychometrics_laajaj_2019` |
| Values and aversions | Explicit value statements, praise/blame, tradeoff language | Norms, coercion, convenience, unexpressed priorities, value-action gap | Versioned value graph with owner declaration, behavioral evidence, conflicts, and scope | Owner declaration outranks inference; behavior can support or contradict, never silently define | `paper:schwartz-2012-refined-values`, `paper:bardi-schwartz-2003-values-behavior` |
| Goals and motives | Stated plans, desires, commitments, recurring projects | Goal conflict, resistance, enactment, hidden motive, current activation | Durable goal graph plus time-bounded active-goal workspace and competing hypotheses | Track activation, progress, conflict, abandonment, and uncertainty separately | `paper:shah-2002-goal-shielding`, `paper:hofmann-2012-everyday-temptations` |
| Preferences and decision policy | Expressed choices, recommendations, refusals, retrospective reasons | Option set, frame, anchor, rejected alternatives, incentives, offline outcome | Decision episode with domain, frame, options, reference point, selected action, outcome, and explanation | Learn context-conditioned policy only after repeated or confirmed episodes | `paper:tversky-kahneman-1981-framing`, `paper:tversky-simonson-1993-context-preferences`, `paper:chen-risen-2010-free-choice`, `paper:koren-2009-temporal-dynamics` |
| Affect and appraisal | Emotion words, emoji, tone, timing, direct owner report | Felt emotion, intensity, masking, mixed states, physiology, causal appraisal | Time-indexed owner reports and lower-authority inferred hypotheses with appraisal fields | Owner report outranks classifier; decay quickly unless a durable tendency is separately supported | `paper:smith-ellsworth-1985-appraisal`, `paper:kuppens-2010-dynaffect`, `paper:trampe-2015-everyday-emotions`, `paper:demszky-2020-goemotions` |
| Mental-state hypotheses | Statements about beliefs, expectations, wants, and others’ knowledge | Privileged access to motive, rationality assumptions, unknown options | Competing belief/desire hypotheses with posterior confidence and missing assumptions | Use to generate questions; never promote a convenient hypothesis to fact | `paper:baker-2017-bayesian-mentalizing`, `paper:wu-2018-emotion-mental-states`, `paper:keysar-2003-tom-limits`, `paper:epley-2004-perspective-taking` |
| Social roles and relationships | Chat identity, address forms, shared references, accommodation, disclosure patterns | Contact identity rights, offline relationship quality, untold boundaries | Pseudonymous relationship, role, audience, disclosure scope, shared-history links, style delta | Keep relationship state compartmentalized; globalize only owner facts with explicit support | `paper:andersen-chen-2002-relational-self`, `paper:bell-1984-audience-design`, `paper:brennan-clark-1996-conceptual-pacts`, `paper:marwick-boyd-2011-context-collapse` |
| Narrative identity | Stories, turning points, repeated interpretations, audience and telling purpose | Untold memories, factual truth, current endorsement, cultural prompting effects | Append-only narrative versions linked to episodes, self-images, themes, audience, and current meaning | Preserve contradictory and unresolved versions; never manufacture coherence or positive resolution | `paper:mclean-2005-meaning-telling`, `paper:mcadams-et-al-2006-continuity-change`, `paper:pals-2006-difficult-experiences`, `paper:adler-2012-living-into-story` |
| Communication policy | Length, punctuation, emoji, discourse acts, adaptation to audience and channel | Intent, sarcasm, language competence outside sampled contexts | Global baseline plus relationship, role, channel, time, and intent-conditioned deltas | Estimate from held-out-safe evidence; keep style separate from identity and facts | `paper:psychometrics_pennebaker_king_1999`, `paper:ireland-et-al-2011-language-style-matching`, `paper:persona-dialogue-zhang-2018` |
| Uncertainty and conflict | Source disagreement, changing statements, sparse coverage, failed predictions | Unknown unknowns and unobserved counterevidence | Per-item confidence, evidence strength, support/conflict links, missing fields, stale flag, abstention policy | Recompute from evidence and scope; confidence cannot be inherited from fluent generation | `paper:linguistic-calibration-2022`, `paper:synthetic-replacements-2024`, `paper:cognitive-memory-hashtroudi-johnson-chrosniak-1989` |
| Reflection and consolidation | New clusters, repeated episodes, owner corrections, evaluation failures | Whether a summary captures meaning or introduces model bias | Auditable reflection record: inputs, policy, proposed changes, rejected inferences, conflicts, timestamp | Append a proposal; validate; apply as a new version; support replay and rollback | `paper:cognitive-memory-mcclelland-mcnaughton-oreilly-1995`, `paper:cognitive-memory-hupbach-et-al-2007`, `paper:kirkpatrick-2017-ewc` |

## Cross-Cutting Qualifiers

Every canonical state item, regardless of layer, carries:

- evidence IDs and source ownership
- evidence versus inference versus owner declaration
- confidence and evidence quality
- valid time range and last-confirmed time
- global, relationship, role, audience, and channel scope
- sensitivity, consent, retention, and disclosure policy
- support, contradiction, supersession, and correction links
- whether it may influence simulation, export, or only owner inspection

These qualifiers are not optional metadata. They are the mechanism that prevents a
message, classifier output, or model-generated summary from silently becoming the
owner's identity.
