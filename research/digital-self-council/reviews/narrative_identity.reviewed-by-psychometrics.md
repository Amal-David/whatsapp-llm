## 2. Identifier and metadata corrections

No DOI, PMID, title, author, year, venue, publication type, or primary URL required replacement. All 20 DOI URLs resolved to the expected publisher destinations, and normalized DOI and title checks each yielded 20 unique values.

Two data descriptions did require correction:

- `paper:blagov-singer-2004-four-dimensions`: the prior wording said 103 participants each supplied 10 memories, "yielding" 1,040. The PubMed abstract separately reports 103 participants with 10 memories for participant analyses and 1,040 memories in the rater-agreement analysis, so the record now preserves those as separate reported quantities.
- `paper:lilgendahl-mcadams-2011-self-growth`: the analytic data are now described as an 88-person geographically distributed MIDUS interview subsample, not an unqualified national sample. The record also states that six of 94 completed interviews were excluded as incomplete or insufficiently coherent for coding.

For `paper:libby-eibach-2002-looking-back`, PubMed does not expose a DOI in its article-ID field, but the recorded DOI resolves to the APA publisher and matches the paper metadata; no change was needed.

## 3. Overclaims and wording changes

- `paper:conway-bekerian-1987-organization`: the experiments establish faster retrieval with owner-specific lifetime-period primes, not a uniquely necessary storage representation. The architecture implication and limitation now preserve the paper's explicit alternative explanation that cues may help construct retrieval plans.
- `paper:lilgendahl-mcadams-2011-self-growth`: "predicted well-being" was replaced with noncausal association language because the analysis was cross-sectional and correlational. The selection and exclusion mechanisms are now explicit.
- `paper:reese-et-al-2011-narrative-coherence`: evidence strength was downgraded from `strong` to `moderate`. The full text calls the pooled application preliminary; elicitation and event type were partly confounded with age, preschool scores often tracked narrative length, and cross-cultural measurement invariance was not tested.
- `paper:rathbone-moulin-conway-2008-self-centered`: the limitations now identify the 13-person four-year follow-up and the scarcity of negative self-images.
- The synthesis note for `paper:conway-bekerian-1987-organization` no longer claims that a flat underlying store is inconsistent with the evidence. It recommends exposed owner-defined cue paths without asserting one latent topology.

No claim changes were required for the other 15 records. In particular, the reverse mediation in `paper:haslam-et-al-2011-self-knowledge-identity`, the null coherence change in `paper:adler-2012-living-into-story`, and the short-term elicitation effects in `paper:dargembeau-garcia-jimenez-2024-working-self` were already stated conservatively.

## 4. Missing counterevidence and foundational work

The seat contains meaningful internal counterevidence, especially `paper:habermas-de-silveira-2008-global-coherence`, `paper:haslam-et-al-2011-self-knowledge-identity`, `paper:lilgendahl-mcadams-2011-self-growth`, and `paper:adler-2012-living-into-story`. Three verified primary papers remain useful candidates for a future corpus expansion:

- McLean et al., *The empirical structure of narrative identity: The initial Big Three* ([DOI 10.1037/pspp0000247](https://doi.org/10.1037/pspp0000247), [PMID 30998044](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30998044/)). Across 855 participants and 2,565 narratives, it models motivational/affective themes, autobiographical reasoning, and structural aspects as distinct factors and reports prompt-dependent trait associations. This is missing modern foundational psychometric structure work.
- Gehrt et al., *Measuring narrative identity: rater coding versus questionnaire-based approaches* ([DOI 10.1080/09658211.2024.2359503](https://doi.org/10.1080/09658211.2024.2359503), [PMID 38809783](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38809783/)). Corresponding self-report and rater-coded coherence dimensions did not significantly converge. This directly cautions against treating different narrative measures as interchangeable.
- Dimitrova and Simms, *Construct validation of narrative coherence: Exploring links with personality functioning and psychopathology* ([DOI 10.1037/per0000508](https://doi.org/10.1037/per0000508), [PMID 34570521](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/34570521/)). In a psychiatric sample, narrative coherence did not function as the predicted marker of personality functioning. This strengthens the existing rejection of clinical inference from `paper:adler-2012-living-into-story` and `paper:reese-et-al-2011-narrative-coherence`.

These omissions do not block approval because the current records and note already reject causal, clinical, and universal-coherence interpretations. They do leave the seat without a direct cross-method convergence test, a modern latent-structure study, or a measurement-invariance study across languages and cultures. `paper:chen-mcanally-reese-2013-memory-organization` demonstrates cultural differences in output, not invariance of a narrative-identity measure.

## 6. Architecture claims downgraded or rejected

- **Downgraded:** `paper:conway-bekerian-1987-organization` supports owner-specific period and general-event cue paths. It does not prove a fixed hierarchical storage ontology or rule out a flat store with constructed retrieval strategies.
- **Rejected:** coherence, agency, positive resolution, or growth language as direct measures of a stable trait, maturity, well-being, or clinical state. The relevant evidence is group-level, method-dependent, developmental, and often correlational (`paper:reese-et-al-2011-narrative-coherence`, `paper:pals-2006-difficult-experiences`, `paper:lilgendahl-mcadams-2011-self-growth`, `paper:adler-2012-living-into-story`).
- **Rejected:** repeated narration as factual ground truth. Recurrence measures content stability, while current self-concept and later interpretation can change retrieval and meaning (`paper:kober-habermas-2017-personal-past-stability`, `paper:jardmo-et-al-2023-repeated-narratives`, `paper:libby-eibach-2002-looking-back`).
- **Rejected:** a WhatsApp message, message volume, or linguistic polish as sufficient evidence that an event is self-defining, that a life-period boundary exists, or that an inferred identity is enduring (`paper:mclean-2005-meaning-telling`, `paper:rathbone-moulin-conway-2008-self-centered`, `paper:dargembeau-garcia-jimenez-2024-working-self`).

The defensible architecture remains owner-editable and versioned: preserve source event claims separately from narrative interpretation, retain prompt and audience context, expose competing versions, and require owner confirmation before promoting an identity claim.
