## Review method

Each canonical DOI and HTTPS DOI URL was resolved and checked against official
DOI metadata and the publisher record. PubMed metadata and abstracts were
checked for the 19 records carrying PubMed aliases. The Godden-Baddeley paper,
which has no PubMed alias in the record, was checked against its official Wiley
paper page. Claims marked `abstract` were limited to what those primary-source
abstracts support. Full article text was inspected for
`paper:cognitive-memory-hupbach-et-al-2007`,
`paper:cognitive-memory-murre-dros-2015`, and
`paper:cognitive-memory-schapiro-et-al-2017` using official PMC or publisher
full text.

The audit covered title, author list, year, venue, canonical and alternate
identifiers, primary URL, method, population or data, finding, limitation,
evidence strength, and the inferential distance of architecture implications.

## Claim and wording corrections

One factual correction was required in
`paper:cognitive-memory-murre-dros-2015`. The limitation formerly said there
were "only ten replications per interval." The full text reports ten planned
replications at each interval but only nine at the 9-hour interval, producing
69 experimental lists. The record now states that exact design.

No evidence-strength downgrades were required. No other method, population,
finding, or limitation field exceeded the inspected primary source. In

## Missing counterevidence and foundational work

These are non-blocking coverage gaps, but they constrain council synthesis:

- Working-memory evidence is centered on `paper:cognitive-memory-luck-vogel-1997`
  and `paper:cognitive-memory-baddeley-2000`. Direct resource, variable-precision,
  and binding-cost alternatives are absent, so the seat cannot adjudicate a
  slot model against those accounts.
- Systems consolidation is represented chiefly by
  `paper:cognitive-memory-mcclelland-mcnaughton-oreilly-1995` and the extension
  in `paper:cognitive-memory-schapiro-et-al-2017`. Multiple-trace and
  transformation accounts of remote episodic memory are not directly included.
- `paper:cognitive-memory-hupbach-et-al-2007` supplies a constructive
  reconsolidation result, but the seat lacks a dedicated replication or
  boundary-condition paper and lacks a direct counter-test of ordinary source
  confusion as the sufficient explanation.
- The corpus has no dedicated misinformation or false-memory experiment.
  Reconstructive contamination appears in
  `paper:cognitive-memory-hupbach-et-al-2007`, but that paper does not cover the
  broader eyewitness-misinformation or semantic-associate literature.
- Prospective memory is represented by event-based laboratory work in
  `paper:cognitive-memory-einstein-mcdaniel-1990` and
  `paper:cognitive-memory-einstein-et-al-2005`; time-based, naturalistic, and
  high-stakes intention failures remain underrepresented.
- `paper:cognitive-memory-anderson-et-al-2004` and
  `paper:cognitive-memory-eliasmith-et-al-2012` show integrated computational
  architectures, but no included paper validates autobiographical owner
  replication, long-horizon owner correction, or WhatsApp-derived person models.
