## 4. Missing Counterevidence Or Foundational Work

No blocking counterevidence omission was found. The slice already preserves several important negative or boundary-setting results:

- weak and norm-confounded value-behavior relations in `paper:bardi-schwartz-2003-values-behavior`;
- frame, option-set, anchor, and measurement effects in `paper:tversky-kahneman-1981-framing`, `paper:tversky-simonson-1993-context-preferences`, `paper:ariely-2003-coherent-arbitrariness`, and `paper:chen-risen-2010-free-choice`;
- observer-context dependence and substantial classification error in `paper:carroll-russell-1996-face-context` and `paper:demszky-2020-goemotions`;
- egocentric perspective failures and evidence-sensitive belief recovery in `paper:keysar-2003-tom-limits`, `paper:epley-2004-perspective-taking`, and `paper:wu-2018-emotion-mental-states`.

Three non-blocking evidence gaps should remain visible in later council synthesis:

- prospective longitudinal evidence for genuine within-person value change is thinner than the cross-sectional value-structure evidence;
- cultural and relationship-specific generalization of appraisal, emotion-language, and mentalizing models remains under-tested;
- no included study validates recovering an owner's stable values, affect dynamics, or motives from private messaging alone.

These gaps are already represented as open questions or speculative limits in the seat note, so no additional paper was inserted during cross-review.
